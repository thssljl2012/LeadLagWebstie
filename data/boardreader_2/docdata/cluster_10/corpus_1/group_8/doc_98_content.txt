DUBLIN (Dow Jones)--Ireland's Financial Services Regulatory Authority said Friday that it was informed on Feb. 18 'on the matter of mispricing of trades' in the London branch of Merrill Lynch, the investment bank taken over by Bank Of America Corp. (BAC).   It said Merrill Lynch International Bank Ltd. is an Irish credit institution based in the Irish Financial Services Center in Dublin's docklands, and the regulator had sent officers to the London site to investigate the issue.   The regulator said that it has agreed on 'the scope of the investigation' with the U.K. Financial Services Authority, and said Merrill Lynch/Bank of America Corp. have employed 'external expertise' to investigate the circumstances in conjunction with the bank's corporate audit.   It would not give more detail on the amount of money involved. (END) Dow Jones Newswires
