Bank of America chairman and chief executive Ken Lewis was issued a subpoena by New York state attorney general Andrew Cuomo, who is investigating whether the bank withheld information from investors, reports the WSJ. Lewis is the highest-profile subject of Cuomos investigation into BofAs Jan 1 purchase of Merrill Lynch, and whether investors were misled about the depth of Merrills losses in late 2008 and whether details of Merrill bonuses should have been disclosed to investors.
