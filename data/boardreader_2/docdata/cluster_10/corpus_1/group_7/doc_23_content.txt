In recent weeks, Steele Alphin has gone from a behind-the-scenes power at Bank of America Corp. to a central player in the bank's troubled Merrill Lynch &amp; Co. acquisition.  The bank's chief administrative officer has long been a close confidant to chief executive Ken Lewis, gaining a reputation as the de facto No. 2 executive at the company. But the more than 30-year company veteran has largely stayed in the shadows, compiling key posts running human resources, marketing, public relations, philanthropy and the company's now shrinking corporate jet fleet.
