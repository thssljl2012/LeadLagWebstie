NEW YORK (Dow Jones-- Corp. (BAC) reversed course on a controversial decision to stagger 2008 bonus payments over a four-year period, and instead will pay some bankers their money over an 18-month period.  The Charlotte-based bank confirmed on Thursday it is 'shortening the vesting schedule for a portion' of the awards. But the amount of money being given to employees hasn't increased.  There was a backlash inside BofA after employees in the investment banking and capital markets group were told their bonuses wouldn't be paid all at once because of financial troubles at the bank.
