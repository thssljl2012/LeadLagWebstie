BOSTON (MarketWatch) -- The U.S. financial sector fell along with the broad market in early trading Thursday as investors sold stocks after a government report said weekly jobless claims jumped to a 26-year high. The Financial Select Sector SPDR Fund , an exchange-traded fund tracking banking and financial-services stocks, fell more than 2% in opening trades. Among large-cap bank stocks, Bank of America Corp. (BAC:$15.85,00$-0.84,00-5.03%) slipped 6% and Citigroup Inc. (C:$7.90,00$-0.40,00-4.82%) shed nearly 5%.
