 S&amp;amp;amp;P/ASX 200, All Ordinaries gain on strong banks, miners     http://www.theaustralian.news.com.au...017996,00.html        AUSTRALIAN shares opened higher today as investors took their cue from a strong finish on Wall Street and in European markets on Friday.      The benchmark S&amp;amp;amp;P/ASX 200 was up 76.8 points, or 1.9 per cent, at 4094.8 in the first 15 minutes of trading.      The broader All Ordinaries was higher by 74.4 points at 4057.1.      Mining and banking heavyweights led the market higher.      BHP Billiton gained 80 cents (2.9 per cent) to $28.79 and Rio Tinto was higher by $2.36 (3.04 per cent) to $79.96, despite weaker commodity prices on Friday night.      Westpac, National Australia Bank, ANZ and Commonwealth Bank of Australia gained between 1.24 per cent and 2.42 per cent.      Despite a US Commerce Department report showing a drop in consumer spending, US stocks gained on Friday.      The  Dow Jones  Industrial Average index gained 144.32 points to close at 9325.01 on Friday, and the Standard &amp;amp;amp; Poor&amp;#039;s 500 index rose by 16.66 points to 968.75.      The technology-heavy  Nasdaq  gained 22.4 points to close at 1720.95.      Investors will have their eyes on several key pieces of economic data today.      TD Securities released its monthly  inflation  gauge mid this morning. That report will be followed by the Australian Bureau of Statistics monthly retail sales and quarterly house price index at 11.30am.
