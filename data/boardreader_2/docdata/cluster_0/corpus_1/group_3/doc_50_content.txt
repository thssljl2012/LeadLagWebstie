The Australian stock market was trading sharply lower on Tuesday after Wall Street finished with steep triple-digit losses on Monday amid the continuing uncertainty about the implementation of the U.S. government's financial bailout plan as well as reports of a global recession setting in. Investors are also cautious ahead of Tuesday's Reserve Bank of Australia policy meeting. The central bank is widely expected to announce a 50 basis point rate cut.At 9:12 p.m. ET, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index was down 94 points or 2.06% at 4,447, after closing down 2.61% on Monday. The broader All Ordinaries index was losing 108 points or 2.39% to 4,436.In the currency market, the Australian dollar opened weaker for a tenth straight session on Tuesday after hitting the lowest point since September 2004 in Monday's session. A sharp fall in world commodities prices also added to the Australian dollar sell-off. The Aussie opened at US$0.7200-0.7205, down from Monday's close of US$0.7495-0.7498.On Monday, the Dow closed down 370 points or 3.58% at 9,955, the Nasdaq fell 84 points or 4.34% to close at 1,863 and the S&amp;amp;P 500 dropped 42 points or 3.85% to finish at 1,057.On the economic front, an interest rate decision by the Reserve Bank of Australia tops Tuesday's economic news calendar for the Aussie-New Zealand region.The Australian Industry Group is also scheduled to release its Performance of Construction Index or PCI for September. The PCI reading for August was 43.1, well below the 50.0 level that separates contraction of activity in the sector from expansion.Among banking stocks, Commonwealth Bank lost 1.95%, National Australia Bank was down 2.35%, and ANZ Banking Group fell 1.11%. Westpac slipped 1.87%, investment bank Macquarie Group dropped 3.14%, and rival St. George bank declined 1.91%.In the resources sector, index leader BHP Billiton dropped 1.68%, and Rio Tinto fell 2.75%.
