Australia's sharemarket has ended the day five per cent higher, after tracking in postive territory all day.The All Ordinaries index leapt six per cent in early trading, before closing up 202 points to 4,141. The benchmark ASX 200 enjoyed similar early gains, finishing the day 5.5 per cent higher, after plunging 16 per cent last week. The index rose 220 points to 4,180, with bank stocks leading the charge. ANZ was the top performer, rising 13 per cent. Westpac was hot on its heels, finishing the day nearly nine per cent up. A late surge by Leighton Holdings has seen the company's share price put on more than 15 per cent. On the currency front, the Australian dollar is currently trading at 65.8 US cents.
