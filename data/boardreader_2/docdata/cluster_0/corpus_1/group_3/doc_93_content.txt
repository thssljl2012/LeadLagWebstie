(Xinhua via COMTEX News Network) -- The Australian stock market closed weaker on Friday amid falls on resources and bank stocks. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index fell 42.6 points or 1.06 percent to 3,970.8, while the broader All Ordinaries index lost 43.3 points or 1.09 percent to 3,944.8. Total market turnover was 1.27 billion shares worth 4.44 billion Australian dollars (3.05 billion U.S. dollars), with 616 stocks down, 412 up and 283 unchanged. In the banking sector, ANZ declined 48 cents to 16.85 dollars, Commonwealth Bank retreated 99 cents to 41.41 dollars, National Australia Bank shed 1.10 dollars to 21.60 dollars and Westpac lost 52 cents to 21.48 dollars. In the resources sector, BHP Billiton slid 1.21 dollars to 24. 59 dollars and Rio Tinto dumped 3.39 dollars to 62.62 dollars. In the media sector, News Corp added 30 cents to 13.85 dollars, its non-voting shares gained 39 cents to 13.69 dollars, Consolidated Media eased three cents to 1.95 dollars and Fairfax fell three cents to 2.02 dollars. (1 Australian dollar equals 0.689 U.S. dollar)
