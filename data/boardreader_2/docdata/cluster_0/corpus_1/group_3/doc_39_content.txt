The Australian sharemarket has fallen two per cent, following a sharp overnight fall on Wall Street as investors worried that the proposed $US700 billion bailout won't save the US economy from a recession. At 1139 AEST, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index shed 97.5 points, or 2.04 per cent, to 4,663.6. The broader All Ordinaries lost 2.16 per cent, or 103.3 points, to 4,670.8. New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index fell 2.69 per cent to 3,145.47. All the big four banks lost ground as investors awaited the decision and reaction in the United States. ANZ Banking Group lost three per cent, Commonwealth Bank of Australia fell 1.49 per cent, National Australia Bank slipped 2.1 per cent and Westpac Banking Group dipped 1.02 per cent. Investment banking giant Macquarie Group lost 2.54 per cent, while its troubled rival Babcock &amp;amp; Brown tumbled 10 per cent. Shares in home builder Stockland Group fell 2.06 per cent after it revised its guidance for fiscal 2009 following a write down of its assets in the United Kingdom. Mining companies were weaker on falling commodities prices, as traders eyed a shaky slowing global demand due to a shaky US economy. BHP Billiton shares headed towards $30, falling 4.7 per cent to $30.18, while its takeover target Rio Tinto lost 4.8 per cent to $86.87. Smaller rival Fortescue Metals Group slumped 3.5 per cent to $4.68, well down from its record high of $13.15 on June 25. Fertiliser company Incitec Pivot lost 9.24 per cent to $4.22. In the US, the Dow Jones industrial average fell 348.22 points, or 3.22 per cent, to 10,482.85, while the Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 Index slid 46.78 points, or 4.03 per cent, to 1,114.28. The Nasdaq Composite Index dropped 92.68 points, or 4.48 per cent, to 1,976.72 -- a three-and-a-half-year low.
