(RTTNews) - The Australian stock market was trading mixed on Thursday, after Wall Street finished modestly lower overnight ahead of a Senate vote on a revised bailout package. U.S. Congress failed to pass the earlier rescue plan for the ailing U.S. financial industry. At 9:12 p.m. ET, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index was up 3 points or 0.05% at 4,797, after closing up 4.22% on Wednesday. The broader All Ordinaries index was losing 5 points or 0.10% to 4,810.In the currency market, the Australian dollar opened lower on Thursday. The Aussie opened at US$0.7870-0.7877, down from Wednesday's close of US$0.7962-0.7966.On the economic front, the Australian Bureau of Statistics is scheduled to release the trade balance report for the month of August. Australia posted a trade deficit of A$717 million in July.Among banking stocks, Commonwealth Bank added 1.92%, National Australia Bank rose 2.72%, and ANZ Banking Group gained 1.45%. Westpac was up 2.24%, investment bank Macquarie Group soared 4.05%, and rival St. George bank advanced 1.48%.In the resources sector, index leader BHP Billiton edged up 0.15%, and Rio Tinto was down 0.63%. Gold miners were mixed, after gold closed marginally higher on Wednesday. Lihir Gold was down 0.73%, and Sino Gold fell 2.52%, while Newcrest Mining edged up 0.32%.On Wednesday, crude oil futures for November delivery closed lower, below the $100 mark, as traders considered Energy Information Administration data that showed a build-up in weekly crude oil and gasoline inventories for the first time in six weeks. Oil closed down $2.11 at $98.53 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange, after touching intraday lows of $95.95 in mid-day trading. In the Asian session Thursday, crude was up $0.89 at $99.42 in electronic trading.Among energy stocks, Woodside lost 1.34%, and Santos fell 1.21%, while Oil Search rose 1.04%.In the retail sector, David Jones edged up 0.48%, and Coles' owner Wesfarmers added 0.10%, while giant retailer Woolworths lost 0.17%.
