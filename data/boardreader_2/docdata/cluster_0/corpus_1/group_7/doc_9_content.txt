By 8.15am, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was down 38.4 points, or 1.13 per cent, at 3364 while the broader All Ordinaries index lost 34.6 points, or 1.03 per cent, to 3318.4.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract shed 23 points to 3,339 on a volume of 4677 contracts.  The banking sector was also lower.  National Australia Bank lost three cents to $17.98, Commonwealth Bank lost 37 cents to $29.20, Westpac fell four cents to $16.34, and the ANZ lost 15 cents to $12.30.  In the resources sector, market giant BHP Billiton sank $1.02 to $29.49 while rival Rio Tinto lost $1.99 to $48.30.  Wall Street fell on Friday, sending the Dow Jones industrials to new six-year lows as investors around the world keep selling on pessimism about the global economy.  The Dow Jones industrial average was down 100.28 points at 7365.67.  The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 index fell 8.89, or 1.14 per cent, to settle at 770.05, and the technology-heavy Nasdaq composite index fell 1.59, or 0.11 per cent, to 1441.23.
