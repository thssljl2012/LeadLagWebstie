THE stock market opened higher after a small boost in the financial sector.  By 10.36am AEDT, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was up 14.2 points at 3531.1 while the broader All Ordinaries index added 13.2 points to 3474.1.   The financial sector was stronger.   NAB rose 20 cents to $18.77, Commonwealth Bank added 23 cents to $29.85, Westpac found 6 cents at $17.00, and the ANZ lost 8 cents to $12.90.  In the resources sector, BHP Billiton lost 9 cents to $32.05, rival Rio Tinto found 1 cent to $50.81.  US markets were closed overnight for the President's Day public holiday.  On Friday, US stocks ended lower, pushing the Dow Jones industrial average to its lowest close since last November and leaving it with a weekly decline of 5.2 per cent.  The Dow fell 82.35, or 1.04 per cent, to 7850.41. The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 index lost 8.35, or one per cent, to 826.84, and the Nasdaq composite index decreased 7.35, or 0.48 per cent, to 1534.36.   On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract lost three points to 3395, on a volume of 3964 contracts.
