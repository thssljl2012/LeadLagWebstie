(Adds Asciano)  MELBOURNE, Feb 18 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 1.9 percent on Wednesday, led by top global miner BHP Billiton (ASX: BHP.ax) , after copper and oil prices fell on more signs of a deepening global recession. The benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 .AXJO shed 68 points to 3,396.3 by 0355 GMT, extending a 2.7 percent fall so far this week.  New Zealand's benchmark NZX 50 index .NZ50 fell 2.0 percent to 2,617.9.  STOCKS ON THE MOVE  * Port and rail operator Asciano AIO.AX jumped 17 percent to A$0.65, reversing a 19 percent slide over the previous two days, on speculation it might be close to reaching a deal, either involving its Pacific National coal business or even a complete takeover. The company declined to comment on the speculation.
