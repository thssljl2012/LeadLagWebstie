Article from: AAP  Font size: Decrease Increase  : Email  : Print  Submit comment: Submit comment  AUSTRALIAN stocks opened weaker following a negative lead from Wall Street and negative corporate earnings reports.  By 1015 AEDT, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was down 38.4 points, or 1.13 per cent, at 3,364 while the broader All Ordinaries index lost 34.6 points, or 1.03 per cent, to 3,318.4.  Fairfax reported heavy losses today, with shares down nearly 4 per cent in early trading at $1.01.  Transfield Services has booked a 25 per cent fall in first half profit, sending shares down nearly one per cent to $1.82.  Budget airline Virgin Blue is also due to report its results today.  The banking sector was also lower. National Australia Bank lost three cents to $17.98, Commonwealth Bank lost 37 cents to $29.20, Westpac fell four cents to $16.34, and the ANZ lost 15 cents to $12.30.  In the resources sector, market giant BHP Billiton sank $1.02 to $29.49 while rival Rio Tinto lost $1.99 to $48.30.  Wall Street fell on Friday, sending the Dow Jones industrials to new six-year lows as investors around the world keep selling on pessimism about the global economy.  The Dow Jones industrial average was down 100.28 points at 7,365.67.  The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 index fell 8.89, or 1.14 per cent, to settle at 770.05, and the technology-heavy Nasdaq composite index fell 1.59, or 0.11 per cent, to 1,441.23.
