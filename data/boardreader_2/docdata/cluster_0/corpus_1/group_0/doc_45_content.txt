 SYDNEY, July 17 (Reuters) - Australian shares rose 1.2 percent on Thursday, as financials such as Macquarie Group  rallied after surprisingly strong results at a big U.S. bank helped ease credit-market worries. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index  rose 57.7 points to 4,928.3 by 0224 GMT, adding to a 1.1 percent rise in the previous session.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index  rose 1.1 percent, or 33.41 points, to 3,092.77. The most heavily weighted stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand , was up 0.6 percent at NZ$3.40.
