 AUSTRALIAN shares were lower at noon, but recovering after the banks recouped some of their losses and gold and energy stocks gained.The market recovered some of the earlier losses as gold and energy stocks gained on price rises in the two commodities. At 1200 AEST the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index fell 7.7 points, or 0.15 per cent, to 4972.2. The index earlier declined to 4936.4. The broader All Ordinaries lost 14.7 points to 5,053.1. Pattersons Securities adviser Sam Fimis said miners, including majors BHP Billiton and Rio Tinto, contributed to declines as the perception of iron ore cooled. Mr Simis said the banks had done quite well today, given the negative lead from the US. Westpac gained 30 cents, or 1.58 per cent, to $19.31 and its takeover target, St George added 12 cents to $25.56. Westpac said today it bought the 40 per cent stake held by the Government of Tonga in Westpac Bank of Tonga for an undisclosed sum. ANZ rose 12 cents to $18.07 while National Australia Bank fell 45 cents, or 1.64 per cent, to $27.00 and Commonwealth Bank of Australia lost five cents to $40.27. Fortescue Metals slipped 32 cents, or 3.23 per cent, to $9.58. The property sector also added to the market slump, Mr Fimis said. 'Goodman are a market leader in the sector and they're doing badly, which doesn't help with sentiment,'' he said. Goodman fell 21 cents, or 7.95 per cent, to $2.43, Stockland declined 19 cents, or 4.17 per cent, to $4.37 and Mirvac lost 11.5 cents, or 5.45 per cent, to $1.995. GPT, which last week announced a earnings downgrade, slipped 1.5 cents, or 0.96 per cent, to $1.545. The rise in oil and gold prevented the market from falling further, given the losses in US equities. Both New York and European stocks closed sharply lower on Friday after shares of Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac sank, as investors considered whether the US Government would need to provide them with support. The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 128.48 points, or 1.14 per cent, to close at 11,100.54. Light sweet crude for August delivery soared to an all-time high of $US147.27 a barrel before settling at $US145.08 on rising hostilities between the West and Iran and unrest in Nigeria. Woodside gained 39 cents to $60.99 and Santos added 44 cents, or 2.32 per cent, to $19.39. Oil Search was steady at $5.83. At 1214 AEST, the spot price of gold in Sydney was $US965.00 per fine ounce, up $US20.15 from Friday's close of $US944.85. Gold miner Newcrest added 98 cents, or 3.11 per cent, to $32.45, Lihir gained 11 cents, or 3.41 per cent, to $3.34 and Sino Gold rose 12 cents, or 2.39 per cent, to $5.15.
