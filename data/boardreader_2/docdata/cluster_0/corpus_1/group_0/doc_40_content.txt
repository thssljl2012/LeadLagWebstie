 SYDNEY, July 22 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 0.95 percent on Tuesday, with financials such as National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX: NAB.ax) leading losses after disappointing earnings at American Express Co AXP.N dented sentiment in the sector.However, firmer oil and metals prices buoyed resource firms such as BHP Billiton Ltd (ASX: BHP.ax) and Woodside Petroleum Ltd (ASX: WPL.ax) , keeping a lid on losses. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index .AXJO fell 47.7 points to 4,964.1 by 0126 GMT, after rising 3.5 percent in the previous session.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index .NZ50 edged down 0.1 percent, or 3.82 points, to 3,144.50. The most heavily weighted stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand (NZX: TEL.nz) , was down 1.5 percent at NZ$3.40.
