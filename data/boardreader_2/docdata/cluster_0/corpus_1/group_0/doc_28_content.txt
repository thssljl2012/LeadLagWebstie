 (Adds Service Stream)SYDNEY, July 4 (Reuters) - Australian shares rose 0.7 percent on Friday, buoyed by gains in heavyweight mining and banking firms as investors hunted for bargains after five straight sessions of losses. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index .AXJO rose 35 points to 5,033.3 by 0222 GMT, after falling 1.9 percent to the lowest level since September 2006 in the previous session.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index .NZ50 added 1.7 percent, or 52.57 points, to 3,146.99. The biggest stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand (NZX: TEL.nz) , was up 3.7 percent at NZ$3.40. STOCKS ON THE MOVE* Diversified infrastructure services company Service Stream Ltd SSM.AX rose 9.4 percent to A$0.935. It reiterated that it will likely report an EBITDA of A$37 million ($35.6 million) in fiscal 2008 and A$48 million in 2009, underpinned by its current order book.0220 GMT * Babcock &amp;amp; Brown Power BBP.AX climbed 25 percent to A$0.825. The investment fund said it will sell its Uranquinty Power Station to energy retailer Origin Energy Ltd (ASX: ORG.ax) for an enterprise value of A$700 million ($673 million), allowing it to reduce debt.0122 GMT* Top miners rose, recovering from hefty losses in the previous session.BHP Billiton Ltd (ASX: BHP.ax) , the world's top miner and Australia's top oil and gas producer, rose 1.7 percent to A$40.49, while its main rival and takeover target, Rio Tinto Ltd (ASX: RIO.ax) , gained 2.6 percent to A$125.06. Chinese steel mills have reached an iron ore agreement with BHP Billiton, in line with the near-doubling of prices won by rival Rio Tinto, sources told Reuters on Friday [ID:nSP9603].0057 GMT* The top banks all rose, extending their recovery for the third straight session as investors continued to take the view that the recent sell-off in the sector was overdone.National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX: NAB.ax) added 2.3 percent to A$27.09 and Commonwealth Bank of Australia Ltd (ASX: CBA.ax) added 1.6 percent to A$41.99. Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Ltd (ASX: ANZ.ax) rose 1.2 percent to A$18.94 while Westpac Banking Corp (ASX: WBC.ax) was up 1.1 percent at A$19.90. 0056 GMT* Fortescue Metals Group Ltd (ASX: FMG.ax) gained 4.2 percent to A$9.90.
