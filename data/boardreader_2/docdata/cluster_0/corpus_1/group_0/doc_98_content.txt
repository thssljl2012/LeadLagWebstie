 Australia's treasurer, Wayne Swan, has tried to allay fears over the health of the Australian banking sector, saying it can withstand the volatility on the global credit market.His comments come after one of the nation's largest banks, the ANZ, revealed its provisions for bad debt-backed investments will rise to $A2 billion ($US1.9 billion), because of the credit crisis and economic slowdown.The ANZ statement follows the nation's largest bank, the National Australia Bank, increasing its provisions to $A1 billion ($US958 million). But Australia's treasurer, Wayne Swan, says the banks are strong and well-regulated and have been transparent with the market about their financial position. 'As you know, these things change over time,' he said.'But I'm advised by the regulators that they are satisfied that all of our banks have made full disclosure.'
