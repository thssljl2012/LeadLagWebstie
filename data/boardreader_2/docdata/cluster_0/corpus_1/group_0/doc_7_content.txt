 (RTTNews) - The Australian stock market was trading lower Friday despite Wall Street closing with triple-digit gain for the second straight session overnight following a sharp drop in oil prices and positive quarterly results announced by JP Morgan and United Technologies. The resource stocks were lower on weaker commodity prices. The Australian share market closed in positive territory for the second consecutive day on Thursday after banks posted strong gains.At 9:10 p.m. ET, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index was down 25 points or 0.51% at 4,876 and the he broader All Ordinaries index was losing 22 points or 0.45% to 4,955.The Australian dollar has opened weaker Friday after falling oil prices supported the U.S. dollar. The Aussie opened at US$0.9724-0.9730, down from Thursday's close of US$0.9769-0.9773.On the economic front, Australian Bureau of Statistics reports that index of Australian import prices was up 1.4% in the second quarter of 2008, compared to the first quarter. That beat analyst expectations of a 2.2% increase, and it was also higher than the 2.7% increase in the previous quarter. On year, the import index was higher by 3.5%.Exports soared 13.5% on quarter and 13.3% on year. That beat forecasts of a 10.0% increase, and it was also higher than the 3.5% increase in the previous quarter.Also, Melbourne Institute issues its bulletin of economic trends.Banks were mixed in early trade. Commonwealth Bank added 0.48% and Westpac gained 0.59%, while ANZ edged down 0.05% and National Australia Bank lost 0.80%. St. George bank edged up 0.41%, while investment bank Macquarie Group was up 0.90%.In the resources sector, index leader BHP Billiton edged down 0.96% and its takeover target Rio Tinto lost 1.66%. Gold miners were weak, with Lihir Gold losing 3.48% and Newcrest Mining falling 2.42%. Gold prices edged higher overnight.On Thursday, oil for August delivery plunged for the third straight session and closed below $130 a barrel for the first time in six weeks after it touched a record high of $147.27 last Friday. Oil climbed as high as $136.75 in the mid-morning amid concerns of supply disruption in Nigeria, but closed down $5.31 at $129.29 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange. In Asian trade Friday, crude was up $0.87 at $130.16 a barrel. Among energy stocks, Woodside fell 2.43%, Oil Search lost 3.86%, but Santos slipped 3.54%.
