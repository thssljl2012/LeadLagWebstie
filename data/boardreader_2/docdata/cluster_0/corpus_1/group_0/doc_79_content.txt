 (RTTNews) - The Australian stock market was trading lower Wednesday, extending Tuesday's losses after the Reserve Bank of Australia left key interest rates unchanged. Top miners slipped after recent strong gains, while banks continued to decline on lingering credit concerns. At 9:24 p.m. ET, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index was down 31 points or 0.60% at 5,108 and the broader All Ordinaries index was losing 35 points or 0.66% to 5,226.The U.S. markets closed with modest gains after a choppy trading session on Tuesday. While continuing concerns over the rising price of oil sent the markets sharply lower early on, a better than expected report on manufacturing along with some positive data within the automotive sector moved stocks into positive territory later in the day.In currency trading, the Australian dollar opened firmer on Wednesday, as a better than expected U.S. manufacturing data boosted appetite for high-yielding currencies. The local unit opened at US$0.9584-0.9588, down from Tuesday's close of US$0.9548-0.9552.On the economic front, the Australian Bureau of Statistics or ABS releases its retail sales report for May. The data for April showed a 0.2% decline from the previous month. At the same time, the ABS also publishes its building construction approvals for May. The April data showed a 7.8% increase in approvals over March.Meanwhile, Australia's Department of Employment and Workplace Relations issues its report on skilled job vacancies for June. Last month, the data for May showed a 0.1% decrease in vacancies.Commonwealth Bank lost 1.20%, Westpac dipped 0.68% and National Australia Bank edged down 0.43%, but ANZ was up 0.34%. St George bank slipped 0.46%, while investment bank Macquarie Group lost 1.43%.Index leader BHP Billiton fell 2.34% and its takeover target Rio Tinto lost 2.33%. Gold miners were stronger after gold prices closed higher as investors turned to the metal as an inflation hedge. Lihir Gold gained 2.14% and Newcrest Mining was up 2.58%.
