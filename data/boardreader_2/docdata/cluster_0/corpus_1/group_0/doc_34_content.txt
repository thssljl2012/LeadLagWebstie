 (Adds ConnectEast)SYDNEY, July 7 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 2 percent on Monday as resource firms lost ground on weaker metals prices, while an earnings downgrade at GPT Group (ASX: GPT.ax) hit property shares.Top banks fell on persistent credit concerns, adding further pressure on the market. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index .AXJO fell 103.9 points to 4,978.2 by 0258 GMT, after rising 1.7 percent in the previous session to end a five-session slide.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index .NZ50 fell 0.4 percent, or 11.53 points, to 3,146.39. The most heavily weighted stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand (NZX: TEL.nz) , was down 0.3 percent at NZ$3.40. STOCKS ON THE MOVE* Toll road operator ConnectEast Group CEU.AX added 5.9 percent to A$1.07. Credit Sussie started coverage on ConnectEast with an outperform rating and a 12-month price target of A$1.58. The brokerage said concerns about initial traffic volumes at ConnectEast's new toll road were likely overplayed by the market and that it sees strong upside to the firm's current share price.0257 GMT* Bell Financial Group Ltd BFG.AX, which owns stock broking firm Bell Potter Securities, rose 13.5 percent to A$1.05. Bell Financial said it plans to buy Southern Cross Equities Ltd for about A$150 million ($144.2 million), a deal it said would create one of the largest Australian-owned broking businesses.It said in a separate statement that its net profit in the first-half to June fell 46 percent to A$9 million due to weaker corporate fee income and brokerage revenue.0146 GMT* Mining firms declined after the price of industrial metals such as copper, nickel and lead fell in London on Friday.BHP Billiton Ltd (ASX: BHP.ax) , the world's top miner and Australia's top oil and gas producer, shed 0.9 percent to A$40.35, while its main rival and takeover target, Rio Tinto Ltd (ASX: RIO.ax) , fell 1.1 percent to A$124.30. 0043 GMT* Property investor GPT Group dropped 16.7 percent to A$2.05 after cutting its earnings per share forecast for 2008 to 21.2 cents and its full-year distribution forecast to 20 cents per share, citing turmoil in financial markets [ID:nSYD96113].The news also hit other property-related firms.Westfield Group (ASX: WDC.ax) was down 3.5 percent at A$15.54, Stockland Group Ltd (ASX: SGP.ax) fell 5.6 percent to A$5.21 and Valad Property Group (ASX: VPG.ax) declined 6.3 percent to A$0.60. 0042 GMT* Financial firms declined after their European counterparts fell on Friday on worries about further writedowns.Goldman Sachs said European banks may need to raise 60-90 billion euros in total. The broker lowered 2008-09 estimates for than 40 banks and said the sector would be hampered by the risk of additional capital raisings.National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX: NAB.ax) lost 1.7 percent to A$27.07 and Commonwealth Bank of Australia Ltd (ASX: CBA.ax) fell 1.4 percent to A$41.75. Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Ltd (ASX: ANZ.ax) shed 2.1 percent to A$19.12 while Westpac Banking Corp (ASX: WBC.ax) was down 1.3 percent at A$20.03. 0037 GMT* Challenger Financial Group Ltd (ASX: CGF.ax) rose 2.4 percent to A$2.10 after it said it would buy back up to 10 percent of its share capital [ID:nSYD109924]. 0036 GMT ($1=A$1.04) (Reporting by Geraldine Chua) More Quotes and Company Information:AUSTRALIA AND NEW ZEALAND BANKING GROUP LIMITED (ASX: ANZ.ax)BHP BILLITON(ASX: BHP.ax)CHALLENGER FINANCIAL SERVICES GROUP LIMITED(ASX: CGF.ax)COMMONWEALTH BANK OF AUSTRALIA.(ASX: CBA.ax)GENERAL PROPERTY TRUST(ASX: GPT.ax)NATIONAL AUSTRALIA BANK LIMITED(ASX: NAB.ax)RIO TINTO LIMITED(ASX: RIO.ax)STOCKLAND(ASX: SGP.ax)VALAD PROPERTY GROUP(ASX: VPG.ax)WESTFIELD GROUP(ASX: WDC.ax)WESTPAC BANKING CORPORATION(ASX: WBC.ax)Telecom Corporation of New Zealand Limited(NZX: TEL.nz)
