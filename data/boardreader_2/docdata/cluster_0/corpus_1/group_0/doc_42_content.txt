 SYDNEY, July 15 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 0.7 percent to their lowest in over two years on Tuesday as financial firms came under fresh pressure on worries about further fallout from credit markets. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 share index .AXJO fell 34.6 points to 4,886.4 by 0033 GMT, a level not seen since June 21, 2006.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index .NZ50 fell 0.5 percent, or 14.38 points, to 3,065.72. The most heavily weighted stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand (NZX: TEL.nz) , was down 1.5 percent at NZ$3.27. STOCKS ON THE MOVE* Financial firms fell as persistent credit concerns continued to dampen sentiment in the sector.Among the big banks, National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX: NAB.ax) fell 1.6 percent to A$26.05 and Commonwealth Bank of Australia Ltd (ASX: CBA.ax) lost 2.6 percent to A$39.10. Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Ltd (ASX: ANZ.ax) shed 2.4 percent to A$17.38 while Westpac Banking Corp (ASX: WBC.ax) was down 2.7 percent at A$18.81. Macquarie Group MQG.AX, Australia's top investment bank, fell 2.5 percent to A$48.57. 0026 GMT* Resource firms rose after gold prices XAU= hit their highest level in nearly four months, while oil prices CLc1 hovered near $145 a barrel. BHP Billiton Ltd (ASX: BHP.ax) , the world's top miner and Australia's top oil and gas producer, added 0.7 percent to A$40.14, while its main rival and takeover target, Rio Tinto Ltd (ASX: RIO.ax) , rose 0.5 percent to A$124.12. Oil and gas firm Woodside Petroleum Ltd (ASX: WPL.ax) gained 1.8 percent to A$61.77 and gold miner Newcrest Mining (ASX: NCM.ax) rose 3.2 percent to A$33.50. 0024 GMT* Centro Properties Group CNP.AX, a high-profile victim of the global credit crunch, surged 28.3 percent to A$0.295 after it said it is selling almost all of the shopping malls in its Centro America Fund for $714 million, using the cash to pay down debt [ID:nSYD247196].0021 GMT* Allco Finance Group Ltd AFG.AX, an Australian asset manager which was hit by high debt concerns earlier this year, climbed 20 percent to A$0.45. Allco said it had reached agreement with its bankers on a new debt facility that does not contain any market capitalisation clause.
