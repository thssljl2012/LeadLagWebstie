 SYDNEY, July 29 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 1.5 percent on Tuesday as worries about growing credit-related losses sparked more selling among financial firms such as Commonwealth Bank of Australia Ltd . The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index  fell 71.8 points to 4,850.3 by 0204 GMT, after falling a total of 4.3 percent in the previous two sessions.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index  fell 0.7 percent, or 22.22 points, to 3,234.35.
