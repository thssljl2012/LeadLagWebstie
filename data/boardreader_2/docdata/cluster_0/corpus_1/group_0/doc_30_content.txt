  * Financials lead rally after Wall St jump, oil's drop * Falls in oil, gold hurt some resource firms * Aristocrat shares slump on profit warning (Adds details, comment)SYDNEY, July 30 (Reuters) - Australian shares rose 1.8 percent on Wednesday as a drop in oil prices brightened the outlook for global economic growth and company profits, spurring buying in banks and other recently battered stocks.Despite the gains, analysts said equity markets face a tough road to recovery given persistent worries about credit-related losses, deteriorating consumer sentiment and slowing corporate earnings.'For the time being, one would conclude that it is just a technical rebound after being oversold. It's still too early to say that the new bull market is being formed,' said Albert Hung, chief investment officer at Alleron Investment Management.'There will still be turbulence in credit markets and people are still concerned about energy costs and the general economic situation.'The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index .AXJO rose 89.3 points to 4,936.7, based on the latest available data, after dropping a total of 5.8 percent in the previous three sessions.The index has still lost 22.1 percent so far this year following an 11.8 percent gain in 2007.The index had been trading up 2 percent for most of the session, but dropped by nearly 100 points to trade flat at one point as a wrong trade at a brokerage saw shares in QBE Insurance Group Ltd (ASX: QBE.ax) sold for as little as a tenth of a cent [ID:nSYD315639].New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index .NZ50 rose 1.6 percent, or 52.26 points, to 3,287.76. The most heavily weighted stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand (NZX: TEL.nz) , closed up 2.5 percent at NZ$3.68.BANKSREBOUND, ARISTOCRAT SLUMPSFinancial firms rose as investors sought bargains in the sector which had been battered in the previous three sessions.National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX: NAB.ax) rose 2.7 percent to A$25.46 and Commonwealth Bank of Australia Ltd (ASX: CBA.ax) added 2.7 percent to A$40.39.Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Ltd (ASX: ANZ.ax) gained 3.7 percent to A$16.10 while Westpac Banking Corp (ASX: WBC.ax) rose 3.6 percent to A$20.94.Aristocrat Leisure Ltd (ASX: ALL.ax) slumped 20.2 percent to A$4.85, logging its biggest one-day fall since May 2003, after it cut its full-year profit forecast by up to 23 percent, citing deteriorating economic conditions in the U.S. and Australia and a strengthening Australian dollar.The games machine maker said it now sees fiscal 2008 operating profit between A$190 million ($181 million) and A$200 million.Some resource firms fell after oil prices CLc1 fell below $122 a barrel on mounting evidence high prices and a slowing economy were cutting into world energy demand, while gold prices XAU= fell to trade near a three-week low.Oil and gas firm Oil Search Ltd (ASX: OSH.ax) shed 2.7 percent to A$5.50, while among gold miners, Newcrest Mining (ASX: NCM.ax) fell 3.1 percent to A$28.50 and Sino Gold (ASX: SGX.ax) dropped 6.8 percent to A$5.08.Oil producer AED Oil AED.AX gained 7.7 percent to A$2.39.The firm announced a special dividend of A$0.20 per share, to be paid on Sept. 5, 2008, following the sale of a 60 percent stake of its assets to Sinopec Group's Hong Kong-listed unit Sinopec Corp 0386.HK for A$600 million ($571 million).
