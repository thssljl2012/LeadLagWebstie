 SYDNEY, July 11 (Reuters) - Australian shares rose 0.7 percent on Friday led up by resource firms such as BHP Billiton Ltd  and Woodside Petroleum Ltd  on gains in oil and metals prices.The market moved higher in afternoon trade as financial firms pared early losses after the New York Times reported that the U.S. government is considering taking over the top U.S. mortgage lenders, Fannie Mae  and Freddie Mac  [ID:nTKW002990].The report gave a boost to investors troubled by worries over the financial condition of the mortgage lenders. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index  rose 33.7 points to 4,971.1 by 0443 GMT, after trading little changed earlier in the session.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index  edged up 0.1 percent, or 3.75 points, to 3,116.31. The most heavily weighted stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand , was up 0.3 percent at NZ$3.38.
