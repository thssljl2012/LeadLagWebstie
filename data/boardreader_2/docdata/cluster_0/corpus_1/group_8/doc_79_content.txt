Australian shares are flat despite a solid start following a second session of gains on Wall Street.  In mid-morning trade, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was flat at 3244.4 after trading as much as 0.9% higher this morning. The broader All Ordinaries index was down 6 points at 3198.5.  The Australian dollar gained ground overnight, rising to 64.94 US cents recently from yesterday's local close of 64.32 US cents. It was also buying 63.24 yen and 50.66 euro cents.  Todd Kerslake of Shaw Stockbroking said Australian shares may gain but 'our market isn't going to run away' based on Wall Street's moderate rise.  'Overall, a lot of people will be keeping their powder dry and waiting to see if the upward movement is sustained before stepping back in.'  NAB soars  The financials sub-index posted the biggest gains in early trade, up 1.5% on strong gains by National Australia Bank. The materials sub-index was up 1.3%, but energy was down 0.6%.  This morning National Australia Bank announced an overhaul of the bank's management under the direction of new chief executive Cameron Clyne.  NAB also said its first-half dividend would be reduced by 25% as the bank refocuses on its domestic business amid continuing international uncertainty. Its shares jumped in early trading, gaining as much as 4.8%, or 79 cents, or $17.09.  In earlier trading, the Commonwealth Bank was up 30 cents, or 1%, at $28.91.  ANZ was up 8 cents, or 0.6%, at $13.23 and Westpac rose 13 cents, or 0.8%, to $17.00.  Investment bank Macquarie Group was up 37 cents, or 2%, at $18.63 and Suncorp Metway was up 4 cents, or 0.8%, at $18.63.  QBE Insurance rose 18 cents, or 1.1%, to $16.18 and IAG was up 4 cents, or 1.2%, at $3.26.  The big miners were higher. BHP Billiton added 52 cents, or 1.7%, to $31.02 and Rio Tinto gained $1.27, or 2.5%, to $51.07.  Fortescue Metals was up 1 cent, or 0.4%, at $2.46, OZ Minerals was down half a cent, or 0.8%, at 60.5 cents and BlueScope Steel was down 7 cents, or 2.9%, at $2.33.  Energy down  Energy stocks were lower after the price of oil slumped overnight. Nymex crude oil futures recovered some ground this morning, rising 47 US cents to recently trade at $US42.80 a barrel.  Woodside Petroleum was down 38 cents, or 1%, at $36.61 and Santos was down 15 cents, or 0.9%, at $15.77.  AGL Energy lost 7 cents, or 0.5%, to $13.31 and Origin was up 5 cents, or 0.3%, at $14.35. Macarthur Coal was down 3 cents, or 1.1%, at $2.69.  Spot gold was lower in recent trade, losing 47 US cents to $US908.95 and ounce.  Lihir Gold was up 9 cents, or 3.1%, at $2.95, Newcrest was up 34 cents, or 1.1%, at $31.25 and Newmont was up 15 cents, or 2.7%, at $5.65.  The retailers were mixed. Woolworths lost 2 cents, or 0.1%, to $25.48 and Wesfarmers, which owns Coles and Bunnings, was up 10 cents, or 0.6%, at $17.44.  Harvey Norman added 1 cents, or 0.5%, to $2.19, David Jones was down 1 cent, or 0.4%, at $2.27 and JB Hi Fi was down 1 cent, or 0.1%, at $10.29.  Shopping centre operator Westfield was up 14 cents, or 1.5%, at $9.44 and Lend Lease lost 10 cents, or 1.9%, to $5.23.
