) - The Australian stock market was trading higher on Wednesday, extending previous sessions strong gains, following a triple-digit gain on the Wall Street overnight. The local market was being driven higher by strong gains from the financial stocks, while resource stocks were weak. In early trading, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index was gaining 11 points or 0.32% to 3,463, after closing more than three percent higher on Tuesday. The broader All Ordinaries index was up 9 points or 0.27% to 3,403.  In the currency market, the Australian dollar opened stronger for a sixth straight day. The Aussie opened at US$0.6616-0.6620, up from Tuesday's close of US$0.6598-0.6602.  On Wall Street, U.S. stocks ended Tuesday's trading session higher, as some buying interest was generated by positive economic data from the Commerce Department showing an unexpected increase in housing starts in the month of February. The Dow closed up 179 points or 2.48% at 7,396, the Nasdaq gained 58 points or 4.14% to 1,462, and the S&amp;P 500 added 24 points or 3.21% to finish at 778.  On the economic front, the Westpac/Melbourne Institute leading Index, which signals the likely pace of economic activity three to nine months in the future, shrank by 3.1% in January, compared to a downwardly revised fall of 2.8% in December and a January annualized growth rate of 1.8%.  Among banking stocks, Commonwealth Bank of Australia was up 2.34%, ANZ Banking Group gained 2.22%, and National Australia Bank rose 1.58%. Westpac added 1.30%, and investment bank Macquarie Group surged 5.31%.  ANZ revealed that it would absorb its separately managed specialist asset finance business Esanda Finance Corp. Ltd. into the bank.  In the resources sector, index leader BHP Billiton fell 1.47%, and Rio Tinto dropped 6.54%. Gold miners were weaker, after gold closed lower for second straight session on Tuesday. Sino Gold dropped 5.17%, Newcrest Mining lost 3.15%, and Lihir Gold fell 2.30%.  On Tuesday, crude oil futures for April delivery closed higher, nearly touching the key $50 a barrel mark on the last day of April contract expiry, after some positive housing data provided encouragement for energy demand. Traders also await the weekly inventory report from the U.S. Energy Information Administration or EIA, which is released every Wednesday.  Oil closed up $1.81 at $49.16 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange on Tuesday, after hitting an intraday low of $46.53 and a high of $49.82. In the Asian session Wednesday, crude is currently down $0.38 at $48.78 a barrel in electronic trading.
