 (RTTNews) - The Australian stock market was trading lower on Wednesday, at new 5-year lows, following the release of weak GDP data as well as a negative lead from Wall Street overnight. Resources and financial stocks led the fall. Reserve Bank of Australia also left cash rate unchanged at 3.25 on Tuesday. However, the markets pared some losses after RBA issued an optimistic economic outlook later.  At 8:27 p.m. ET, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index was losing 62 points or 1.94% to 3,157, after closing nearly a percent lower on Tuesday at five-year lows. The broader All Ordinaries index was down 57 points or 1.78% to 3,115.  On Wall Street, U.S. stocks ended Tuesday's violate trading session modestly lower, keeping the Dow below the 7,000 mark for the first time since 1997, as investors reacted to a bombardment of news from the economic, corporate, and political fronts. The Dow closed down 37 points or 0.55% at 6,726, the Nasdaq lost 2 points or 0.14% to 1,321, and the S&amp;P 500 fell 4 points or 0.64% to finish at 696.  On the economic front, the Australian Bureau of Statistics' data showed that Australia's gross domestic product for the fourth quarter dropped 0.5% on quarter. On an annual basis, the Australian economy expanded 0.3%.  Further, the Australian Industry Group and Commonwealth Bank of Australia survey showed that Australia's Performance of Service Index declined 8.8 points in February to 32.2, marking the lowest point for the index since the survey began in 2003. Readings below 50.0 indicate contraction of activity in the surveyed sector.  Among banking stocks, Commonwealth Bank of Australia was down 3.25%, ANZ Banking Group fell 3.09%, and National Australia Bank dropped 1.62%. Westpac lost 1.99%, and investment bank Macquarie Group edged down 0.11%.  In the resources sector, index leader BHP Billiton fell 1.98%, and Rio Tinto lost 3.16%. Gold miners were weaker, after gold closed lower on Tuesday, extending its losing streak to seven days. Sino Gold dropped 6.84%, and Newcrest Mining lost 3.08%, while Lihir Gold is in a trading halt ahead of a US$325 million capital raising by institutional placement of ordinary shares.  On Tuesday, crude oil futures for April delivery rebounded and closed higher, staying above the key technical support level of $40 a barrel, as bargain-hunters capitalized on a sharp sell-off in the previous session. Traders also await the Energy Information Administration's weekly inventory report, which is released every Wednesday. Economists are looking for a build of about 1.5 million barrels.
