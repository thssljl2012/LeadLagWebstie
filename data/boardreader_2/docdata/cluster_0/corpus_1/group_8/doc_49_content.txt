By Miranda Maxwell MELBOURNE, March 6 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 1.4 percent on Friday to their lowest close since August 2003, led down by banks and miner BHP Billiton Ltd as concerns over the health of the global economy continued to weigh. Investors are nervous ahead of data expected to show a 25-year high in unemployment in the United States in February . U.S. stocks hit fresh 12-year lows on Thursday. 'It was kicked off by the U.S. and the dreadful numbers coming out of the market there. The market's getting very rattled about the inability of the (U.S.) administration to deal with the financial situation,' said Robert Hook, portfolio manager with S.G. Hiscock &amp; Co. 'Everyone is now completely convinced it's a longer and deeper recession at this stage. Investors are looking into early next year and not seeing particularly good news,' he said. The benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index fell 43.0 points to 3,145.5, according to the latest available data, taking losses for the week to 6 percent -- the biggest weekly loss since early December. New Zealand's benchmark NZX 50 index fell 20.4 points, or 0.8 percent, to 2,471.04. 'The catalyst that will change the markets' mood is positive, sustainable stories and we're not hearing any of that. So we are getting a losing streak on the back of that,' said Lucinda Chan, division director at Macquarie Private Wealth. Resources stocks slipped after oil and base metals prices retreated. Shares in BHP Billiton fell 2.3 percent to A$27.65. Financial stocks mostly fell, led by Macquarie Group which lost 4.6 percent to end at A$17.26. Shares in Australia and New Zealand Banking Group, which said that along with Merrill Lynch it had reached agreement with liquidators of a failed Australian broker to settle all claims for A$253 million ($162 million), fell 2.3 percent to A$12.36. The Australian government is lobbying local banks to cut fees, Prime Minister Kevin Rudd said on Friday, after a report found they were charging some of the rich world's highest banking costs at a time of economic crisis. Regional lender Bank of Queensland Ltd said it will cut 150 jobs, 10 percent of its staff, by the end of March. . Its shares fell 2 percent to A$6.44. Shares in investment fund Babcock &amp; Brown Power Ltd fell 9.8 percent to A$0.037 after news it will be removed from the benchmark index from March 20. Utility infrastructure owner Hastings Diversified Utilities Fund tumbled 32 percent to A$0.53 after cutting its annual dividend by more than half.
