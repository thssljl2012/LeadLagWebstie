THE Australian sharemarket opened higher after early gains by big miners and a marginal rise on Wall Street overnight. The benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 rose 27.2 points, or 0.84 per cent, to 3271.6, while the broader All Ordinaries gained 24.3 points (0.76 per cent) to 3223.4.   On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index contract was 35 points higher at 3264 on 7228 contracts.   The big miners were stronger - BHP Billiton added 1.67 per cent and rival Rio Tinto gained 2.07 per cent.   The spot price of gold rose to $US907.75 an ounce, putting on $US10.05 on Wednesday's local close.   The gold miners were stronger, as Newcrest added 31 cents to $31.22, Newmont gained 15c to $5.65 and Lihir found 9c at $2.95.   US stocks ended marginally higher overnight, and financial stocks led an early rally for the second straight session, pushing the Dow Jones Industrial Average up 3.91 points to close at 6930.4.   Austock Securities senior client adviser Michael Heffernan said early gains from the big miners were driving the bourse at the open, while investors responded positively to National Australia Bank's strategic review. National Australia Bank, the biggest mover in the top 20, is up 4 per cent after its strategy review this morning, which has gone down particularly well with investors so far today, Mr Heffernan said.   The others banks were mixed, with ANZ steady at $13.15, Westpac losing 7c to $16.80 and Commonwealth Bank finding 18c to $28.43.   In retail, Wesfarmers gained 23c, Harvey Norman put on 2c, David Jones dropped 2c and Woolworths fell 20c to $25.30.   Junior explorer Admiralty Resources was the most traded stock by volume. Its shares rose by 10 per cent to 2.2c.   Market turnover reached 206.96 million shares worth $543.75 million.   AAP
