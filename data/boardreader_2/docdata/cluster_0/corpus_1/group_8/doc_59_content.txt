At 1015 AEDT, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was down 26.6 points, or 0.84 per cent, at 3,127.9, while the broader All Ordinaries index lost 24.6 points, or 0.79 per cent, to 3,097.1.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract was 23 points lower at 3,126 on a volume of 4,275 contracts.  The big miners were lower, with market leader BHP Billiton down 36 cents, or 1.24 per cent, at $28.75 and rival Rio Tinto falling 66 cents, or 1.37 per cent, to $46.84.  The banking sector was mostly higher.  National Australia Bank was down 10 cents at $15.93, Commonwealth Bank added 28 cents to $26.92, ANZ increased two cents to $12.55 and Westpac added two cents to $15.92.  In the US on Monday, the Dow Jones industrial average fell 79.89 points, or 1.21 per cent, to 6,547.05.  The S&amp;P 500 index lost 6.85 to 676.53, while the Nasdaq composite index fell 25.21, or 1.95 per cent, to 1,268.64.  'It's going to be a fairly flat day, it could go 20 points either way,' ABN Amro Morgans private client adviser Bill Bishop said.  'We've got a fairly mixed bag today with the resources a little bit lower and not a very exciting lead from America overnight.'  Making headlines this Tuesday, Telstra said it would begin upgrading its capital cities cable broadband networks, starting with Melbourne. Its stock fell three cents to $3.25.  Battery recycling plant operator Hydromet Corp will receive a $3 million cash injection from a share placement to a Chinese company to fund its expansion.  Hydromet shares are in a trading halt. They closed at 4.1 cents on Monday.  David Jones is looking to attract a portion of the cash handouts due to be sent to Australian families this week by the federal government.  Its shares were four cents lower at $2.12.  Among other retailers, Woolworths fell 37 cents to $25.34, Coles owner Wesfarmers rose six cents to $16.87, Harvey Norman added two cents to $2.02 and JB Hi-Fi sank 16 cents to $9.65.  At 1040 AEDT, the spot price of gold in Sydney was $US920.25 per fine ounce, down $US16.75 on Monday's local close of $USS937.00.  Newcrest was down 52 cents at $30.88, Newmont Mining lost 13 cents to $5.86, Lihir Gold fell eight cents to $3.07.  Energy stocks were higher, with Santos up 20 cents at $15.92, Woodside added 74 cents to $36.63 and Oil Search 12 cents higher at $4.95.  The most traded stock was Telstra, with 11.41 million shares worth $41.06 million changing hands by 1045 AEDT.  Market turnover was 217.44 million shares traded for a value of $657.95 million, with 191 stocks up, 330 down and 193 unchanged.
