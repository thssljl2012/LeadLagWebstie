THE resources sector boosted the stock market today, which rose after small gains on Wall Street on Friday.  Higher commodity prices also helped the rise in the Australian share market after the opening bell. The benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index rose 15.4 points, or 0.49 per cent, to 3160.9, while the broader All Ordinaries index gained 14.5 points (0.47 per cent) to 3126.2.   On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract was 10 points higher at 3156, on 5443 contracts.   The big miners were stronger, with market leader BHP Billiton up $1.27 (4.59 per cent) to $28.92 and rival Rio Tinto up 95 cents (2.08 per cent) to $46.65.   The banking sector was mostly weaker.   National Australia Bank was down 15c at $16.65, Commonwealth Bank lost 35c to $26.65 and Westpac fell 7c to $15.63, but ANZ added 8c to $12.44.   In the US on Friday, stocks gained some lost ground after a jobs report was not as soft as many traders had feared.   The US unemployment rate rose 0.5 percentage points to 8.1 per cent in February as firms shed 651,000 workers during the month.   The Dow Jones Industrial Average added 32.5 points (0.49 per cent) to 6626.94 and the Standard &amp; Poor's 500 index gained 0.83 (0.12 per cent) to 683.38, while the Nasdaq Composite Index fell 5.74 (0.44 per cent) to 1293.85.
