Shares briefly fell over 2% this afternoon after a number of erroneous trades in insurer QBE, before the trades were cancelled and the index returned to trading flat, amid a quiet session due to a public holiday in Victoria.  In late trade, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index was down 0.03%, or 1 point, at 3144.5.  'There's just so much ambiguity there still ... there does not seem to be any concrete, unambiguous resolution, or way forward for financials offshore, and as long as that's the case it's going to be very hard for our financials to make headwind themselves,'' said Peter Wright, dealer at Burrell &amp; Co before the slip in QBE trade.  The majority of financial stocks fell, led by a 4.4% fall in NAB to $16.06. No. 3 lender, Commonwealth Bank was down 1.5% at $26.60.  Victoria was marking the Labour Day public holiday on Monday.  BHP surges  The big miners bucked the negative trend and posted string gains.  BHP Billiton, the biggest company on the stock exchange, rose $1.50, or 5.4%, to $29.15 and rival Rio Tinto gained $2.23, or 4.9%, to $47.93.  Copper prices rose on Friday to take the weekly gain to nearly 10%, as another drop in stockpiles resuscitated hopes of a demand pick-up in China.  Iron ore miner Fortescue Metals Group said it was working to catch up on lost output after recent rains. The shares gained 9 cents, or 3.6%, to $2.59.  Gold and oil also gained.  Making headlines this morning, Rio says further development of the Oyu Tolgoi joint venture in Mongolia needs to be carefully planned amid the economic downturn, as the company awaits an investment agreement from the government.  Shares in Nexus Energy have been placed in a trading halt while the company assesses proposals regarding possible asset sales. It last traded at 38 cents.  Santos gained 44 cents, or 2.9%, to $15.64, Woodside added 60 cents to $36.15 and Oil Search gained six cents to $4.87.  The spot price of gold in Sydney was $US938.4 per fine ounce, up $US0.25 on Friday's local close of $US938.15.  Gold miner Newcrest advanced 88 cents to $31.95, Newmont Mining fell 26 cents to $5.90, Lihir Gold was flat at $3.15.  Insurers slump  Meanwhile, the insurers fell again, with QBE losing 78 cents, or 4.8%, to $15.52, Insurance Australia Group declining 12 cents, or 3.7%, to $3.10 and AMP slipping 12 cents, or 3.1%, to $3.71.  'With what happened with AIG (US insurance giant American International Group, which was taken over by the government), our insurers are being slammed,'' Wilson HTM's Mr Pagliaro said.  AGL Energy says Telstra chief financial officer John Stanhope will join its board as a non-executive director. Its shares declined 15 cents to $13.30.  Printing group PMP is offering shareholders with small parcels of shares the chance to sell their stock without incurring brokerage fees.  Its stock lost 1 cent to 27.5 cents.  US shares mixed  In the US on Friday, stocks gained some lost ground after a jobs report was not quite as bad as many traders had feared.  The US unemployment rate rose 0.5 percentage points to 8.1% in February as firms shed 651,000 workers during the month.  The Dow Jones added 0.5% and the S&amp;P 500 index gained 0.1%, while the Nasdaq fell 0.4%.
