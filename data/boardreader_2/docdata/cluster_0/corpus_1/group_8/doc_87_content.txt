 Market watch top headlines Australian reports Aust markets:  :  :  World reports World commodities:  World markets:   AXA, ANZ, DJS, PPT, AWB,   AAP 2009-03-18   The Australian stock market slipped into negative territory at noon after stronger opening following a positive lead from Wall Street.  At 1205 AEDT the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 was down 2.4 points, or 0.07 per cent, at 3449.5, while the broader All Ordinaries fell 3.1 points, or 0.09 per cent, to 3390.3.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange the March share price index contract was 22 points lower at 3457 on a volume of 63,592 contracts.  ABN Amro Morgans private client adviser Bill Bishop said the local market had given up its morning gains. 'We started off with a hell of a bang and it's easing off a little now,' he said. Mr Bishop said the local banks were lifting the market, while the big miners were pulling the market lower. 'We've seen quite nice rises in all the banks. 'The energy stocks are doing okay. 'However, BHP Billiton is down and Rio Tinto is back quite a long way - it's down amid opposition to the Chinalco deal.' HP Billiton lost 90 cents, or 2.73 per cent, to $31.00 and rival Rio Tinto shed $4.11 to $47.86 by 1227 AEDT.  However, the market returned into positive territory in the first few minutes of the early afternoon and by 1222 AEDT the S&amp;P/ASX200 up 5.8 point, or 0.17 per cent, to 3458.1 and the All Ordinaries was up 3.8 points, or 0.11 per cent, to 3397.2.  Rio Tinto has appointed non-executive director and British American Tobacco chairman Jan du Plessis as the global miner's new chairman, replacing incumbent Paul Skinner in April.  Australian political opposition is intensifying over Rio Tinto's planned $19.5 billion tie-up with China's state-owned Chinalco.  The local market received a positive lead from Wall Street after shares rallied on an upbeat report on home construction and pushed the Dow Jones Industrial Average 178.73 points higher to 7,395.70.  The energy sector was stronger at 1231 AEDT, with Woodside gaining 15 cents to $36.92, Santos adding 31 cents to $15.47 and Oil Search putting on one cent to $5.19.  Upmarket retailer David Jones gained 16 cents, or 6.43 per cent, to $2.65 after lifting its first half profit by 2.4 per cent and affirming its guidance, saying it is well prepared for the economic downturn.  The other retailers were mixed, with Woolworths adding 12 cents to $25.46 by 1232 AEDT, Wesfarmers adding 20 cents to $18.60 and Harvey Norman falling 10 cents to $2.48.  AXA Asia Pacific Holdings added 19 cents to $3.41 by 1232 AEDT after the group raised $500 million from a placement of shares to institutional investors, with its largest shareholder taking up more than half of the offer.  The media sector was mostly stronger. News Corp added four cents to $10.56 by 1233 AEDT, its non-voting shares gained 11 cents to $9.39, Fairfax picked up five cents to 98 cents, Consolidated Media Holdings added one cent to $2.14, while APN News &amp; Media lost 6.5 cents to $1.275.  National Australia Bank added 11 cents to $19.04 by 1238 AEDT, ANZ gained 23 cents to $14.66, Commonwealth Bank put on 48 cents to $32.95 and Westpac found 15 cents to $17.86.  The spot price of gold was trading at $US914.25 an ounce by 1238 AEDT, down $US5.85 on Tuesday's local close of $US920.10 an ounce.  The gold miners were weaker, with Newcrest shedding 72 cents to $31.38 by 1239 AEDT, Lihir falling five cents to $2.99 and Newmont losing 27 cents to $5.50.  Telstra was the most traded stock, with 179.37 million shares changing hands, worth $179.37 million.  The telco was steady at $3.03 by 1240 AEDT.  Market turnover was 854.12 million shares for a value of $1.95 billion, with 493 stocks moving up, 285 stocks down and 238 unchanged.
