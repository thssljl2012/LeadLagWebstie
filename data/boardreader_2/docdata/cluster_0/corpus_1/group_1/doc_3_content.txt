 (RTTNews) - The Australian stock market was trading flat on Thursday after a firm opening and Wednesday's 3% rally. Investors locked in profits, despite Wall Street's extended gains overnight and another drop in crude oil prices. At 9:11 p.m. ET, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index was down 3 points or 0.06% at 4,966 and the broader All Ordinaries index was trading flat at 5,019.In the currency market, the Australian dollar opened weaker on Thursday after the greenback strengthened as oil and commodity prices continued their fall. The Aussie opened at near four-month lows of US$0.9160-0.9165, down from Wednesday's close of US$0.9218-0.9222.On the economic front, the Australian Bureau of Statistics publishes its July employment data. For June, unemployment rate was 4.2%. The employment participation rate for June stood at 65.3%, with 29,800 workers added to payrolls in the month.Among banking stocks, Commonwealth Bank edged up 0.21%, while ANZ Bank edged down 0.33% and Westpac lost 0.61%. National Australia Bank lost 0.85%, while St. George bank added 0.35%, and investment bank Macquarie Group was up 0.47%.In the resources sector, index leader BHP Billiton was up 1.59% and Rio Tinto gained 1.70%. Gold miners were mixed after gold closed lower on Wednesday, in line with oil prices. Newcrest Mining dropped 2.83%, while Lihir Gold was up 2.99%.On Wednesday, crude oil for September delivery closed marginally lower, dropping over $6 in three straight sessions and nearly $30 off its record highs of $147.27 hit on July 11. The U.S. Weekly inventory data showed an increase of 1.7 million barrels in crude supplies, while analysts had expected a drop of about 1.3 million barrels. Oil closed down $0.59 at $118.58 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange, after touching intraday lows of $117.17. In Asian trade Thursday, crude was up $0.24 at $118.82.Among energy stocks, Woodside was up 1.50%, while Oil Search advanced 0.98%, and Santos gained 1.15%.
