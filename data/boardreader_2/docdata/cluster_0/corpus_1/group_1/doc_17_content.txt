 U.S. stock indexes on Monday dropped nearly 2%, retreating from last session's strong gains, as oil reached above $115 a barrel and as concerns about the financial sector continued to weigh on investor sentiment. 'Financial stresses are still permeating global financial systems, despite massive accommodation from the Fed,' said analysts at Action Economics. The Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJI) was recently down 225.20 points, or 1.9%, to 11,402.86. All of the Dow's 30 components posted declines, led by insurance giant American International Group Inc. (AIG), down 5.3% after Credit Suisse analysts lowered their third-quarter profit estimate and target price on the insurance giant. The S&amp;amp;P 500 (SPX) was off 22.72 points, or 1.8%, to 1,269.48, with all of the index's 10 industry groups tallying losses, paced by the consumer discretionary, financial and material sectors, all down more than 2%. Among the hardest hit, shares of Lehman Brothers Holdings Inc. (LEH) lost 7.6% . Shares of Fannie Mae (FNM) and Freddie Mac (FRE) both gained, with the latter recently up 15.3%, amid ongoing speculation that a government bailout of both could be unavoidable. . The Nasdaq Composite (RIXF) declined 48.79 points, or 2%, to 2,365.92. Trading volume was light, topping 307 million on the New York Stock Exchange, where declining issues overtook those advancing more than 3 to 1. On the Nasdaq, 238 million shares exchanged hands, and advancers topped decliners roughly 4 to 1. Still for sale Down from the session's start, stock indexes retained their losses after the National Association of Realtors reported existing-home sales rose in July, but inventories also increased, hitting record levels. On Friday, stocks rallied, with the Dow striking its largest one-day gain in two weeks, after oil fell more than $6 a barrel, its greatest percentage decline in more than four years. In Monday trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange, crude-oil futures were lately up 56 cents at $115.15 a barrel. . In corporate developments, Canadian energy-services provider Precision Drilling Trust plans to buy Grey Wolf Inc. (GW) for an estimated $1.12 billion in cash and 42 million shares of its stock. . Overseas, stocks in Europe drifted lower in thin trade, paced by a decline in banking shares. .
