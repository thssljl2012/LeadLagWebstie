 HOUSE prices in Brisbane rose slightly in the June quarter despite a trend which saw a widely anticipated drop in many cities across the nation.Economists had expected a fall of 1.0 per cent for the June quarter.The figures are expected to worsen with . The ABS report adds to a slew of other data showing the economy is slowing sharply, raising speculation the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) will cut interest rates before the end of the year.The RBA holds its monthly board meeting tomorrow, but economists expect it to keep its key cash rate unchanged for a fifth straight month.Cities where prices rose included Darwin (+1.9%), Brisbane (+0.6%), Adelaide (+0.4%) and Sydney (+0.3%). But Perth suffered the biggest drop in house prices, falling 2.4 per cent in the June quarter, and are now dropping at an annual rate of 0.9 per cent.Hobart reported a 2.0 per cent decline and Canberra a 1.4 per cent fall.Darwin saw the biggest increase with a rise of 1.9 per cent, while the country's largest housing market - Sydney - rose 0.3 per cent.Other private data also showed the country's job prospects are looking gloomy with businesses further winding back hiring intentions.Job advertisements fell for a third successive month, according to the ANZ bank.It says newspaper and internet job ads fell by a seasonally-adjusted 0.3 per cent in July.ANZ co-head of economics Warren Hogan said jobs growth was likely to slow in coming months, and predicted the jobless rate would climb to 4.9 per cent by June 2009, up from 4.2 per cent in June 2008.Official labour force data is released on Thursday.
