 SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) -- Canadian stocks finished with losses Monday, as the financial sector led a broad-based slump. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/TSX Composite Index ended down 158.33 points, or 1.2%, to 13,288.96. On the Toronto Stock Exchange, decliners led advancers 867 to 616. All but one of the TSX's 10 component sector gauges finished down, led by financials, which shed 1.9%, and information technology, which lost 1.8%. BlackBerry maker Research In Motion, which has a weighting equal to about three-quarters of the S&amp;amp;P/TSX's technology component, was down 3.1%. Financial shares slumped in line with their U.S. counterparts, which also led broad declines. Toronto-Dominion Bank and Bank of Montreal both lost 2%. Manulife Financial Corp. lost 1.8%, after Credit Suisse analysts lowered their third-quarter profit estimate and target price on U.S. insurance giant American International Group Inc. (AIG) . The most actively traded issue in volume terms, data-center company Q9 Networks Inc., surged 28.5%. Private-equity firm Abry Partners LLC agreed to buy Q9 C$361 million ($345 million), or C$17.05 for each share outstanding of Q9, a 31% premium to Friday's closing price. In currency trading, the Canadian dollar was at $C1.0498 against its U.S. counterpart, which rose 0.3%.
