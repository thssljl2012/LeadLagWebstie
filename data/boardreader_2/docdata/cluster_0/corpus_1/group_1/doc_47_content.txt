 (RTTNews) - The Australian stock market was trading sharply higher Wednesday, reversing the three-day negative trend. Wall Street provided a positive lead, with high triple-digit gains Tuesday, following the Federal Reserve's decision to hold interest rates and another sharp drop in oil prices. The local market closed Tuesday at a two and a half year low with heavy losses in the resources sector due to weaker commodity prices.At 9:11 p.m. ET, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index was up 145 points or 3.00% at 4,965 and the broader All Ordinaries index was gaining 135 points or 2.77% to 5,017.In the currency market, the Australian dollar opened weaker on Wednesday after the central bank left interest rates unchanged at 7.25% on Tuesday and after the greenback strengthened on a fall in oil and commodity prices. The Aussie opened at near four-month lows of US$0.9160-0.9165, down from Tuesday's close of US$0.9216-0.9219.On the economic front, the Australian Industry Group publishes its Performance of Construction index for July. The index reading for June was 40.3, with readings below 50.0 indicating contraction in the sector.Further, the Australian Bureau of Statistics releases its data on home loans and investment lending for June. Home loan totals for May showed a decline of 7.9 percent on month.Apart from these, SEEK.com.au releases its employment index for July.Among banking stocks, Commonwealth Bank climbed 4.90%, ANZ Bank rose 3.88%, and Westpac gained 3.98%. National Australia Bank added 3.88%, St. George bank increased 4.30%, and investment bank Macquarie Group was up 4.07%.In the resources sector, index leader BHP Billiton was up 1.93% and Rio Tinto gained 2.44%. Gold miners were weak, as gold fell again on Tuesday in line with oil prices. Newcrest Mining dropped 1.94%, and Lihir Gold fell 1.24%.On Tuesday, crude oil for September delivery plunged for a second consecutive day as Tropical Storm Edouard spared the key offshore rigs along the Gulf of Mexico and weakening demand. Oil closed down $2.24 at $119.17 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange, after touching intraday lows of $118.00. In Asian trade Wednesday, crude was down $0.91 at $118.26. Among energy stocks, Woodside was up 1.15%, while Oil Search edged down 0.38%, and Santos slipped 2.54%.
