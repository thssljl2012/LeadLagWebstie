  (RTTNews) - The Australian stock market closed sharply lower Tuesday, with both the financial and resources sectors ending in the red. The market started off lower, tracking a weak lead from Wall Street, and extended its losses. More than A$25 billion was wiped off the value of the market. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index closed down 118.6 points or 2.4% at 4,866.4, its lowest close since August 5. The broader All Ordinaries index dropped 113.1 points or 2.2% to finish at 4,930.4.Overnight, the Dow fell 1.55% on news that the U.S. Government might have to bail out mortgage finance giants Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac. The S&amp;amp;P 500 shed 1.51% and the Nasdaq lost 1.45%.Crude oil closed down US$0.90 at a three-month low of US$112.87 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange on Monday as the tropical Storm Fay steered clear of oil-producing infrastructure in the Gulf of Mexico. In the Asian session Tuesday, oil was at $112.02 a barrel, down 85 cents, by 2:07 a.m. ET.In the currency market, the Australian dollar closed weaker after a drop in the euro and gold prices boosted the U.S. dollar. The release of the minutes of the Reserve Bank of Australia'sAugust 5 board meeting hardly impacted the domestic currency, as the market had already factored in a September interest rate cut. The local unit finished the session at US$0.8642-0.8645, down from Monday's close of US$0.8746-0.8750.On the economic front, the minutes of the Reserve Bank of Australia'sAugust 5 policy meeting showed that the central bank was poised to consider a drop from the current 12-year high cash rate of 7.5%. The Reserve Bank of Australia Policy Board thinks that an interest rate reduction might be necessary to help steer the nation's economy away from a deeper economic slowdown. The Board's next meeting is scheduled for September 2.Meanwhile, the Australian Bureau of Statistics reported that merchandise imports in Australia increased in July to A$19.66 billion from a revised A$17.52 billion in June.The financial sector posted sharp losses. National Australia Bank and Westpac Banking Group fell 3.0% each, while Commonwealth Bank lost 2.7% and Australia and New Zealand Banking Group plunged 3.7%. Takeover target St George Bank dropped 2.8% and investment bank Macquarie Group plummeted 4.0%.Among insurers AXA Asia Pacific tumbled 4.2%, QBE Insurance shed 1.9% and AMP slumped 5.2%, but Insurance Australia advanced 0.5%.Big miners lost ground, on weaker base metals prices, despite strong results from BHP Billiton Monday. Index leader BHP Billiton plunged 4.2%, while rival Rio Tinto shed 3.8%. Gold producer Newcrest Mining added 1.7%, but Lihir Gold fell 1.8%.Energy stocks closed lower on weaker crude oil prices. Sector leader Woodside Petroleum lost 2.3%, Santos plunged 5.0% and Oil Search gave away 3.1% even though the company said that net profit for the six months to June increased more than five-fold to US$264.4 million.In the retail sector, David Jones closed little changed, while Wesfarmers shed 1.1% and Woolworths climbed 0.5%.Construction and material firm Boral shed 1.3% after the company announced that its full-year net profit fell 19% due to weakness in the U.S. housing market. Virgin Blue slumped 27.9% after it posted a 55% drop in net profit for 2007-2008 and warned that conditions would remain challenging.GWA International tumbled 6.1% after the company reported that its annual profit declined to its lowest in seven years. Babcock &amp;amp; Brown plunged 23.5% on speculation that its chief executive had resigned and on concerns about its funding position and business model.
