Gold's huge rally came as the government moved overnight to rescue troubled insurer American International Group Inc. with an $85 billion bailout loan. Gold for December delivery rose as much as $90.40, or 11.6 percent, to $870.90 an ounce in after-hours trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange after jumping $70 to settle at $850.50 in the regular session. That was the biggest one-day price jump ever; gold's previous single-day record was a $64 gain on Jan. 29, 1980. In percentage terms, it was gold's largest one-day advance since 1999.In other commodities, crude oil rebounded after the AIG bailout eased worries that the insurance giant and other financial firms would liquidate commodities holdings to raise cash.Light, sweet crude for October delivery rose $6.01, or 6.59 percent, to settle at $97.16 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
