The Australian sharemarket was higher at noon after gains in the financial sector and following Wall Street's rally on Friday.  At 1200 AEDT, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index had gained 117.9 points, or 3.38 per cent, to 3,607.8, while the broader All Ordinaries was up 105.3 points, or 3.07 per cent, at 3,532.5.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the December share price index contract was up 98 points at 3,621 on a volume of 15,760 contracts.  ABN AMRO Morgans private client adviser Bill Bishop said it was 'green across the screen'. 'The American market went up on Friday because the employment figures were so bad the market was hoping the government would pump more cheap money into the economy,' Mr Bishop said. 'There has been a lot of optimism after the rally on the American market. 'Our banks are up between 2.5 and five per cent and it has been a very good start to the day.' Commonwealth Bank rose $1.77, or 5.73 per cent to $32.67, National Australia Bank increased 98 cents, or 4.95 per cent, to $20.79, Westpac added 55 cents, or 3.26 per cent, to $17.40 and ANZ advanced 39 cents, or 2.67 per cent, to $14.99.  Rio shares fell 36 cents, or 1.12 per cent, to $31.64 but BHP Billiton shares lifted 56 cents, or 2.14 per cent, to $26.71.  On Friday, the Dow industrials jumped 3.09 per cent to 8,635.42.  The Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index rose 3.65 per cent to 876.07, and the Nasdaq composite index rose 4.41 per cent to 1,509.31.  In news on Monday, retailer Gerry Harvey warned that some of the stores in his Harvey Norman empire will close during the next six months because of the economic slowdown.  Harvey Norman shares increased six cents or 2.55 per cent to $2.41.  Pacific Brands Ltd stock fell half a cent to 60 cents after the company announced that it was not considering a capital raising, although deteriorating economic and retail conditions had forced it to reconsider its dividend policy.  At 1212 AEDT, other retailers were stronger.  Wesfarmers, which owns Coles, was 58 cents or 3.51 per cent higher at $17.09, Woolworths rose $1.26, or 4.83 per cent, to $27.35 and David Jones gained eight cents, or 2.88 per cent, $2.86.  James Hardie Industries NV says a dramatic rise in its share price last week was in line with its counterparts in the US, where the majority of its sales are made.  James Hardie was 20 cents higher, or by 4.84 per cent, at $4.33.  In energy, Woodside Petroleum picked up $1.79, or 5.88 per cent, at $32.25, Santos lifted $1.23, or 10.12 per cent, to $13.38 and Oil Search increased 12 cents, or three per cent, to $4.12.
