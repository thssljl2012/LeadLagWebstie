(RTTNews) - The Australian stock market closed higher on Wednesday, ending a two-day losing streak, as investors shrugged off a weak overnight lead from Wall Street and a fall in commodity prices. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index closed up 50.8 points or 1.4% at 3,582.2, in the shortened session ahead of Christmas holidays. The broader All Ordinaries index advanced 46.8 points or 1.3% to finish at 3,515.0.  In the currency market, the Australian dollar was quoted at US$0.6791 by late afternoon, down from US$0.6837 late Tuesday in domestic trade.  U.S. stocks plunged Tuesday on the back of worse-than-expected housing data and confirmation that the U.S. economy shrank in the third quarter. The Dow closed down 100.3 points or 1.2% at 8,419.5, the Nasdaq shed 10.8 points or 0.7% to 1,521.5 and the S&amp;amp;P 500 dropped 8.5 points or 1.0% to 863.16.  Crude oil was little changed near $39 a barrel in Asian trade Wednesday as demand for energy fell on the back of a deepening global recession. At 11:38 p.m. ET, oil was quoted at $39.10 a barrel, up 12 cents, after the contract for delivery in February fell 93 cents to $38.98 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange on Tuesday.  Investors had little economic data to digest on Wednesday.  In the resources sector, global miner BHP Billiton rose 1.2% and Rio Tinto jumped 3.5%. Oil and gas producer Woodside Petroleum climbed 2.3% and Santos gained 2.3%, but gold miner Newcrest Mining fell 2.0% and Lihir Gold lost 1.4%.  AGL Energy dropped 1.2% after it made an A$171 million takeover offer for coal seam gas producer Sydney Gas. Sydney Gas surged up 54.5%.  Banks finished higher. National Australia Bank advanced 2.6%, Westpac rose 1.5%, and Commonwealth Bank edged up 0.4%, while ANZ closed unchanged. Commonwealth Bank said that it would acquire up to A$4 billion of mortgages originated by Wizard Home Loans.  In the retail sector, David Jones climbed 1.7%, Woolworths jumped 2.6%, and Coles owner Wesfarmers surged up 4.4%.
