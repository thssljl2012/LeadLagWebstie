Mining stocks have pushed the Australian share higher, as the bourse shrugged off falls on Wall Street and on commodity markets.  The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index closed up 1.4%, or 50.8 points, to 3582.2. The stock market closed at 2.10pm and will not trade again until Monday.  Shares in Woodside Petroleum closed up 74 cents, or 2.3%, to $32.90.  Commonwealth Bank shares also rose after the bank said it would acquire up to $4 billion of Wizard originated home loans.  The acquisition of 'prime mortgages' was in conjunction with the purchase by Aussie Home Loans of the Wizard brand and distribution network, the bank said.  Shares in CBA closed up 11 cents, or 0.4%, to $27.15.  National Australia Bank gained 51 cents, or 2.6%, to $20.11, ANZ shares were unchanged at $14.87, and Westpac gained 24 cents, or 1.5%, to $16.50.
