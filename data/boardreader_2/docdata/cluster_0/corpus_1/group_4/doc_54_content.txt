Babcock &amp;amp; Brown called a trading halt on Thursday, it last traded at 25 cents.  Its rival Macquarie Group was down by $1.56, or 6.2 per cent, at $23.60.  The resource giants were also down heavily.  BHP Billiton decreased $1.47, or 6.34 per cent, to $21.73 while rival Rio Tinto was down by $5.23, or 7.94 per cent, at $60.67.  On Wall Street on Wednesday, Wall Street hit levels not seen since 2003, with the Dow Jones industrial average falling below the 8,000 mark, as the fate of Detroit's Big Three auto makers and the economy disheartened investors.  The Dow finished down 427.37 points, or 5.07 per cent, at 7,997,28.  The Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index fell 52.54 points, or 6.12 per cent, to 806.58, and the Nasdaq slumped 96.85, or 6.53 per cent, to 1,386.42.  ABN Amro Morgans private client adviser Bill Bishop said no Australian stock or sector was safe after the lows seen on the US market overnight.  'It's pretty much red everywhere,' Mr Bishop said.  'The banking sector is taking a savage hit and all the resources are being hit pretty hard.  'The market has now descended to a more than 50 per cent drop from November 1 last year.'
