(Adds Nexus Energy, updates indices)  SYDNEY, Nov 17 (Reuters) - Australian shares sank to a fresh four-year low on Monday, undermined by another dark day on Wall Street as investors voiced disappointment at efforts by G20 nations to stabilise markets and revive the world economy.  Australian stocks fell as much as 3.5 percent in thin morning trade before recovering some ground, and stock analysts said the market would remain subdued.  At 0412 GMT, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index  stood 64.6 points lower at 3,683.5, off a low of 3,616.4, its weakest level since October 2004.  New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index  closed down 0.9 percent at 2,741.9 points.  STOCKS ON THE MOVE * Nexus Energy's  shares rose 1.8 percent to A$0.57. The company said it had been granted an exploration permit in the Browse Basin off Western Australia.
