SYDNEY, Nov 26 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 0.9  percent on Wednesday as the market's three-session rise  encouraged some investors to take profits in banks, while Rio  Tinto Ltd dived after BHP Billiton Ltd dropped  its takeover offer.  The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index lost 32.2 points to  three sessions. The index jumped 5.8 percent on Tuesday for its  biggest one-day percentage gain since October 1997.  New Zealand shares were steady at 2,632.8 points.  STOCKS ON THE MOVE  * Toll road operator ConnectEast Group fell 21.2  percent to A$0.51 as it resumed trading after raising A$295  million through an institutional share placement. The shares  dropped below the offer price of A$0.55 each.  0200 GMT  * Rio shares fell as much as 36.2 percent to A$40.80 -- its  lowest level since January 2005 -- as traders who had bet on the  success of the takeover exited the stock. BHP shares firmed 7.5  percent to A$28.19.  0058 GMT  * NRW Holdings Ltd, an engineering contractor,  slumped 37 percent to A$0.28 after Fortescue Metals Group Ltd halted construction work on a rail line from its mine  site in Western Australia.  Fortescue was off 5.1 percent at A$1.945.  0057 GMT  * Banks, which led the recent uptrend along with miners,  fell back. Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Ltd  slipped 2.5 percent to A$14.18 and Commonwealth Bank of  Australia Ltd lost 3.2 percent to A$32.89.  2338 GMT  * A sharp fall in oil prices pressured energy stocks.  Woodside Petroleum Ltd slipped 5.4 percent to A$31.70  and Santos Ltd lost 4.4 percent to A$13.92.  2338 GMT  ($1=A$1.53)  (Reporting by Denny Thomas;)  ((denny.thomas@reuters.com; +61 2 9373 1812; Reuters Messaging:  denny.thomas.reuters.com@reuters.net))  DIARIES&amp;amp;DATA: IPO diary&amp;amp;data Asia earnings diary U.S. earnings diary European diary Australia diary Wall Street Week Ahead Eurostocks Week Ahead ................................................................
