midday  10:11am   Gov to reduce super red tape  10:11am   ANZ to sell UBS 75 million shares  09:42am   10,000 bank jobs could go: Union  09:42am   Property market improving: Mirvac  08:58am   Market stronger on back of energy stocks  08:54am   Austal secures US$185m US Navy contract  08:40am   Kiernan underpins Redbank Mines with $6m  08:40am   WA harvest nears record level: CBH  The Australian sharemarket remained strong at noon with overall gains in major sectors, boosted by a rebound on Wall St overnight.  At 1200 AEDT, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index was up 86.2 points, or 2.33 per cent, at 3783.5, while the broader All Ordinaries index had risen 84.9 points, or 2.31 per cent, to 3757.3 points.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the December share price index futures contract was 60 points higher at 3813 on a volume of 18,986 contracts.  Aberdeeen Asset Management Australia director of equities Mark Daniels said the rebound in US stocks had driven the gains.  'It's the bounce-back from the states,' Mr Daniels said.  'You've got Wall St up six per cent, having been down earlier in the day, hence our market opened up quite strongly, although it has come back a little but since then.'  Mr Daniels said major sectors, including energy and banking stocks, had led the gains.  Energy and resources stocks bounced back after the price of oil increased overnight.  At 1203 AEDT, mining giant BHP Billiton had risen $1.28, or 5.12 per cent to $26.28, while its rival Rio Tinto was up $3.90, or 5.65 per cent, to $72.90.  Australia's biggest oil and gas producer, Woodside Petroleum, was up $1.57, or 4.25 per cent, to $38.47.  Among the other energy stocks, Oil Search had gained 15 cents, or 3.16 per cent, to $4.90 and Santos had risen 70 cents, or 5.41 per cent to $13.65.  Making headlines today, media reports say ANZ bank could cut up to 3,500 jobs - or ten per cent of its workforce - as the world financial crisis takes its toll.  At 1213 AEDT, shares in ANZ had risen 17 cents, or 1.21 per cent to $14.22.  Among the other banks, Commonwealth Bank shares were up 58 cents, or 1.76 per cent, to $33.58, NAB had risen by 67 cents, or 3.45 per cent, to $20.07, while Westpac had shed 12 cents to $16.85.  As St George shareholders yesterday approved Westpac Banking Corp's $16 billion-plus merger proposal, the bank's shares were down 54 cents, or 2.25 per cent, to $23.46.  Fertiliser maker Incitec Pivot has raised $819 million through a share sale to its institutional investors. Incitec said that it was seeking to raise a further $351 million from retail investors, for a total $1.17 billion.  Shares in the company were trading at $3.03, down 70.84 cents, or 18.95 per cent.  At 1218 AEDT, spot gold in Sydney was trading at $US731.70 an ounce, up $US16.95 on yesterday's local close of $US714.75.  Gold miners all gained, with Newcrest Mining rising 94 cents, or 4.71 per cent, to $20.90, Newmont Mining up 44 cents, or 12.22 per cent at $4.04 and Lihir Gold was up 15.5 cents, or 8.73 per cent, to $1.93.  In other company news, Western Australian ship builder Austal Ltd has been awarded a contract to build high speed transport vessels for the US military, worth up to $US1.6 billion ($A2.4 billion).  At 1226 AEDT, shares in the company were up 39 cents, or 24.07 per cent, to $2.01.  Mirvac Group says it is starting to see early signs of growth in demand in the residential property market. At 1229 AEDT, shares in the property developer had gained five cents, or 3.33 per cent, to $1.55.  Among the retailers, Harvey Norman shares had risen three cents, or 1.24 per cent to $2.44, Woolworths had gained 89 cents, or 3.22 per cent, to $28.55, David Jones was up ten cents, or 3.29 per cent, to $3.14, and Coles owner Wesfarmers had gained 24 cents, or 1.16 per cent, to $18.30.  At 1231 AEDT, the most traded stock was satellite and broadband services provider Newsat, with 30.05 million units traded for a total of $59,884.  Shares in the company were steady at 0.1 cent.  Market turnover was 519.7 million shares, valued at $1.7 billion, with 499 stocks up, 282 down and 246 unchanged.
