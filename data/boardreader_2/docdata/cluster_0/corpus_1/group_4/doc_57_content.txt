The benchmark Australian iron-ore price for the year starting April 1 may drop 50% to US$46/t from US$93/t this year as demand from China slows, according to ANZ bank. The group had forecast a decline of 20%. Goldman Sachs JBWere, UBS AG and Macquarie Group have lowered forecasts for iron-ore prices since October, while Merrill Lynch &amp;amp; Co and Credit Suisse Group AG expect prices to remain at a record.
