The Australian share market has opened lower despite Wall Street managing modest gains for a third straight session.  At 8.15am, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index was down 25.4 points, or 0.7 per cent, at 3598, while the broader All Ordinaries fell 24.1 points, or 0.67 per cent, to 3551.3.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the December share price index contract was up 26 points at 3645 on volume of 10,952 contracts. BHP Billiton has abandoned its multi-billion-dollar hostile tilt for rival Rio Tinto due to the turmoil in global financial and commodity markets and Rio's high debt.  BHP Billiton and Rio Tinto shares were in adjustment, with BHP last closing at $26.22 and Rio at $63.90.  Among the banks, Commonwealth Bank fell $1.01, or 2.97 per cent, to $32.99, National Australia Bank dropped 19 cents to $19.68, ANZ lost 43 cents to $14.12 and Westpac declined 38 cents to $17.45.  Wall Street showed some signs of stability on Tuesday as investors, heartened by US government plans to aid consumer lending companies, selectively bought more stocks following a huge two-day rally.  The Dow Jones closed up 36.08 points, or 0.43 per cent, to 8,479.47, while the Standard &amp;amp; Poors 500 index added 5.58 points, or 0.66 per cent, to 857.39.  The Nasdaq lost 7.29 points, or 0.5 per cent, to 1464.73.
