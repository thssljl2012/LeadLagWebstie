The Australian share market has opened slightly higher, led by the banks, after US equities gained overnight.   Westfield Group and Boral slumped on profit warnings, however, while energy stocks fell as the oil price declined.   At 1027 AEDT, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was up 8.1 points or 0.24 per cent at 3,452.1, while the broader All Ordinaries index rose 4.7 points or 0.14 per cent to 3,397.   On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index contract was 46 points higher at 3,422 on a volume of 8,356 contracts.   Among the major banks, Commonwealth Bank of Australia was 72 cents, or 2.91 per cent higher, at $25.46 and National Australia Bank rose 45 cents to $17.76.   ANZ added 25 cents, or 2.02 per cent, to $12.65 and Westpac gained 34 cents, or 2.29 per cent, to $15.16.   Some of Wall Street's earnings anxiety eased after companies including US Steel Corp and American Express posted profits amid the recession. The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 index added 9.14 points, or 1.09 per cent, to 845.71.
