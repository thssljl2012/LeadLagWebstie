NEW YORK, Jan 15 (Reuters) - U.S. stocks briefly pared losses on Thursday after data showed a measure of U.S. regional factory activity contracted in January, but less severely than expected.   * The boost was short-lived as indices dropped further on concerns about funding for Bank of America * Bank of America, down 23 percent, is the top drag on the Dow.   * Benchmark indexes are all down more than 1.8 percent.
