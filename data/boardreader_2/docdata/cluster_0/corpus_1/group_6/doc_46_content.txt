The Australian share market opened about one per cent lower after US equities declined on further evidence of the economic slowdown.  At 8.13am, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was down 38.7 points, or 1.1 per cent, at 3487.5, while the broader All Ordinaries index fell 40.1 points, or 1.16 per cent, to 3421.2.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index contract was 75 points lower at 3441 on a volume of 6386 contracts.  In a broad market decline, the banks fell.  Commonwealth Bank of Australia lost 65 cents, or 2.42 per cent, to $26.25, National Australia Bank declined 17 cents to $18.51, ANZ slipped 28 cents, or 2.11 per cent, to $12.99 and Westpac retreated 38 cents, or 2.42 per cent, to $15.32.  Mining giant Rio Tinto slumped $1.60, or 3.93 per cent, to $39.11 and its rival BHP Billiton fell 55 cents, or 1.79 per cent, to $30.10.  Other major companies on the exchange also fell, with Telstra losing four cents, or 1.06 per cent, to $3.74, Woolworths declining 22 cents to $26.98, Westfield slipping 17 cents, or 1.39 per cent, to $12.08 and Woodside Petroleum retreating 18 cents, to $34.41.  Caution returned to Wall Street on Thursday as weak earnings reports and record unemployment claims offered the latest evidence of the economy's struggles.  The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 2.7 per cent to 8,149.01, the Standard &amp; Poor's 500 index sank 3.31 per cent to 845.14 and the Nasdaq composite index dropped 3.24 per cent to 1507.84.
