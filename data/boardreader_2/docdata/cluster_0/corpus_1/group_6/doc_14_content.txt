The Australian share market was slightly higher at 10am with mild gains in resources sector and despite a flat lead from Wall Street.  At 10am, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index was up 19.1 points, or 0.52 per cent, at 3713.4, and the broader All Ordinaries index rose 16.2 points, or 0.44 per cent, to 3659.8.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index contract was five points lower at 3,687 on a volume of 7286 deals.  The US closed relatively flat and we had no surprises so that gave our markets a little confidence but I'm not sure if we're going to maintain it, CMC Markets senior dealer Dominic Vaughan said.  The market is firmer at this stage but I expect a bit of profit taking later in the day.  We're still trading thin and they're so real money into the market yet.  BHP Billiton, the country's largest company, rose 55 cents, or 1.79 per cent, to $31.30 while Rio Tinto increased six cents, or 0.14 per cent, to $44.06.  In the banking sector, Westpac fell eight cents to $16.49, National Australia Bank decreased three cents to $20.83, ANZ sank six cents to $15.16 but Commonwealth Bank added three cents to $28.59.  Wall Street shares shook off opening losses and ended mixed Thursday as investors looked past weak sales reports from the retail sector and other grim data.  At settlement, the Dow Jones dropped 27.24 points, or 0.31 per cent, to 8742.46.  The Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index rose 3.08 points, or 0.34 per cent, to 909.73 points, while the Nasdaq was 17.95, or 1.12 per cent higher, at 1617.01.
