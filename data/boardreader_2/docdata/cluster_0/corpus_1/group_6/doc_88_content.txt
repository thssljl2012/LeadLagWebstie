NEW YORK (Reuters) - Stocks plunged on Thursday, sending the Dow briefly below the 8,000 level for the first time since late November, as investors worried about mounting credit losses for banks.  * A plea for cash by Bank of America (NYSE:BAC - News) and fears about Citigroup's (NYSE:C - News) fate fueled concerns that fallout from the credit crisis is worsening.   * The S&amp;P 500 (^SPX - News) slid 3 percent, while the Dow (DJI:^DJI - News) and the Nasdaq (Nasdaq:^IXIC - News) both declined more than 2 percent.  * Citigroup shares were down more than 18 percent at $3.69, while shares of Bank of America, the largest U.S. bank, tumbled nearly 21 percent to $8.06.  (Reporting by Ellis Mnyandu; Editing by James Dalgleish)
