 TOP MARKET NEWS  Featured Broker  (RTTNews) - The Australian stock market was trading sharply lower on Tuesday, in the absence of a lead from the holiday-shuttered U.S. market, dragged by financial and resource stocks. The local markets followed a negative lead from the European markets and the fall in oil prices. At 8:06 p.m. ET, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index was losing 126 points or 3.52% to 3,463, after closing a percent higher on Monday. The broader All Ordinaries index was losing 117 points or 3.31% to 3,414.  In the currency market, the Australian dollar opened lower. The Aussie opened at US$0.6714-0.6719, down from Monday's close of US$0.6812-0.6814.  On Wall Street, U.S. markets were closed on Monday in observance of the Martin Luther King Jr. day holiday. When U.S. stocks last traded on Friday, markets closed with modest gains. Tuesday, Barack Obama will officially be sworn in as the 44th president of the United States.  On the economic front, the markets have very little economic reports to digest in today's trading.  Among banking stocks, Commonwealth Bank of Australia was down 4.37%, National Australia Bank lost 4.17%, and ANZ Banking Group dropped 4.78%. Westpac slipped 5.22%, and investment bank Macquarie Group fell 3.48%.  In the resources sector, index leader BHP Billiton fell 4.44% and Rio Tinto dropped 5.57%. Gold miners were weaker, as Sino Gold dropped 5.49%, Lihir Gold fell 3.85%, and Newcrest Mining lost 2.72%.  On Monday, crude oil futures for February delivery was trading lower in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange, giving back its gains from the previous session. Trading was light with U.S. stock markets closed for Martin Luther King Jr. Day. Floor trading was closed for the public holiday. Oil closed down $1.11 at $36.51 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange on Friday, after hitting an intraday low of $34.18 and a high of $36.87. In the Asian session Tuesday, crude was down $1.86 at $34.65 in electronic trading at 7:08 p.m. ET.  Among energy stocks, Oil Search dropped 5.10%, Santos lost 4.34%, and Woodside fell 3.47%.  In the retail sector, David Jones fell 1.86%, Coles' owner Wesfarmers dropped 4.19%, and Woolworths lost 2.44%.  by RTT Staff Writer  For comments and feedback: contact editorial@rttnews.com
