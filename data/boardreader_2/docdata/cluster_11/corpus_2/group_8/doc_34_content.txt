 FNM Message Board Posts          Recs: 1              BEFORE THE BELL: US Stock Futures Down                   U.S. stock futures failed to extend gains Thursday following a rare up day,  with disappointment over a lack of new Chinese spending  and a warning from General Motors Corp. (GM) on its ability to survive dampening sentiment.           S&amp;P 500 futures slipped 14.9 points to 693.50 and Nasdaq 100 futures dipped 10 points to 1091. Dow Jones Industrial Average futures slipped 129 points to 6701.           U.S. stocks closed higher Wednesday after five sessions of losses, with hopes for China spending driving the broad-based gains, save for the financial sector. The Dow industrials climbed 149 points, the S&amp;P 500 rose 16 points and the Nasdaq Composite added 32 points.            Chinese Premier Wen Jiabao on Thursday deflated spending hopes, however, as he largely reiterated a $585 billion stimulus package.            While financial markets had anticipated additional fiscal stimulus measures, in one form or another, the Premiers report today did not deliver such surprises, except for reiterating the supportive macro/industry-specific measures that have already been announced, said economists at J.P. Morgan Chase.           Though the Shanghai Composite built on Wednesdays gains, the Hang Seng slipped 1%.            The U.K. FTSE 100 and the German DAX 30 nursed losses of more than 2%.             The Bank of England as expected cut rates to a new record low of 0.5%, and also announced plans to buy 75 billion pounds of securities as it engages in quantitative easing. The European Central Bank also cut rates, to a record euro-zone low of 1.5%.            Turning stateside,  productivity dropped 0.4%  in the fourth quarter while weekly  jobless claims fell by 31,000 . Markets also will be positioning ahead of Fridays monthly payrolls report.           Retailers reported same-store sales:  among 11 retailers that have reported their results, about two-thirds of them exceeded Wall Street expectations , according to Thomson Reuters.  Still, retailers are expected to report a 1.2% decline , with department stores and specialty retailers setting that pace, Thomson Reuters data showed. Analysts cautioned that better results were also driven at the expense of profit margins.           General Motors fell 17% in pre-open trade after expressing doubts over its ability to survive as a going concern.           Shares of J.P. Morgan Chase &amp; Co. (JPM), Bank of America Corp. (BAC) and Wells Fargo &amp; Co. (WFC) each slumped in pre-market trade after negative comments from Moodys Investors Service, which gave notice that it may downgrade the latter two lenders debt ratings and put J.P. Morgans outlook as negative.            The dollar rose vs. rivals, notably the euro and the British pound, and crude oil futures fell by over $1 a barrel.             Gold futures rose over $8 an ounce .
