Stocks on the move Real-Time Equity news U.S. stock market report 0822 ET 05March2009-Wal-Mart up after February same-store sales ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Discount retailer Wal-Mart Stores on Thursday reported February same-store sales that rose 5.1 percent, more than expected, saying that lower gas prices meant that shoppers had more money to spend in its stores. Analysts were expecting same-store sales growth of 2.4 percent. Shares of the company, a Dow component, rose 2.8 percent to $49.85 in premarket trading. Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 0816 ET 05March2009-Cuomo issues subpoenas to 7 Merrill executives ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Andrew Cuomo, the attorney general of New York, late Wednesday issued subpoenas to seven executives who received tens of millions of dollars in 2008 pay from Merrill Lynch before it was acquired by Bank of America, a person familiar with the investigation said. Cuomo is seeking information about the bonuses, which were paid out as the company's net loss grew to $27.6 billion last year. Recently, Cuomo's lawyers have taken testimony under oath from John Thain, the former head of Merrill Lynch, and Kenneth Lewis, the CEO of Bank of America. Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 0810 ET 05March2009-Merrill: environment good for U.S. dollar ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Merrill Lynch on Thursday said that 'in general, the macroeconomic and financial landscape continues to evolve in a U.S. dollar-positive manner.' The firm noted that growth expectations were weakening while policy reponses to the recession 'are falling short of what is required to stabilize markets.'
