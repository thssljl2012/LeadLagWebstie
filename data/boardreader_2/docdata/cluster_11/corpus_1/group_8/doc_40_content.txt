0740 ET 03March2009-Crude oil rises after Monday's steep decline ------------------------------------------------------------------------------  April crude futures rose on Tuesday, following a steep decline of 10 percent that came in Monday's session. The contract showed no sign of returning to last week's bull run, however, as investors continue to fret over the global economy and how it might way on demand, overshadowing any posibive benefit from OPEC's strong compliance with supply curbs.  The contract rose 2.1 percent to $41.01 a barrel.  Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 0730 ET 03March2009-Citi looks to start JVs with 3rd parties: NY Post ------------------------------------------------------------------------------  Citigroup is looking to create joint ventures with third parties as a way of getting rid of businesses it doesn't want, the New York Post reported on Tuesday, citing high-level insiders at the bank.  The bank is looking to strike partnerships with non-core assets like CitiFinancial, CitiMortgage, Primerica, and its private-label credit card business, the paper reported.  Such partnerships could be similar to what Citigroup arranged when it combined its Smith Barney unit with Morgan Stanley's wealth management unit, giving Morgan Stanley 51 percent control of the venture, the New York Post wrote.  Shares of Citigroup, a Dow component, rose 12 cents to $1.32 in premarket trade on Tuesday.  Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 0725 ET 03March2009-JPMorgan made profits with OTC derivatives: BBG ------------------------------------------------------------------------------  JPMorgan Chase generated $5 billion in profits during a turbulent year for Wall Street and financial stocks by trading over-the-counter fixed-income derivatives, according to a Bloomberg report, which cited two people with knowledge of the results.  As of Sept. 30, Bloomberg wrote, JPMorgan held $87.7 trillion worth of outstanding OTC contracts, more than the next two banks-- Bank of America and Citigroup-- combined.  JPMorgan hasn't disclosed earnings for its interest-rate swap, municipal bond and foreign exchange derivatives group, the agency reported, adding that the division was among JPMorgan's most profitable.  Shares of JPMorgan rose 2.6 percent to $21.70 in premarket trade.  Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 0719 ET 03March2009-K+S may make bid for Compass Minerals: source ------------------------------------------------------------------------------  German fertilizer company K+S AG is considering a bid for Compass Minerals International, said a source familiar with the matter.  According to the Daily Telegraph, K+S was planning a cash bid of $2.4 billion for the company, and plans an initial offer of $78 a share. Based on Compass's Monday closing price of $56.02, $78 a share would represent a premium of 39.2 percent.  Shares of Compass gained 8.9 percent to $61 in premarket trade.
