March 5 (Reuters) - PrivateBancorp Inc, a Chicago banking company, said it will slash its quarterly dividend by 87 percent for the first quarter, in a bid to enhance capital and liquidity.   The company said it will pay a dividend of 1 cent a share on March 31 to stockholders of record on March 17.   The company currently pays a quarterly dividend of 7.5 cents a share.   The company provides personalized financial services primarily to entrepreneurial and middle-market companies, affluent individuals, wealthy families, professionals, entrepreneurs and real estate investors.   Dozens of U.S. lenders, including Bank of America Corp , JPMorgan Chase&amp;Co, Citigroup Inc and U.S. Bancorp, have reduced or eliminated dividends to preserve capital for covering rising credit losses.   Shares of PrivateBancorp fell 4 percent to $10.33 in trading before the bell. They closed at $10.73 on Wednesday on Nasdaq.
