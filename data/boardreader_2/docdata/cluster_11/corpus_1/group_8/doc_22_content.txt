Led by the high-profile meltdown of American International Group, multi-line insurance companies are struggling through the financial crisis. Over the past year, their stocks have tumbled 87 percent, compared with a 46 percent decline by the Standard &amp; Poor's 500-stock index. AIG is down 99 percent during this period, while Genworth Financial has sunk 94 percent. Hartford Financial Services Group has lost 92 percent, and Unitrin is off 74 percent. The best-performing in the group, HCC Insurance, has slipped 2 percent.
