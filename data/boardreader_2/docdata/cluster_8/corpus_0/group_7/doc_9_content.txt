You may not want to learn how much smaller your bank account has gotten. But banks are making it easier than ever for consumers to access account information on their mobile devices. Big banks are offering new services or improving existing ones that allow people to access their accounts while on the go. In January, Bank of America Corp. launched an updated software application that allows consumers to check their balances and pay bills through Apple Inc.'s iPhone. Wells Fargo &amp; Co. has begun promoting a service that lets business clients approve wire transfers through their cellphones. And mobile banking is at the center of a major new ad campaign from Chase, a division of J.P. Morgan Chase &amp; Co, which is offering a service that lets customers check their balances and get other information via text messages.  ...
