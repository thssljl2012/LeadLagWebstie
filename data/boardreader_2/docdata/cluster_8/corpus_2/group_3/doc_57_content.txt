It is equal parts the bank and the person. When you sign on those lines you agree to all terms of the contract including the consequences of failure on your part.  This root cause wasn&amp;#039;t the loans nor the people. It was companies like AIG selling credit default swaps. They were selling insurance for these risky loans but got around the insurance regulations by calling the policies swaps. Because of this they were not required to have enough cash to cover the policy if it was collected. When people started defaulting on loans because of droping house prices the house of cards fell.
