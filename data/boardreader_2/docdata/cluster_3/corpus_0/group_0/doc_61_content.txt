Bank of America Corp. completed its purchase of Countrywide Financial Corp. on Tuesday, making the bank the nation's leading mortgage originator and servicer.The Charlotte bank is picking up a mortgage giant that became the symbol of the nation's subprime mess. Countrywide faces mounting loan losses and an avalanche of lawsuits alleging dicey lending practices, including a complaint filed Tuesday by the state of Florida. Florida's attorney general alleged Tuesday that Countrywide engaged in deceptive practices that left borrowers in the state with mortgages they couldn't afford.The Federal Reserve and Countrywide's shareholders cleared the way for the acquisition last month. As of Tuesday's close, Bank of America controls between 20 percent and 25 percent of the country's home loan market.
