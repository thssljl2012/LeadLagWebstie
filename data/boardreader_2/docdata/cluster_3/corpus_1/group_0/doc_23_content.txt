 The ANZ bank says its 2008 cash earnings per share are likely to be between 20 and 25 per cent lower than last year, because of higher credit costs.The bank says its provisions in the second half are likely to be about $1.2 billion because of the ongoing deterioration in global credit markets.The news comes after NAB announced on Friday its own vulnerability to the credit crunch, with provisions for a possible $1 billion loss on investments.
