 TEL AVIV, July 21 (Reuters) - Israeli holding company Koor Industries (KOR.TA: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz) said on Monday it will raise its potential investment in Credit Suisse (CSGN.VX: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz) to a maximum 3 billion shekels ($886 million) from a previous target of 1.5 billion shekels.Last week, Koor said it had accumulated 8.97 million shares -- or 0.89 percent -- in Credit Suisse for 1.28 billion shekels. 'The investment will be carried out by taking advantage of opportunities to buy and sell in accordance with the market's situation,' Koor said in a statement to the Tel Aviv Stock Exchange, adding there was no certainty it would buy more shares.Koor said its board decided in principle to sell shares via a rights offering to shareholders of up to 680 million shekels.In April, Koor said it would invest up to 1.5 billion shekels in Credit Suisse and Barclays Bank (BARC.L: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz). Koor bought some shares in Barclays, but not in significant amounts.Goldman Sachs (GS.N: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz) has approved a credit framework to enable Koor to make the purchases.Koor is 49.5 percent held by Discount Investment Corp (DISI.TA: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz), a subsidiary of conglomerate IDB Development (IDBD.TA: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz). IDB also holds another 10 percent of Koor. (Reporting by Tova Cohen, Editing by Steven Scheer and Quentin Bryar)
