Frank McGarahan, chief operating officer of Barclays Wealth, has died from injuries received after intervening to stop an assault, according to various press reports in UK press.A report in The Times cited a police statement, which said: 'Mr McGarahan was spending a quiet evening out with family and friends ahead of a family christening in Norwich on Sunday when he witnessed an assault while queueing for a taxi ... after which he sustained a fatal injury.' Reports said McGarahan suffered head injuries when he intervened to try to stop the attack.The police have opened a murder investigation after the death. The Times report also cited a spokeswoman for Barclays, who said: As it is a matter for police investigation, I am sure you will appreciate that we cannot say very much. We can confirm that he was the deceased and our thoughts are with his wife and family. They have asked for privacy.
