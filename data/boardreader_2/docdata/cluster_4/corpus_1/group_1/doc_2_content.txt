 West Ham manager Alan Curbishley claims his club have accepted a bid from Sunderland for Anton Ferdinand.The 23-year-old defender is reportedly considering the offer, thought to involve a fee of approaching 8million.Curbishley, whose team lost 3-0 in the Barclays Premier League at Manchester City on Sunday, told BBC Radio 5 Live: 'The board have accepted the bid - he's not renegotiated his contract - and the decision was taken out of my hands.'He added: 'I don't know where Anton is in terms of signing for Sunderland - but once the club accept the bid it moves on.'Ferdinand's current contract is set to end in 2010.Curbishley, meanwhile, is hoping to sign a defender before the transfer window closes on September 1.'We are thin on the ground,' he said.'If we lose another defender we will have to react to that.'Copyright PA Sport 2008, All Rights Reserved
