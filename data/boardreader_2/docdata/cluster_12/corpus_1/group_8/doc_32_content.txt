The ANZ Bank and Merrill Lynch have offered $A253 million to unsecured creditors of Opes Prime. The payout is conditional on creditors agreeing to end all litigation over the stockbroker's collapse. It is anticipated that creditors will receive approximately $A0.37 in the dollar after wind-up costs are paid, including the fees of liquidator, Ferrier Hodgson, and law firm, Mallesons Stephen Jaques.
