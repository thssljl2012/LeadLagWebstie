MELBOURNE -(Dow Jones)- Australia &amp; New Zealand Banking Group Ltd. (ANZ) said Wednesday that Deputy Chief Executive   Bob Edgar  will retire April 30 after 25 years with the lender.  Following Edgar's decision, the Melbourne-based company named   Graham Hodges  as the lender's new deputy chief, who is responsible for driving its operational performance and managing key client and other external relationships.  Hodges has been chief of the bank's New Zealand unit since late 2005 and was formerly managing director of ANZ's corporate unit, as well as having previously served as the bank's chief economist, ANZ said.
