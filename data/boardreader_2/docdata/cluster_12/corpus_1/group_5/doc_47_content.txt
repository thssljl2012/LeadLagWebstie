SYDNEY, Dec 12 (Reuters) - Australia's top four banks have   been forced to raise large amounts of capital in equity and debt   markets worldwide as the worst global financial crisis in over 80   years puts pressure on their balance sheets.   National Australia Bank (NAB), Westpac Banking Corp , Commonwealth Bank (CBA) and Australia and New   Zealand Banking Group have raised a combined US$10.6   billion of capital between Oct. 9 and Dec. 11.
