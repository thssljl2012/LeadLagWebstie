 DUBLIN Jan 23 (Reuters) - The Irish government said on Friday it had asked for European Union aid for the region where Dell (DELL.O  Dell, which ranked itself as Ireland's largest exporter and second-largest company, said this month it would move production to take advantage of lower costs in Poland, where the minimum wage is about 80 percent lower than in Ireland. 'The key task of the European Globalisation Fund is to assist in addressing economic dislocation and its impact on local, regional and national economies,' Minister for Labour Affairs Billy Kelleher said after meeting EU Employment Commissioner Vladimir Spidla in the Czech Republic. 'The scale of redundancies in Dell, and more widely in the Mid-West region, has brought such economic dislocation to that area, and particularly to Limerick,' Kelleher said, adding that he had received an encouraging response from Spidla.  Dell's plant is based in the traditionally impoverished Irish county of Limerick.  After a decade-long 'Celtic Tiger' boom, Ireland became the first of the euro zone members to enter recession last year, with the downturn gathering pace rapidly.  With finances dwindling, the Irish government is holding talks with unions and employers on Friday to find a way of cutting 2 billion euros ($2.6 billion) in public spending.
