 * Q4 loss $5.91/shr  * Cuts 200 positions  * Slashes dividend to 1 cent a share  Jan 23 (Reuters) - Webster Financial Corp (WBS.N  The largest bank in New England posted a loss of $300.5 million, or $5.91 a share, compared with a loss of $8.7 million, or 16 cents a share, a year earlier.  Analysts on average were looking for a loss of $2.24 a share, excluding items, according to Reuters Estimates.  Pretax operating loss was $35.9 million or 83 cents a share.  The company took a non-cash goodwill impairment charge of $188.9 million related to its commercial banking, consumer finance and other business segments during the quarter.  It also recorded another non-cash impairment charge of $129.6 million on certain investment securities.  Net interest income rose slightly to $126.1 million, the parent of Webster Bank said.  Net interest margin, the difference between what the bank earns on loans and pays on deposits, fell 6 basis points to 3.20 percent for the fourth quarter.  The company cut its quarterly dividend to 1 cent a share from 30 cents a share.  The company has 3213 employees, according to Reuters data.  Webster said last week its quarterly credit loss provision will more than double sequentially to $100 million, and expects to record a non-cash charge of $118 million against its trust preferred investments in the fourth quarter.  The company's shares, which have fallen as much as 80 percent in the last 12 months, closed at $5.33 Thursday on the New York Stock Exchange.
