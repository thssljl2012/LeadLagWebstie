The issue would be jointly led by Commonwealth Bank of Australia and UBS. Investec and the lead banks declined to comment. Australian banks have this year raised more than $A37 billion equivalent of funds under the government guarantee. Most of the bonds were sold in US and Australian dollars and are all rated triple A by S&amp;P and Moody's. Spreads of government-guaranteed bond have tightened significantly in the last weeks as investors became more familiar with the asset class, a banker said. Bonds issued by Australia's major banks only pay around 25 basis points more than similarly rated bonds issued by semi-government borrowers such as Australian state treasury departments, down from a gap of around 100 basis points in December. Investec sold earlier in the month $A400 million of three year bonds backed by the government at 125 basis points over swap and BBSW.
