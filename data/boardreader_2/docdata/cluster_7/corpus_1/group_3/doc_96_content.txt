WELLINGTON, Oct 24 (Reuters) - Following are some of the lead stories from New Zealand metropolitan newspapers on Friday. Stories may be taken from either the paper or Internet editions of the papers. Reuters has not verified these stories and does not vouch for their accuracy. DOMINION POST(www.stuff.co.nz) Poll puts Maori in box seat The ground has shifted under National 16 days out from the election, as a new poll suggests the Maori Party will hold the balance of power. Giant rate cut to ease the pain The economy will feel the pain of a global recession for the next year as unemployment rises and house prices continue falling, economists say, despite the biggest interest rate cut on record and more to come. ANZ holds steady in turmoil ANZ National Bank has reported a profit of $1.16 billion after tax for the year to September, down only slightly from last year, underlining the strength of the New Zealand banking sector. NEW ZEALAND HERALD (www.nzherald.co.nz) Gap closes, but National stays on top With two weeks to go, Labour has improved its polling a little, but not enough to dent National's commanding election race lead, the latest Herald-DigiPoll survey shows. Banks ease mortgage rates Amid the economic gloom there's good news for homeowners as most major banks cut interest rates after the Reserve Bank's lowering of the official cash rate.
