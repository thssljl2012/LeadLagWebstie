Sister banks the ANZ and National Bank have cut rates today on some fixed rate home loans ahead of the Reserve Bank's March 12 review of official interest rates.  Both ANZ and National Bank's six-month fixed rate is now 5.79 percent, down from 5.99 percent.  Their three-year rates have been cut by 0.36 percent to 5.99 percent while their four-year rate is now 6.40 percent, down from 6.50 percent.  The Reserve Bank will consider changing the Official Cash Rate at its next monetary policy meeting on March 12. The OCR is currently at 3.5 percent and economists expect the rate to be cut by between 75 and 100 basis points.
