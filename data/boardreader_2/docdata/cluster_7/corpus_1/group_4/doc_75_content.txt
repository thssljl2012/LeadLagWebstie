American Tower Corp. (AMT   Quote   Chart   News   PowerRating), an operator of communication tower sites, reported a 1.4% increase in profit for the third quarter, driven by double-digit growth in top line that was nearly offset by a tax provision compared to a tax benefit last year. Further, the company tightened its fiscal 2008 forecast for rental and management segment revenue, and also issued fiscal 2009 view.  Among peers, SBA Communications Corp. (SBAC   Quote   Chart   News   PowerRating) in late October reported a narrower third-quarter net loss of $16.42 million on a 15% increase in revenues.  Boston, Massachusetts-based American Tower's third-quarter net income increased to $60.47 million or $0.15 per share from $59.63 million or $0.14 per share last year. The latest quarter results included loss from discontinued operations of $50 thousand, lower than prior year's $511 thousand.  On a continuing operations basis, quarterly income edged up to $60.52 million or $0.15 per share from $60.14 million or $0.14 per share a year ago. On average, 16 analysts polled by First Call/Thomson Financial expected the company to earn $0.14 per share for the quarter.  Total revenue in the third quarter increased 11.3% to $409.27 million from $367.59 million in the previous year, and beat analysts' consensus estimate of $401.58 million.  In the preceding quarter, American Tower had recorded net income of $158.79 million or $0.38 per share, income from continuing operations of $50.68 million or $0.12 per share, and revenues of $393.73 million.  On a divisional basis, rental and management revenues in the third quarter rose 10% to $394.40 million from $358.62 million a year earlier. The revenue growth included about 1.1% rise from favorable foreign currency fluctuations and a negative impact to growth of about 1.5% from straight-line revenue recognition. In the quarter, network development services revenue climbed to $14.87 million from $8.96 million a year ago.  Operating income was $154.37 million in the quarter, higher than $98.49 million a year ago. Adjusted earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortization, or EBITDA, for the quarter increased 11.6% to $277.53 million from $248.63 million a year ago. During the quarter, the company recorded an income tax provision of $34.92 million, compared to last year's tax benefit of $14.48 million.  Commenting on the results, Jim Taiclet, American Tower's chief executive officer stated, 'Our company's strong growth in tower revenue and adjusted EBITDA, as demonstrated by our third quarter results, expectations for the remainder of 2008 and outlook for 2009, reflects our continued confidence in the stability and growth prospects of our business.'  American Tower's net income for the nine-month period surged to $261.41 million or $0.62 per share from $61.87 million or $0.15 per share a year earlier. Income from continuing operations grew to $153.37 million or $0.36 per share from $93.25 million or $0.22 per share in the prior year. Total operating revenues for the nine months increased to $1.19 billion from $1.08 billion last year, and rental and management revenues grew to $1.15 billion from prior year's $1.06 billion.  Going forward, American Tower tightened its fiscal 2008 outlook for rental and management segment revenue to a range of $1.545 billion to $1.550 billion from previous forecast of $1.538 billion to $1.552 billion. The revised outlook represents a mid point growth of 8.5% from last year.  Adjusted EBITDA is now estimated to be in a range of $1.086 billion to $1.093 billion, compared to $1.078 billion to $1.097 billion estimated earlier.  Excluding the impacts of foreign currency fluctuations and straight-line revenue and expense recognition, full-year tower revenue and adjusted EBITDA are now anticipated to increase approximately 10% and 14%, respectively.  For the year 2008, the company expects rental and management segment gross margin between $1.195 billion and $1.201 billion.  Further, for the fiscal year 2009, American Tower projects rental and management segment revenue between $1.640 billion and $1.665 billion, of which the mid point represents a growth of 6.8% from last year. Rental and management segment gross margin is estimated in a range of $1.277 billion to $1.297 billion, and adjusted EBITDA is projected between $1.161 billion and $1.185 billion.  The outlook for 2009 represents an increase in tower revenue and adjusted EBITDA of approximately 10% and 11%, respectively that excludes the impacts of foreign currency fluctuations, straight-line revenue and expense recognition and any potential benefits from a widespread WiMAX network build out. American Tower noted that it anticipates strong core growth from operations in 2009, based on the company's customers' continued investment in their wireless networks.  According to the company, the fiscal 2009 forecast assumes that the Mexican Peso and Brazilian Real will strengthen against the US Dollar during the year. Under a 10% change in the average exchange rates from these assumptions, the company estimates that fiscal 2009 rental and management segment revenue and adjusted EBITDA would vary either positively or negatively by about $17 million and $9 million, respectively from the given outlook.  On October 31, brokerage Credit Suisse upgraded its rating on American Tower to 'Outperform' from 'Neutral'.  In an October 20 research note, Barclays Capital trimmed its fiscal 2009 earnings estimate for American Tower to $0.64 per share from $0.69. In early October, the brokerage had reiterated its rating on the company at 'Overweight', while cut its price target to $49 from $51.  AMT closed Friday's regular trading session at $32.31, up $2.29, on a volume of 8 million shares. In the past 52 weeks, shares have been trading in a range of $22.34 -$46.20.
