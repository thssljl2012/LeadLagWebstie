 BRUSSELS, Aug 30 (Reuters) - A consortium of Belgium's Dexia (News/Aktienkurs) and  (News) have pulled out of the bidding for a joint private-public sector project to invest one billion euros ($1.5 billion) in renovating 211 Flemish schools, the De Tijd newspaper reported on Saturday. It said three groups remained in the running: a consortium involving Fortis Bank and Fortis Real Estate; Cofinimmo together with a public holding group; and Barclays, Meridiam Infrastructure and NIB Capital. It added that the aim was to launch the project next year.
