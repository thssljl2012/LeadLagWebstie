SYDNEY, Sept 16 (Reuters) - Commonwealth Bank of Australia Ltd has an exposure to collapsed U.S. investment bank Lehman Brothers (News) of less than A$150 million ($123 million), it said on Tuesday. Commonwealth, the country's second largest bank, said it continues to work with relevant parties in regard to its position. National Australia Bank, the country's largest lender, said its exposure was negligible, and it would not be making an announcement. St. George Bank, Australia's fifth largest, said it had no exposure to Lehman, while Australia and New Zealand Banking Group and No.4 Westpac Banking Corp. were not immediately able to comment or says whether they would be making any announcements. Lehman filed for bankruptcy on Monday in the U.S., sending banking shares worldwide tumbling. ANZ led the fall in Australian banking shares on Tuesday with a 5.04 percent fall to A$16. NAB shares were down 3.9 percent at A$21.9 at 0056 GMT, while CBA's shares were down 2.29 percent at A$41. Westpac was down 2.38 percent at A$22.6, and St. George was down 2.5 percent at A$29.58. The financials sector sub-index was down 2.23 percent. (Reporting by Mette Fraende).ngCOPYRIGHTCopyright Thomson Financial News Limited 2008.
