 ATHENS, Greece -- A bomb exploded early Monday (March 9th) outside a Citibank branch in the Athens suburb of Psychiko. The blast caused significant damage to parked cars but no injuries. Nobody made a warning call or claimed responsibility for the attack. On February 18th, police defused a powerful time bomb placed outside the Citibank headquarters in the district of Kifissia. Police said it contained enough explosives to demolish a four-story building.  In other news, arsonists threw firebombs at two offices of the Greek Employment Organisation and a sporting-goods store in Athens on Sunday morning. The bombs caused significant property damage but no injuries. (ANA-MPA, In.news, SKAI, AFP, AP, BBC, RIA Novosti - 09/03/09; AFP, ANA-MPA - 08/03/09)
