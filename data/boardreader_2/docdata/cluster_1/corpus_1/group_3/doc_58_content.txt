AIG has said that acting chief financial officer Steven Bensinger has resigned, with David Herzog taking his place in the permanent role. According to the group, Bensinger, who was also vice-chairman of AIG's financial services group has 'left AIG to pursue other opportunities'. Herzog, 48, has served as AIG senior vice-president and comptroller since June 2005. He joined American General Corporation in February 2000 and, following the AIG acquisition of American General in 2001, was named chief operating officer for the combined domestic life insurance companies. Herzog was elected AIG vice president of the group's life insurance arm in 2003 and in 2004 and was named CFO of AIG's worldwide life insurance operations.
