AIG, the stricken insurance giant, on Wednesday  of its Asian life assurance unit  one of its most prized assets  in the hope of raising up to $20bn to help repay its $60bn US government loan. AIG sent the sales memorandum for American International Assurance with limited information to a group of selected potential bidders including China Life, the worlds largest life assurer; HSBC; Prudential, the UK insurance group; and Prudential Financial of the US. ManuLife Financial, a leading US insurer, and Allianz of Germany have also requested information. First-round bids are due near the end of next month. AIG asked potential bidders to bid for 49% of AIA, but said it would look at offers for all of the unit.
