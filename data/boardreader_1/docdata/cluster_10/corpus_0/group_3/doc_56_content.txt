 Wall Street Executives Made $3 Billion Before Crisis Merrill Lynch &amp;amp; Co. paid its chief executives the most, with Stanley O'Neal taking in $172 million from 2003 to 2007 and John Thain getting $86 million, including a signing bonus, after beginning work in December. The company agreed to be acquired by Bank of America Corp. for about $50 billion on Sept. 15. Bear Stearns Cos.'s James "Jimmy'' Cayne made $161 million before the company collapsed and was sold to JPMorgan Chase &amp;amp; Co. in June.
