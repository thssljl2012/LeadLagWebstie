 NEW YORK, April 1 (Reuters) - The Federal Reserve Bank of New York is meeting with large credit derivative dealers on Wednesday to discuss procedures to centrally clear credit default swaps, said a source familiar with the meeting.  Central clearing of trades in the $27 trillion credit default swaps market is viewed as key to removing the systemic risks posed by the failure of a large counterparty.  Credit default swaps are used to insure against a borrower defaulting on their debt or speculate on their credit quality. A spokesperson for the Federal Reserve Bank declined to comment on the meeting.
