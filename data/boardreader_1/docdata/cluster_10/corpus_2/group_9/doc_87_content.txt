The Bogus Bank Recovery       http://www.newsweek.com/id/195099       If you take the headlines at face value, it has been a good month for banks. Wells Fargo announced $3 billion in first-quarter profits, Goldman Sachs racked up $1.8 billion, JPMorgan Chase had $2.1 billion, Bank of America $4.25 billion and even beleaguered Citigroup tallied $1.6 billion in profits. Treasury Secretary Tim Geithner validated the good news by declaring that the "vast majority" of the nation's banks are now well capitalized and solvent. Markets rallied. The worst of the financial crisis, it seemed, had passed.       Smart investors know better. At the core, this financial crisis has been driven by uncertainty
