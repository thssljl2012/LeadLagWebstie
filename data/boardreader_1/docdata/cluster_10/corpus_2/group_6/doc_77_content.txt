               Government takes on risky Merrill exposures Firm still has at least $75 bln of exposure to CDOs, MBS, leveraged loans   Excerpt: SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) - Despite reporting a $15 billion fourth-quarter net loss on Friday, Merrill Lynch is still exposed to at least $75 billion of collateralized debt obligations, mortgage-backed securities, leveraged loans and other potentially risky assets. The government is now responsible for a lot of this. The government also agreed to cover a big chunk of future losses on $118 billion of exposures, mainly from Merrill. Under the agreement, Bank of America will cover the first $10 billion in losses and the government is responsible for 90% of any further losses.
