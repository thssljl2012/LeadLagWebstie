NEW YORK, Feb 25 (Reuters) - U.S. stocks rebounded in late trade on Wednesday after financial shares reversed losses as investors bet banks will be able to withstand new federal government stress-tests on their capital levels with relative ease.  Bank of America (BAC.N: Quote, Profile, Research) jumped nearly 14 percent to $5.39 and Citigroup (C.N: Quote, Profile, Research) jumped over 12 percent to $2.92. The S&amp;P Financial Index .GSPF gained 3.1 percent. 'I think people realize as they look at the details of this stress test that a lot of these banks are going to be able to pass it,' said Stephen Massocca, managing director at Wedbush Morgan in San Francisco  The Dow Jones industrial average .DJI gained 32.65 points, or 0.44 percent, to 7,383.59. The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 Index .SPX rose 4.75 points, or 0.61 percent, to 777.89. The Nasdaq Composite Index .IXIC added 7.98 points, or 0.55 percent, to 1,449.81.  Earlier in the session, investors sold off shares of financial services companies and big manufacturers, including Boeing (BA.N: Quote, Profile, Research) and United Technologies (UTX.N: Quote, Profile, Research).  Sales of previously owned U.S. homes plunged an unexpectedly high 5.3 percent in January, an industry group reported.
