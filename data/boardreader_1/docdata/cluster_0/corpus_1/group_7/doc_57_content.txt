 THE stock market was marginally stronger at noon following gains in the financial sector.  By 12.00pm (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was up 30 points, or 0.9 per cent, at 3357.5 while the broader All Ordinaries index had gained 26.6 points, or 0.81 per cent, to 3308.1.   CMC Markets market analyst David Taylor said investors were feeling secure about banking stocks and consumer staples. 'We've had a couple of stocks dragging on the market, but generally speaking we're up,' he said. 'The banks, Westfield, there are a few heavyweights doing well. 'Really, the only thing dragging on the market are BHP and Telstra and Macquarie, but fortunately there are enough big cap stocks up to put us in positive territory.' Financials were higher.  ANZ was up 33 cents, or 2.64 per cent, at $12.83 after saying its unaudited net profit fell 11 per cent in the first four months of fiscal 2009, to the end of January.  The bank also said cash earnings for the period, excluding provisions and credit intermediation trades, were up 18 per cent on the corresponding period of fiscal 2008.  NAB added 36 cents to $17.69, Commonwealth Bank found 27 cents to $29.40 and Westpac rose 32 cents to $16.50.  In the resources sector, market giant BHP Billiton held steady at $29.01 while rival Rio Tinto was up 29 cents to $46.47.   The energy sector was positive.   Santos was up 45 cents to $14.93, Woodside was up $1.19 to $35.36 and Oil Search gained 15 cents to $4.86.   Gold stocks were down.   Gold miner Newcrest lost 87 cents to $30.42, Newmont fell 8 cents to $6.17 and Lihir shed 7 cents to $3.28.   At 12.32pm, the spot price of gold in Sydney was $US953.40 per fine ounce, down $US8.95 on Tuesday's local close of $US962.35.   Among the retailers, Woolworths added 4 cents to $28.12, David Jones added 2 cents to $2.32 and Coles owner Wesfarmers lost 9 cents to $17.37.  Wesfarmers announced overnight that it would raise $1.7 billion through the retail component of its January capital raising.  In company news, Telstra was down 7 cents at $3.70, after it announced chief executive Sol Trujillo would leave the telco on June 30.  The group also reported a 1 per cent dip in first half profit.  The property sector was abuzz with activity, Lend Lease reported a loss of almost $600 million for the first half, and has warned of continuing market volatility for the foreseeable future.  Lend lease climbed 2.45 per cent, to $5.02.  Centro Properties reported a $2.4 billion first half loss as the shopping centre owner was hit by falling property values, slumping derivative values and losses from the movement in exchange rates.  Centro was down 0.5 cent to 7.8 cents a share.  Shopping centre developer Westfield Group has reported a net loss for fiscal 2008 of $2.1966 billion, although it says underlying earnings were strong.   The property giant's shares added 51 cents, or 5.23 per cent, to $10.26.   On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract was up 17 points to 3344 on a volume of 13,177 contracts.
