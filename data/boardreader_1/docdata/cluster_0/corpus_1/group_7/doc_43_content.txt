(New York, NY) -- It's a rough start to the trading day on Wall Street.  The Dow fell more than 250 points in the opening hour of trading, a drop of nearly three-and-a-quarter percent.  The Nasdaq was down three-and-a-half percent, and the S&amp;P 500 dropped three-and-three-quarters percent.  Bank of America shares have taken a hit of more than ten percent on the New York Stock Exchange.  JPMorgan and Wells Fargo were down about eight percent.  Energy shares are falling too.  Exxon Mobil shares dropped four percent, and crude oil prices are down about seven percent.  (Copyright 2009 by Newsroom Solutions)
