NEW YORK, Feb. 17 (UPI) -- U.S. stock indexes closed in on the bear market low Tuesday following a Presidents Day holiday with uncertainties still clouding the financial picture.  Long-struggling financial firms Bank of America, Citigroup Inc. and J.P. Morgan &amp; Chase all fell more than 8 percent. General Motors Corp. (NYSE:GM), due to file a financial viability plan in Washington, fell 14.8 percent, as rumors of impending bankruptcy persist.  In early afternoon trading, the Dow Jones industrial average was off 288.33 points, or 3.67 percent, to 7,562.08. The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 fell 4.35 percent, 35.97 points, to 790.87. The Nasdaq composite index lost 61.69 points, 4.02 percent, to 1,471.67.  The benchmark 10-year U.S. Treasury bond rose 1 31/32 to yield 2.668 percent.  The euro fell to $1.2584, compared to Friday's $1.2779. Against the Japanese yen, the dollar rose to 91.35 yen, up from Friday's 91.73 yen.  In Tokyo, the Nikkei average lost 104.66 points to 7,645.51, down 1.35 percent.  In London, the FTSE 100 index lost 100.62 points, 2.43 points, to 4,034.13.
