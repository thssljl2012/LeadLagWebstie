THE Australian stock market opened higher on Friday following a surprising comeback on Wall Street overnight.  By 1015 AEDT, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was up 8.8 points, or 0.25 per cent, at 3,523.1 while the broader All Ordinaries index added 7.1 points, or 0.21 per cent, to 3,465.6.   On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract improved 13 points to 3,484, on a volume of 5,584 contracts.   In the resources sector, mining giant Rio Tinto lost $1.52 or 2.92 per cent to $50.48.   The resources giant came out of a trading halt after the market closed on Thursday, and said on Friday it was 'very confident'' it will win federal government backing for a $19.5 billion deal with major shareholder Chinalco.   Rival miner BHP Billiton sank 38 cents to $32.09.   Among the major banks, the National Australia Bank rose 16 cents to $18.76, Commonwealth Bank fell eight cents to $30.87, Westpac found nine cents at $16.74, and the ANZ rose 44 cents to $12.33.   On Wall Street overnight, US stocks staged a stunning comeback on reports the US government plans to subsidise mortgage payments for troubled homeowners.   The Dow Jones Industrial Average had fallen nearly 250 points in earlier trading, but closed down just 6.77 points, or 0.09 per cent, to 7,932.76.   The tech-heavy Nasdaq gained 11.21 points, or 0.73 per cent, to 1,541.71 and the broad Standard &amp; Poor's 500 index was up 1.45 points, or 0.17 per cent, to 835.19.'There was an amazing turnaround in America overnight and anything like that would have a positive influence on our market,'' Austock Securities senior client adviser and strategist Michael Heffernan said. ''The only decline is in the materials sector with both Rio and BHP down, but with Rio's announcement it's probably not a bad result.''   Mr Heffernan said he expected Rio's share price to rise in the next couple of months.   Making headlines this Friday, Australia's biggest gold company, Newcrest Mining Ltd, lifted first half profit and said it was in a strong position in a weak economy.   Newcrest climbed 36 cents to $34.36, rivals Lihir Gold added three cents to $3.44 and Newmont rose two cents to $6.42.   At 1039 AEDT the spot price of gold in Sydney was $US943.70 an ounce, up $US4.55 on Thursday's local close of $US939.15.   Financial services provider AMP Ltd expects its annual underlying profit for 2008 to be around $800 million, compared to $882 million in the 2007 financial year.   Its shares were six cents higher to $5.16.  The energy sector also was mixed.   Woodside rose 21 cents to $32.36, Santos lost three cents to $14.87 and Oil Search increased six cents to $4.66.   Among the major retailers, Woolworths added 24 cents at $27.88, David Jones gained one cent to $2.31 and Coles owner Wesfarmers rose 23 cents to $15.71.   The most traded stock by volume was St Barbara Ltd with 12.67 million shares worth $4.78 million changing hands, pushing the stock four cents, or 11.43 per cent higher to 39 cents.   AAP
