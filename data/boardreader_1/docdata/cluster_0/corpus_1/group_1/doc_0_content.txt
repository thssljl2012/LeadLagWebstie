 THE share market opened lower today, dragged down by the financial and resources sectors and on a flat lead from Wall Street.At 10.15am (AEST), the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 was 38.8 points lower, or 0.78 per cent to 4942.9, while the broader All Ordinaries fell 36.1 points, or 0.72 per cent to 5002.8. The September share price index futures contract decreased ten points to 4928 on a volume of 4038 contracts. The financial sector remained weak with Commonwealth Bank of Australia down $1.43, or 3.27 per cent to $42.27, while National Australia Bank slid 15 cents to $24.90. ANZ dipped 22 cents, or 1.3 per cent to $16.68, takeover-target St George Bank fell three cents to $29.69 but Westpac added five cents to $22.85. Mining giant BHP Billiton fell 13 cents to $37.85 while take-over target Rio Tinto shed $1.43, or 1.24 per cent, to $113.72. Wall Street ended a volatile week with a mixed showing on Friday as worries about credit markets and the economy tempered investors' upbeat sentiments about falling oil prices. The Dow Jones Industrial Average advanced 43.97 points, or 0.38 per cent, to close at 11,659.90, while the tech-rich Nasdaq composite fell 1.15 points, or 0.05 per cent to 2452.52. The broad-market Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index gained 5.27 points to finish at 1298.20.Austock Securities senior client adviser Michael Heffernan said he expected the local market to have a reasonable day. 'The banks are just holding their head above water and BHP is down a bit but its annual report is coming out today and that's expected to be pretty robust,'' he said. Making news this morning, gloves and condoms maker Ansell reported annual net profit for 2007/08 of $102.6 million, up 2.6 per cent on the prior year. Its shares went up two cents to $11.25. BlueScope Steel lost four cents to $9.09 after it reported a fall in annual profit on one-off charges. Online employment agency Seek fell 15 cents, or 2.95 per cent, to $4.94 despite lifting full year profit by 37 per cent. At 10.34am AEST, the spot price of gold was $US796.65, up $US6.00 from Friday's Sydney close of $US790.65. Local gold major Newcrest dropped 43 cents to $24.00, Newmont was steady at $4.89 while Lihir Gold fell three cents to $2.23. At 10.38am AEST, Woodside Petroleum had lost 18 cents to $54.02, Santos was down 26 to $17.46 but Oil Search had gained two cents to $4.95. In media, Olympics broadcaster Seven Network was down 30 cents, or 3.68 per cent, to $7.85 and Ten Network was up two centS at $1.67. News Corporation - the parent company of the publisher of NEWS.com.au - dropped 13 cents to $16.23, while its non-voting scrip also fell four cents to $15.82. Consolidated Media was steady at $3.25 and Fairfax Media dipped three cents to $2.72.At 11.05am AEST, Woolworths was up 52 cents at $27.22, Wesfarmers was down 48 cents to $35.22, and David Jones added three cents to $4.00. Qantas was steady at $3.50 while rival Virgin Blue added 2.5 cents to $1.18. At 10.51am AEST, the top traded stock by volume was Newstat with 22.2 million shares changing hands worth $44,307.
