 Japanese shares faltered after opening higher as worries about a slowing global economy dragged on banks such as Sumitomo Mitsui Financial Group, while  declined on a report the auto giant planned to cut its 2009 sales target. The Nikkei 225 Average slipped 0.7% to 12,763.62 and the broader Topix index lost 0.7% to 1,224.57, after both benchmarks gained earlier in the day. Other Asian markets declined as well, with Australia's S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 fell 1.1% to 4,877.30 and South Korea's Kospi dropped 1% to 1,524.97, while New Zealand's NZX 50 index added 0.2% to 3,338.60. Shares of Toyota (TM) fell 1.2% after the Nikkei business daily reported the company, which has already cut its 2008 sales target, planned to reduce its 2009 sales forecast to 9.8 million vehicles from 10.4 million. Financial stocks declined across the region, with Sumitomo Mitsui FG (SMFJY) losing 1.2% in Tokyo and Commonwealth Bank of Australia shrinking 1.7% in Sydney, while Kookmin Bank (KB) slipped 0.8% in Seoul. Inpex Holdings added 2.5% in Tokyo and Santos (STOSY) jumped 7% in Sydney after crude-oil prices advanced overnight. October crude-oil futures climbed as much as 26 cents to $115.82 a barrel in electronic trading, after gaining $1.02 to $115.56 a barrel Wednesday on the New York Mercantile Exchange. In Sydney, shares of Qantas Airways gained 0.3%, although the airline's 44% jump in annual net profit fell below analyst expectations, after it increased its full-year dividend payout. In Asian currency trading, the U.S. dollar bought 109.63 yen, compared with 109.91 yen late Wednesday. On Wall Street, the Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJI) rose 0.6% to 11,417.43, the Nasdaq Composite (RIXF) gained 0.2% to 2,389.08 and the S&amp;amp;P 500 index (SPX) advanced 0.6% to 1,274.54.
