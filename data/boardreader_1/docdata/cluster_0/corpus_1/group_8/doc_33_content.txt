By a staff reporter   The Australian share market has opened stronger after Wall Street posted marginal gains in a choppy session overnight.   At 1015 AEDT, the S&amp;P/ASX200 index had risen 23 points, or 0.7 per cent to 3,267.4 points, while the All Ordinaries had gained 20.9 points, or 0.65 per cent, to 3,220 points.   National Australia Bank Ltd led a rise in banking stocks after saying it would cut its first half dividend by 25 per cent. NAB shares rose 4.66 per cent to $17.06.   ANZ Banking Group Ltd gained 0.15 per cent to $13.17, Commonwealth Bank of Australia Ltd firmed 0.24 per cent to $28.68 and Westpac Banking Corporation Ltd found 0.47 per cent to $16.95.   Investment firm Macquarie Group Ltd rose 1.31 per cent to $18.50.   The insurers were mixed. QBE Insurance Group Ltd jumped 1.31 per cent to $16.21, Insurance Australia Group Ltd climbed 0.93 per cent to $3.25, while Suncorp-Metway Ltd dipped 0.2 per cent to $4.89.   Resources firms received a mixed lead from commodities. Gold and silver prices rose overnight, will oil and copper sank.   Mining giant BHP Billiton Ltd climbed 1.7 per cent to $31.02, while rival Rio Tinto Ltd was trading 1.94 per cent higher at $50.77.   It was a mixed start to the session for second-tier base metal miners. Fortescue Metals Group Ltd gained 0.4 per cent to $2.46, OZ Minerals Ltd was trading flat at 61 cents, while OneSteel Ltd fell 2.51 per cent to $1.745 and Murchison Metals Ltd dropped 2.98 per cent to 65 cents.   Gold miners rose with the price of the precious metal. Newcrest Mining Ltd gained 0.77 per cent to $31.15, Lihir Gold Ltd firmed 2.44 per cent to $2.93 and Sino Gold Mining Ltd rose 0.79 per cent to $5.10.   On Wednesday, the Australian share market closed nearly two per cent higher buoyed by the mining and financial sectors and following a rally on Wall Street. The benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was up 59.9 points, or 1.88 per cent, at 3,244.4, while the broader All Ordinaries index added 55.9 points, or 1.78 per cent, to 3,199.1.
