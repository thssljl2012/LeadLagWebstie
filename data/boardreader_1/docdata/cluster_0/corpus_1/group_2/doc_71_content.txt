(Xinhua via COMTEX News Network) -- The FTSE 100 index in the London stock market fell in Monday morning trading, dropping below the 5,000-point level, despite news of an agreement over the 700-billion-dollar U.S. financial rescue package. By 08:50 BST FTSE index fell 88.48 points to 4,999.99, or 1.74 percent. Banks saw the largest falls as the government announced details of the nationalization of Bradford &amp;amp; Bingley. HBOS shares fell 9.4 percent, Royal Bank of Scotland dropped 8 percent and Lloyds TSB was 7.7 percent lower.
