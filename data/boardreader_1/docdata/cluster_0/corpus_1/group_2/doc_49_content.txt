(Xinhua via COMTEX News Network) -- Shares in the owner of the Halifax bank, HBOS, swung back and forth in a tumultuous trading session that knocked 2 percent off the FTSE 100 index on Wednesday. The benchmark FTSE 100 index lost 113.40 points, or 2.25 percent, to end at 4,912.40. Despite news of a potential merger between HBOS and Lloyds TSB, HBOS shares sank 19 percent to 147.1 pence. Bank shares were volatile with Royal Bank of Scotland and HSBC falling, but Barclays gained 3.2 percent after agreeing to buy Lehman Brothers' U.S. business.
