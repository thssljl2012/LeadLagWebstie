SYDNEY, Sept 26 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 0.7 percent on Friday, reversing initial gains as financial stocks such as Macquarie Group and miner BHP Billiton (News) gave up gains made earlier in the session. Confidence was eroded by a slide in U.S. stock futures as Congress continues to debate the United States' planned $700 billion rescue package for the financial sector. The S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index was down 35 points at 4,892.4 at 0321 GMT. Earlier it had been over 1 percent higher at 4,990.5. New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index was down 0.9 percent at 3,208.9 New Zealand fell into recession for the first time in more than a decade, data showed on Friday. STOCKS ON THE MOVE * Alumina Ltd. fell 10 percent to A$3.44. The company said it raised A$266 million ($222 million) in a retail offer at A$3.35 a share. 0316 GMT * Bank stocks were mixed, with Commonwealth Bank of Australia down a cent at A$44.04 after earlier touching A$44.98. National Australia Bank was up 1.1 percent at A$25.26. 0316 GMT * Investment bank Babcock and Brown gave up initial gains of around 10 percent to be down 2.5 percent at A$2.31. Larger rival Macquarie Group Ltd, Australia's top investment bank, fell 6.2 percent to A$36.83. Macquarie said it plans to sell its investment lending business, which contributes less than 1 percent to Macquarie Group's profits. 0317 GMT * BHP Billiton Ltd fell 1.2 percent to A$35.96, almost A$1 off earlier highs. 0318 GMT * Foster's Group shares gained 6 percent to A$5.67. Australia's largest brewer on Friday named Ian Johnston as chief executive, a move likely to speed up a decision on the fate of the company's troubled A$5 billion ($4.2 billion) wine business. 0319 GMT * Pan Australian Resources Ltd rose 6 percent to 61 cents.
