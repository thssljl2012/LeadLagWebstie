(Reuters) - European shares fell further by late morning on Monday as concerns about the health of the financial markets deepened after U.S. investment bank Lehman Brothers (LEH.N: Quote, Profile, Research) filed for bankruptcy protection.By 1027 GMT, the FTSEurofirst 300 index of top European shares was down 4.1 percent at 1,114.19 points, after falling as low as 1,113.65 -- the lowest in nearly two months. The benchmark, which is down about 26 percent so far this year, was on track for its biggest one-day fall since mid-March.Lehman filed for bankruptcy protection after trying to finance too many risky assets with too little capital, making it the largest and highest-profile casualty of the global credit crisis. Its shares (LHMH.F: Quote, Profile, Research) plummeted 80 percent in Frankfurt.Banks were the biggest weighted losers on the index, with HBOS (HBOS.L: Quote, Profile, Research), Barclays (BARC.L: Quote, Profile, Research) BNP Paribas (BNPP.PA: Quote, Profile, Research), Credit Agricole (CAGR.PA: Quote, Profile, Research), Dexia (DEXI.BR: Quote, Profile, Research), Fortis (FOR.BR: Quote, Profile, Research) and Societe Generale (SOGN.PA: Quote, Profile, Research) down between 7.6 and 21 percent.(Reporting by Atul Prakash)
