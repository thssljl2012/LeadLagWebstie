Asian equity markets opened higher Friday after a nearly 5% surge on Wall Street overnight. However, the enthusiasm didn't last long, with most major indexes falling into the red by midday.A day after its worst loss since the 1987 stock market crash, Japan's Nikkei 225 average rose as much as 3.6% in the morning session, while the broader Topix topped out at a 4% gain, led by high-tech stocks such as Kyocera Corp (nyse: KYO - news - people ), which gained 2.2%, and the automakers. Honda Motor (nyse: HMC - news - people ) bounced 4.3% and Toyota Motor (nyse: TM - news - people ) rose 2.1%.Yet, buying orders were not strong enough to sustain the rally at those levels.
