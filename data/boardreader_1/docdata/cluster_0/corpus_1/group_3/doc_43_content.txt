Australian shares surged in early trade and remained high at noon, after Wall Street staged an amazing afternoon rally with the Dow Jones Industrial Average rocketing 11%.At noon, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index was up 4.5%, or 170.1 points, to 3967.7 points. 'So far so good,' said Kevin Massey, manager of corporate broking at Foster Stockbroking. 'The market has moved ahead quite strongly at the open but is steadying now. It's good to see the market is not running away. 'Hopefully, it's building a bit of a base here and it's not going too strong.' Mr Massey said it was preferable the market should remain at the current level rather than surging 400 points only to lose 200 points later in the session. Stocks were pretty strong 'across the board', he said.
