SYDNEY, Oct 17 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 0.8 percent on Friday, reversing earlier gains of over 3 percent, as bank shares and BHP Billiton (News) turned lower amid ongoing worries about a global recession. By 0217 GMT, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index was down 30 points at 3,983.4, after rising as high as 4,150.4. It had been down as much as 1 percent. New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index rose 1.9 percent to 2,817.08. STOCKS ON THE MOVE * BHP stock fell 2.7 percent to A$25.07 after starting out higher. The price of oil and other commodities such as copper and gold fell in offshore trade. 0210 GMT * ABB Grain stock rose 7 percent to A$7.37 after the company lifted its forecast net profit for 2008 to A$47-49 million ($32-34 million), from an earlier A$33-38 million, saying its second half had been especially good. Final results are due Nov. 25. 0215 GMT * Banks turned lower. National Australia Bank was down 2.3 percent at A$22.19 after earlier reaching A$23.49. Commonwealth Bank of Australia fell 1.3 percent to A$41.86, off a day's high of A$43.40 and Australia and New Zealand Banking Group was down 1.2 percent at A$17.09 from an earlier high of A$17.93. 0135 GMT * Miner Fortescue Metals Ltd fell 2.6 percent to A$3.02. The company is is renegotiating iron ore delivery contracts with Chinese customers following sharp falls in steel and shipping freight prices, the Sydney Morning Herald reported. Some customers were asking Fortescue to wear some freight prices, it said. 0219 GMT * Gold producer Newcrest Mining slipped 3.8 percent to A$23.32 and Lihir Gold fell 5.5 percent to A$1.965 after a fall in the gold price. 0015 GMT * Macquarie Airports rose 3.5 percent to A$2.35 after it said earnings before interest, tax, depreciation and amortisation rose 9.4 percent in the nine months to end September. 2355 GMT (Reporting by Miranda Maxwell).ntCOPYRIGHTCopyright Thomson Financial News Limited 2008.
