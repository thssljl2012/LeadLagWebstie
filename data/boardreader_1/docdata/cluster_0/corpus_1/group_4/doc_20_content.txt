Australian shares are likely to open lower after another disappointing day on Wall Street.  In recent trading, the December Share Price Index futures contract on the Sydney Futures Exchange was down 26 points at 3,525.  Yesterday, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 share index ended 3.6% lower, wiping more than $30 billion off the value of stocks, to 3523.2 points, a closing level not seen since August 2004.  The Australian dollar was holding its own, trading recently at 64.65 US cents, little changed from yesterday's local close of 64.6 US cents. It was also buying 62.39 yen.  A closely watched commodity price gauge, the Reuters/Jefferies CRB index, was recently down 0.6%, indicating miners will also decline at the opening.  One variable today will be the resumption of short-selling on non-banking shares after a two-month ban imposed by the Australian Securities and Investment Commission to stabilise markets. (See story here).  With Wall Street yet to close, the Dow Jones Industrial Average was down 156.19 points, or 1.9%, at 8,117.39. The Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 Index was down 23.17 points, or 2.7%, at 827.58. The Nasdaq Composite Index was down 50.02 points, or 3.4%, at 1,432.03.  European markets were mostly higher overnight. London's FTSE 100 index of leading shares ended up 1.9%, while Germany's DAX advanced 0.5%. In Paris, the CAC 40 added 1.1%.  In economic news today, the Westpac/Melbourne Institute leading index of economic activity for September will be released, as will new motor vehicle sales data from the Australian Bureau of Statistics.  The Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA), deputy governor, economics, Malcolm Edey addresses the Australia and Japan Economic Outlook conference in Sydney, and in Melbourne, RBA governor Glenn Stevens addresses the Committee for Economic Development of Australia's annual dinner tonight.  In equities, AWB and Graincorp release annual results.  Companies holding annual general meetings include Regional Express Holdings, Tatts Group, IOOF Holdings, Allco HIT, Macarthur Coal and Deep Yellow.  Yesterday, the broader All Ordinaries lost 126.4 points, or 3.5%, to 3513.1 led down by resource and banking stocks.
