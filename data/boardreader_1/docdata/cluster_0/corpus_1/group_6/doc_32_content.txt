North American stocks were higher early Friday after the largest U.S. bank received a $20-billion US infusion from the government, overshadowing big quarterly losses in the sector.  Photograph by: Peter Jones, Reuters  North American stocks gave up a strong start to Friday only to be in the hole by midday.  This change of direction seemed linked to differing perceptions of the U.S. governments $20-billion US bailout for Bank of America Corp. While there was enthusiasm about the arrangement as markets opened, the sentiment later turned as investors doubted that such assistance to the industry will prevent the recession from worsening.  The Toronto Stock Exchanges composite index was down about 90 points, or one per cent, to 8,790, with financials being the main drag after rising early in Fridays trading session.  In the U.S., the Dow Jones industrial average was down about 35 points, or 0.4 per cent, to 8,180. The Nasdaq composite index was off by around 10 points, or 0.7 per cent, at 1,500.  This was in contrast to stock indexes in Europe in Asia, which saw strong gains on Friday.  The February contract for crude oil on the New York Mercantile Exchange was down 73 cents to $34.67 US a barrel. Gold was up $29.70 to $837 US an ounce.  The Canadian dollar was down 17 basis points at midday to 79.73 cents US.  On Thursday, the S&amp;P/TSX composite ended up 191.25 points, or 2.2 per cent, to 8,879.61. The Dow closed up 12.35 points, or 0.15 per cent, to 8,212.49, and the Nasdaq rose 22.2 points, or 1.49 per cent, to 1,511.84.
