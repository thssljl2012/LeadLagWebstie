NEW YORK, Jan 15 (Reuters) - U.S. stocks slipped at the open on Thursday as worries stemming from news of a push by Bank of America for more government aid more than offset reassuring quarterly results from JPMorgan Chase .   * Shares of Bank of America dropped more than 15 percent to $8.67, but shares of JPMorgan climbed 3.2 percent to $26.75.   * Shares of Apple Inc fell on Nasdaq after the iPod and iPhone maker said Chief Executive Steve Jobs had decided to take a leave of absence due to health issues. The stock was off 3.6 percent to $82.20.   The Dow Jones industrial average was down 54.00 points, or 0.66 percent, at 8,155.06. The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 Index was down 5.48 points, or 0.65 percent, at 837.14. The Nasdaq Composite Index was down 5.13 points, or 0.34 percent, at 1,484.51.
