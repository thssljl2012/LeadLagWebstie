THE sharemarket was 2 per cent lower at noon after falls in the financial sector outweighed strength in resources.  At 12.00pm (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index fell 74 points, or 2.03 per cent, to 3,566.7.  The broader All Ordinaries index was down 64.8 points, or 1.81 per cent, at 3,508.5.  'The market started off well on the back of a stronger Wall Street last night but the finance and bank sector has seen some ongoing weakness,'' said ABN Amro Morgans Ipswich manager Tony Russell.  'Volumes are fairly moderate, the resource sector is holding it up a little bit with Rio on the back of their announcement of cutting costs and jobs.''  Rio Tinto, which plans to slash 14,000 jobs, cut spending and costs and sell assets, was $3.40 or 9.09 per cent higher at $40.80.  Rival BHP Billiton fell 16 cents to $30.20.  In the banking sector, Westpac gained 4 cents to $16.41, Commonwealth Bank lost 47 cents to $28.03, National Australia Bank sank 12 cents to $19.48 and ANZ lifted 15 cents to $14.30.  Investment bank Macquarie Group lost 35 cents to $30.15 while rival Babcock and Brown dropped one cent, or 6.06 per cent, to 15.5 cents.  In company news, Bank of Queensland says it will consider takeovers and alliances, as the regional lender aims to take the place vacated in the market by St George and BankWest.  Bank of Queensland shares fell 5 cents to $10.22.  Oz Minerals refutes allegations it engaged in misleading or deceptive conduct and says it has only acted in the best interests of shareholders.  Its shares were placed in a trading halt on November 28 amid concern about its ability to refinance debt and last traded at 55 cents.  Fortescue Metals lost 2 cents to $2.71 after it could not explain why its share price moved to a high of $2.85 on Wednesday, from $2.34 on Tuesday.  The retail sector was weaker.  Wesfarmers, which owns Coles, was 71 cents, or 4.34 per cent lower at $15.64 and Woolworths fell $1.26, or 4.65 per cent, to $25.82.  David Jones lost 18 cents, or 5.98 per cent, to $2.83 while Harvey Norman dropped 2 cents to $2.28.  Oil prices rose overnight as a US government report showed crude inventories were much slimmer than expected.  In energy, Woodside dropped $1.31, or 3.89 per cent, to $32.39, Santos added one cent to $13.91 while Oil Search decreased eight cents to $4.68.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the December share price index contract was 43 points lower at 3,578 on a volume of 15,433 contracts.
