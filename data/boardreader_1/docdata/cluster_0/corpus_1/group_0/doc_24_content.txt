 Asian markets rallied Wednesday as financials such as  were spurred by overnight gains on Wall Street, while transportation stocks advanced after crude-oil prices fell sharply. In Tokyo, the Nikkei 225 Average climbed 1.1% to 13,329.26 and the Topix index added 1.6% to 1,308.01. Australia's S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 rose 1.3% to 5,070.10 and South Korea's Kospi advanced 1.5% to 1,584.55, while New Zealand's NZX 50 index jumped 2% to 3,206.92. Shares of  (MFG) climbed 3.4%, Citigroup Inc. (C) soared 8.3% and Sumitomo Mitsui Financial Group (SMFJY) advanced 3.2% in Tokyo. The rise came after Wachovia Corp. (WB) shares spiked as much as 27% overnight on Wall Street. The rise came although Wachovia reported nearly $9 billion in quarterly losses, after the lender said it wouldn't dilute shareholders' equity, and a Deutsche Bank analyst said the quarterly performance 'should mark a bottom' for the bank. In Sydney, National Australia Bank (NABZY) advanced 3.3% and Commonwealth Bank of Australia added 2.9% in Sydney, while Kookmin Bank (KB) gained 3% in Seoul. Earlier in the day, Commonwealth Bank said it was in discussions with Royal Bank of Scotland to acquire ABN Amro Australia Holdings Pty. The announcement came a day after NAB announced it had withdrawn from talks to acquire ABN Amro Australia. The region's transportation stocks also made advances, with  ( HMC) gaining 3.6% in Tokyo and Qantas Airways (QUBSF) rising 2.7% in Sydney. In Seoul, Korean Air Co. (KRNRF) stock surged 4.8%, Hyundai Motor Co. (HYMLF) rose 1.7% and Kia Motors Corp. (KIMTF) added 2.2%. Shares of Suzuki Motor Corp. (7269.TO) added 1.2% in Tokyo, after the Nikkei business daily reported that the compact car-maker is expected to report a record group operating profit for the quarter ended June 30, on robust sales in emerging markets. September crude-oil futures fell $3.40 to $128.42 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange Tuesday, when the August contract expired. The new front- month, September contract recently slipped as much as two cents to $128.40 a barrel in electronic trading. Gains in the transportation sector were countered by losses in energy-related stocks. Shares of oil producer Inpex Holdings dropped 3.6% and commodity trader Marubeni Corp. (MARUY) shed 1.4% in Tokyo. In Sydney, BHP Billiton (BHP) gave up 1.8% and Woodside Petroleum (WOPEY) sank 2.9%. In Asian currency trading, the U.S. dollar recently bought 107.30 yen, compared with 106.47 yen late on Tuesday. On Wall Street, the Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJI) rose 135.16 points to finish at 11,602.50 on the back of gains in financial stocks. The S&amp;amp;P 500 index (SPX) gained 17 points to 1,277, while the Nasdaq Composite (RIXF) advanced 24.43 points to 2,303.96.
