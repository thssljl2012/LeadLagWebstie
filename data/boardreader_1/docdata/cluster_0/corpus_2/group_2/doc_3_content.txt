added a total A$3.015 billion in cash [nSYU005141].    ___________________________________  ____________________________    * The Australian dollar was subdued near recent lows against the U.S. dollar and the Japanese yen as investors dumped riskier assets and higher-yielding currencies amid deepening concerns about the stability of the U.S. financial sector.    * The U.S. government&amp;#039;s bailout of American International Group left investors fretting which company would be next to be hit by the turmoil in the financial markets.    * In a rare development and a pointer to further upheavals were in store, the yields of one-month U.S. Treasury bills briefly fell below zero in anticipation of heavy redemption from investors spooked by deep losses in money market funds.    * Rating agency Moody&amp;#039;s slashed the ratings of the Reserve Management Company money market funds.    * The intensifying credit market turmoil saw regional stock markets fall sharply, prompting investors to unwind leveraged carry trade positions. The Aussie hovered near 2- year lows against the yen, trading at 82.27 yen, not far from its low of 81.39 yen struck earlier this week.    * Shortly after midday, the Aussie slid to $0.7881 from $0.8005 late here on Wednesday, and within sight of a fresh 13-month low of $0.7800 struck in the offshore session.    * Offering the Aussie some support were higher gold prices. Australia is an exporter of the metal and gold extended gains in Asia to the highest since Aug. 5 as investors fled to safe-haven assets.    * Government bonds, gold and the low-yielding yen are preferred by investors at the time of heightened risk aversion.    * Australian bond futures were higher as the financial market troubles led investors to safe haven government bonds.    * Three-year bond futures were up 0.16 points at 94.825, while the 10-year bond contract added 0.085 points to 94.55.
