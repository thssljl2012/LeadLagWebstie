           STOCKS - AUSTRALIAN SHARES CRASH SHARPLY LOWER AT OPEN - JULY 25  Thursday, July 24, 2008; Posted: 09:56 PM  SYDNEY, Jul 25, 2008 (AsiaPulse via COMTEX) -- NAB   Quote   Chart   News   PowerRating -- The Australian stock market has opened over three per cent lower after National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX:NAB) took an additional provision of A$830 million (US$792.89 million) on its exposure to a portfolio of collateralised debt obligations and stocks fell over two per cent in the US overnight.  At 1015 AEST the S&amp;amp;amp;P/ASX200 index fell 157.1 points, or 3.05 per cent, to 4,987 while the broader All Ordinaries dipped 139.4 points, or 2.69 per cent, to 5,049.  The September share price index futures contract fell 55 points to 4,987, on a volume of 8,446 contracts.  NAB dropped A$3.25, or 10.59 per cent to A$27.24 as Commonwealth Bank of Australia (ASX:CBA) fell A$2.39, or 5.15 per cent to A$44.00, ANZ (ASX:ANZ) lost A$1.01, or 5.19 per cent, to A$18.44, and Westpac (ASX:WBC) fell 82 cents, or 3.6 per cent, to A$21.98.  The nation&amp;amp;#039;s fifth largest bank St George (ASX:SGB) decreased A$1.05 cents, or 3.54 per cent, to A$28.60.  Wall Street declined overnight as a weaker-than-expected report on the US housing market raised fresh fears about an economic recovery and ended a rush into banking stocks.  Record losses reported by Ford Motor Co, forced to step up its restructuring, also contributed to the negative tone in the market.  The Dow Jones Industrial Average sank 283.1 points, or 2.43 per cent, to close at 11,349.28 and the broad-market Standard &amp;amp;amp; Poor&amp;amp;#039;s 500 index retreated 29.65 points, or 2.31 per cent, to 1,252.54.  The Nasdaq composite shed 45.77 points, or 1.97 per cent, to 2,280.11. At 1044 AEST, spot gold was trading at US$927.70 per fine ounce in Sydney, up US$1.40 from Thursday&amp;amp;#039;s close of US$926.30.  In the energy sector, Woodside (ASX:WPL) lost seven cents to A$52.61, Santos (ASX:STO) increased 50 cents, or 2.94 per cent to A$17.48 and Oil Search (ASX:OSH) increased nine cents to A$5.27. In the retail sector, Woolworths (ASX:WOW) added 15 cents to A$25.98, David Jones (ASX:DJS) lost six cents, or 1.74 per cent, to A$3.38 and Coles owner Wesfarmers (ASX:WES) lost 60 cents to A$34.80.  Media stocks were mostly lower. Consolidated Media Holdings (ASX:CMJ) increased three cents to A$3.18 and Fairfax (ASX:FXJ) lost nine cents, or 2.89 per cent to A$3.02.
