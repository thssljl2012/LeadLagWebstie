(4695.4)    US10-YR   3.57(3.65)    __________________________ Oct 6_____________________________    * The Australian dollar slumped to two-year lows on the U.S. dollar on Monday, while gouging out a near four-year trough on the yen, pressured by fears about the global economy and expectations local interest rates will have to be cut sharply.    * Bonds added to recent hefty gains on intense speculation the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) will cut the 7.0 percent cash rate by an aggressive 50 basis points at its monthly policy meeting on Tuesday, and ease again in November or December.    * Aussie dived as deep as $0.7573 in volatile trade, down from $0.7737 late in New York on Friday and the first break under 76 cents since October 2006.    * Traders reported heavy stop-loss selling on the break of key chart support at $0.7675, a low from August last year, while Japanese investors dumped it for the safety of the yen.    * The Aussie sank to 79.33 yen, down sharply from 81.45 late on Friday and the lowest since January 2005.    * News that Germany and Denmark had guaranteed bank deposits and EU financial leaders were split on a bailout fund for euro zone banks kept investors on edge.    * Outlook for world economy dimmed by Friday&amp;#039;s dismal U.S. payrolls report, which was bad for commodities and for open economies like Australia that rely on resource exports.    * The CRB index eased 0.58 percent on Friday to 326.51, while oil slid around $2 on Monday to $91.94.    * Australian bond futures were lifted by safe-haven flows with equities in Australia and Asia lower and U.S. stock futures already down around 1.6 percent.    * Three-year Australian bond futures climbed 0.055 points to 95.125, while the 10-year bond contract rose 0.065 points to 94.775.    * Market fully priced for a cut of 50 basis points from the RBA on Tuesday and sees 158 basis points of total easing in the next 12 months.
