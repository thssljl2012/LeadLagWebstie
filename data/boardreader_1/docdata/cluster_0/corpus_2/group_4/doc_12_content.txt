 November 11, 2008    Article from:  Australian Associated Press        THE Australian share market closed 3.5 per cent lower today, sharply reversing its gains of yesterday, as renewed fears of a global recession and a domestic slowdown weighed on the local bourse.         At the 4.15pm (AEDT) close, the benchmark S&amp;amp;amp;P/ASX200 index was 146.9 points, or 3.58 per cent lower at 3960.9, while the broader All Ordinaries lost 138.2 points, or 3.4 per cent to 3921.8.       At 4.18pm AEDT on the Sydney Futures Exchange, the December share price index contract was 96 points lower at 4002, on a volume of 30,227 contracts.       CMC Markets senior dealer Dominic Vaughan said the local market was plagued by a combination of negative global sentiment, expectations Australia was heading for a recession and thin trading volumes.       &amp;amp;quot;There&amp;#039;s a whole lot of negative sentiment still, and there&amp;#039;s talk a recession in Australia is unavoidable, how deep it is we&amp;#039;re not really sure,&amp;#039;&amp;#039; Mr Vaughan said.       &amp;amp;quot;There were falls right across the board today, even the healthcare centre, which is normally relatively resilient has come under pressure.       &amp;amp;quot;On top of that, volumes are very thin and that&amp;#039;s exacerbating these price movements.&amp;#039;&amp;#039;       The market opened almost 3 per cent lower after a fall on Wall Street overnight, with the Dow Jones industrial average dropping 73.27, or 0.82 per cent, to close at 8870.54.       Locally, the big miners were weaker, with BHP Billiton falling $1.09 to $28.80, Rio Tinto shedding $1.51 to $76.49 and Fortescue Metals losing 17 cents to $2.28.      The banking sector was weaker, with Commonwealth Bank giving up $2.39, or 6.29 per cent, to $35.61 and National Australia Bank falling $2 to $20.15 after its trading halt was lifted following its $3 billion capital raising at $20 a share.       ANZ retreated 22 cents to $15.68 and Westpac shedded 61 cents to $19.30.      Insurance Australia Group dipped four cents to $3.97.      Computershare lost 61 cents to $7.18 after the world&amp;#039;s largest share registry cut its earnings guidance this financial year because of the stronger US dollar.       The stapled securities of Asciano Group fell almost 60 per cent to 69 cents before the transport infrastructure called for a trading halt.       The retailers were weaker, with Woolworths falling 53 cents to $28.65, Wesfarmers shedding $2.87, or 12.48 per cent, to $20.13, Harvey Norman losing 6c to $2.83 and David Jones retreating two cents to $3.15.       Health IT company IBA Health Group gave up half a cent to 64 cents despite the group saying its revenues would more than double after the launch of its new healthcare IT platform Lorenzo.       The media sector was mixed, with Consolidated Media Holdings losing 5c to $2.10. News Corporation (the parent company of the publisher of news.com.au) fell 24 cents to $12.40 buts its non-voting shares added 12 cents to $12.70, and Fairfax dropped 12.5 cents to $1.745.
