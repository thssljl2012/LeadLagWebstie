Australia's central bank cut its official interest rate by a bigger-than-expected 1 percentage point Tuesday to ease credit concerns amid a global financial turmoil, cheering investors around the region and lifting Australian stocks into positive territoryThe cut to 6 percent was the largest by the Reserve Bank of Australia since May 1992. Analysts had expected a half-point reduction.Australian stocks jumped on the news after opening sharply lower and other Asian markets gained after the move as well. Sydney's benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX-200 index was up 2 percent in afternoon trading.
