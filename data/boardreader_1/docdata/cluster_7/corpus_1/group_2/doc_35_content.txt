(Adds Mizuho declining comment, background)TOKYO, Sept 19 (Reuters) - Mizuho Financial Group (8411.T: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz), Japan's second-largest bank, will strengthen its ties with Bank of America Corp (BAC.N: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz) and Merrill Lynch &amp;amp; Co (MER.N: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz), Japan's Jiji news agency reported on Friday.Mizuho already has ties with Merrill, having invested $1.2 billion in it in January when the subprime-hit U.S. brokerage was scrambling to raise cash. Bank of America said earlier this week it would spend $50 billion to take over Merrill.Mizuho Corporate Bank, the Mizuho group's wholesale banking unit, will collaborate with Bank of America and Merrill in Asia, Jiji said.A spokesman for Mizuho Financial Group declined to comment. No one was available at Mizuho Corporate Bank.Unlike their Western rivals, Japan's largest banks have largely avoided the massive credit losses that has crippled Wall Street.As such, Japanese lenders have been looking to expand their business abroad.Mizuho rival Sumitomo Mitsui Financial Group (8316.T: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz) has taken a 2 percent stake in British bank Barclays (BARC.L: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz) while Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group (8306.T: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz) has said it will make its U.S. arm, UnionBanCal Corp (UB.N: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz), a wholly owned unit.
