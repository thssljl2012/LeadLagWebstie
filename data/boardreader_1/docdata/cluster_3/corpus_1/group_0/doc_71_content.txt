 NEW YORK, July 9 (Reuters) - ANZ National International Ltd, a unit of Australia and New Zealand Banking Group (ANZ.AX: Quote, Profile, Research, Stock Buzz), on Wednesday sold $2 billion of five-year notes, said International Financing Review.The 6.20 percent notes were priced at 99.906 to yield 6.222 percent, or 3.125 percentage points more than U.S. Treasuries, said IFR, a Thomson Reuters publication.Citigroup Global Markets, Goldman Sachs and JP Morgan were the joint lead managers of the transaction.
