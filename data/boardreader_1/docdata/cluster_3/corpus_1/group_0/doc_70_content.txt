 MELBOURNE (MarketWatch) -- The board of National Australia Bank Ltd. (NAB.AU) is speculated to be considering replacing Chief Executive John Stewart with the head of NAB's New Zealand operation, Cameron Clyne, The Australian reports Wednesday. Without citing its sources, the newspaper also said Stewart has told NAB Chairman Michael Chaney he would like to be back in Britain by the end of the year. The board, which will gather for a meeting Wednesday, recently activated its succession plan, it said. -Contact: 201-938-5400
