EL DORADO HILLS, Calif. -- A Bank of America on Park Drive was robbed Tuesday, the El Dorado County Sheriff's Office said.  Staff members told deputies that the robber waited in line, approached an open teller window and demanded money from the teller.  The robber didn't use a note during the robbery and didn't indicate that he had a weapon, deputies said. He took off with an undisclosed amount of money.  He is described as a white man, about 6 feet tall, 240 pounds, unshaven and about 40 years old. He was seen wearing a heavy blue jacket and blue hat. The man has a tattoo on the left side of his neck, witnesses said, which is described as blue and possibly depicting flames. He also had a raspy or scratchy voice, according to deputies.  Anyone with information on the bank robbery may call the El Dorado County Sheriff's Office 530-621-6600.
