POLICE are investigating an armed robbery at a Commonwealth Bank in Sydney - amid reports the male crook was dressed as a woman.  Police say the man - said by locals to have been clad in black stockings and a wig - stormed into the bank on Concord Rd, North Strathfield about 8.30am armed with a handgun, The Daily Telegraph reports.  Three employees were preparing to open the bank when the man entered, stealing a sum of cash before fleeing on foot.  'The man threatened the employees and left with a sum of cash,' a police spokeswoman said.  The spokeswoman refused to confirm reports that the man was dressed as a woman.
