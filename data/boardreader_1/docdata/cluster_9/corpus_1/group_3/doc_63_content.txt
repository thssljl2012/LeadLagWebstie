Tips to Crime Stoppers led Puyallup police detectives Wednesday to a man they believe robbed the First Security Bank of Washington on Monday. Photos of the robber were sent to local media Tuesday and within minutes tipsters were calling police to identify him, police said.The 48-year-old suspect was arrested Wednesday at a home in the 3700 block of East I Street in Tacoma. The News Tribune isnt naming him because prosecutors hadnt filed charges against him.The robber entered the bank at 307 W. Stewart St. about 11:35 a.m. and showed the teller a note that demanded cash. The note also indicated the man had a weapon, police reported. The robber got an undisclosed amount of cash and left the bank on foot.Ian Demsky, The News Tribune
