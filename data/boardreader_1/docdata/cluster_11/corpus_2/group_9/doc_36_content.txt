American Express Helps Financial Stocks RiseLast update: 4/24/2009 10:26:11 AMBy Greg Morcroft Shares of American Express rose 11% Friday after the company reported better-than-expected first-quarter results. American Express, (AXP) a Dow Jones Industrial Average component, helped the Financial Select Sector SPDR, (XLF) which tracks the financial stocks in the S&amp;P 500, rise 1%. Developers Diversified (DDR), a real estate investment trust and one of the nation's largest mall owners, saw its shares rise 16% after it reported first-quarter net income more than doubled, helped by a gain on the repurchase of senior notes. The KBW Banking ETF (KBE) rose 1% as investors awaited the first word, due Friday afternoon, on a government stress test of the nation's 19 largest banks. Among the nation's largest banks, Bank of America (BAC) rose 2%, Citigroup (C) slipped 1.5%, J.P. Morgan Chase (JPM) rose 1.3% and Wells Fargo (WFC) added about 4%. -Greg Morcroft, 415-439-6400; AskNewswires@dowjones.com (END) Dow Jones NewswiresApril 24, 2009 10:26 ET (14:26 GMT)
