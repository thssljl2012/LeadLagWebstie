Coca-Cola Co. (KO) launched $2.25 billion in notes maturing in five and 10 years Tuesday, according to a person familiar with the deal. The five-year portion was launched at a risk premium, or spread, of 185 basis points over comparable Treasury yields.   The 10-year note was launched at a spread of 205 basis points over Treasurys.   Proceeds will be used to repay commercial paper and for general corporate purposes, according to the deal's prospectus.   The deal is expected to price later Tuesday. Bank of America, Citigroup and HSBC are underwriting the offering.   -By Romy Varghese, Dow Jones Newswires; 215-656-8263; romy.varghese@dowjones.com
