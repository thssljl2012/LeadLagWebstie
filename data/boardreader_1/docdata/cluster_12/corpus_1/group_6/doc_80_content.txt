SYDNEY, Jan 14 (Reuters) - National Australia Bank (NAB) has sold A$650 million ($434 million) of five-year government-backed bonds at 100 basis points over BBSW, two market sources who have spoken to NAB said on Wednesday.  The issue consisted of floating rate notes, they said.  NAB was not immediately available to comment.  Australian banks are rushing to raise funds worldwide under a new government guarantee to help them shore up their balance sheets amid the global financial turmoil.  Australia's move follows similar guarantees introduced in other countries.  Over US$29 billion in government-guaranteed bonds has been raised by Australian banks since Dec. 10.
