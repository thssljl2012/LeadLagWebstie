(For the latest Australia and New Zealand bond news, double click on [AU/CRD] and then double click on the ID number)  SYDNEY, Jan 22 (Reuters) - Fletcher Building Ltd , New Zealand's top building products and construction firm, has raised NZ$100 million ($52.2 million) in capital notes and will close its offer on Feb. 5, the company said on Thursday.  The raising for notes due in May 2014 and May 2016 was flagged by Fletcher in November, when it said it was seeking to raise up to NZ$200 million, paying an initial interest rate of 9 percent.  The funds are to retire debt and for general corporate purposes.  The issue is being arranged by First NZ Capital Securities, which is joined as a lead manager by ANZ National Bank and Goldman Sachs JBWere.  The notes are guaranteed on an unsecured, subordinated basis by Fletcher Building Ltd,  Fletcher Building is the country's biggest construction firm, and the world's biggest laminates maker, and makes everything from boards for building to concrete and steel for construction.
