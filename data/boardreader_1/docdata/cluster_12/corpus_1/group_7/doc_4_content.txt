=======Confirmed Pending High-Yield Issues=============   Tyson Foods - $500 million in senior notes due 2014. The company is taking the issue on the road for the next week or so. Book runners are JPMorgan, Bank of America, Barclay's and Wachovia. Pricing expected: Friday, Feb. 27.   Precision Drilling - $250 million in senior notes due 2015. Via Deutsche Bank and RBC. Proceeds will reduce indebtedness under the company's bridge facility and outstanding convertible notes, which were assumed in connection with Precision's acquisition of Grey Wolf, Inc. in Dec. 2008. Pricing Postponed.  =========High-Yield Debt On the Horizon================  Texas Competitive Electric Holdings Co. (formerly TXU Corp.) - $9.45 billion of term loans. Proceeds, along with bonds and loans already issued, will finance the company's buyout by Kohlberg Kravis Roberts &amp; Co. and TPG, formerly Texas Pacific Group. Pricing expected: N/A.   First Data Corp. - Up to $6.8 billion in notes, including up to $1.55 billion in senior cash pay notes, via Citigroup, Credit Suisse, Deutsche Bank, HSBC, Lehman Brothers, Goldman Sachs and Merrill Lynch. Proceeds, along with a $13 billion term loan, will fund a buyout of First Data by KKR.
