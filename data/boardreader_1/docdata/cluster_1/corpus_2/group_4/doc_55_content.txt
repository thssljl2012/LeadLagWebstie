  The policy may look good, but not only do you have to count on the life insurance company acting in good faith, but being around 20 years from now -- think AIG. AIG&amp;#039;s insurance companies are all just fine and have more than adequate reserves to pay claims.  It&amp;#039;s their financial investment arm that got them into the trouble their in and led to their current issues.  The insurance industry is one of the most regulated industries with extremely strict and conservative reserving and states typically take an insurance company into receivership on behalf of the policy holders to ensure they can be paid.  Having said that, it is smart to make sure the insurance company you buy from has a sound financial rating.
