An AIG lawyer who was general counsel of the insurance companys troubled financial products unit has offered to return his $6.4 million bonus, the largest amount handed out to company executives. The lawyer, Douglas Poling, is returning the bonus because he thought it was the correct thing to do, according to AIG spokesman Mark Herr, who spoke to the New York Times. Poling had overseen legal work on contracts for products such as customized insurance-like swaps and other derivative contracts, the Wall Street Journal reports (sub. req.).
