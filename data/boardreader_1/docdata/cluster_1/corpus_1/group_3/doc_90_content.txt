BANGKOK -(Dow Jones)- American International Group Inc (AIG) said Monday it plans to sell stakes in its Thai life insurance business and consumer finance banking arm.The company is looking for a strategic investor to hold a minority stake in Hong Kong-based AIA Holding, which owns the Thai life insurance unit through American International Assurance Hong Kong, said Thomas White, executive vice president of the Thai business.AIG is also considering selling its Thai consumer finance business to help boost liquidity.'It's possible that the new partner will buy our parent (AIG Consumer Finance Group) and get all the operations in several countries. Or it could buy the Thai unit individually,' said Thai AIG Retail Bank president Charly Madan.AIG said Friday it plans to sell a number of businesses including the U.S. domestic life insurance operations and American Life Insurance Co., which operates in Japan, Europe and the Middle East. AIG also said it plans to sell units that offer life insurance in Japan and Taiwan.Last month, the U.S. Federal Reserve said it would lend up to $85 billion to AIG to skirt bankruptcy, effectively placing the insurer under state control.-By Piyarat Setthasiriphaiboon, Dow Jones Newswires; 66 2 2667044; piyarat.setthasiriphaiboon@dowjones.comClick here to go to Dow Jones NewsPlus, a web front page of today's most important business and market news, analysis and commentary: http://www.djnewsplus.com/al?rnd=p6N7fvxBgNVlFcXE2xeYCg%3D%3D.
