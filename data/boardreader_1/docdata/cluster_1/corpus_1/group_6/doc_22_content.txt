 AIG Global Real Estate has announced that it is planning to divest its fund management business, which operates 15 existing fund programs.  The fund management business is a global asset advisor headquartered in New York with regional operations in Europe, Japan, Latin America and Asia and includes committed equity capital that has been funded or is to be funded by AIG Global Real Estate (GRE) as a sponsor or co-investor.   AIG said that Bank of America and Merrill Lynch, GRE's financial advisors, have begun to solicit interest for the fund management business.  Source: Datamonitor
