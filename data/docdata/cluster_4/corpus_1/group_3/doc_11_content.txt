A 21-year-old man has been charged with assaulting the cousin of a man beaten to death when he intervened in a fight in Norwich.Detectives said Daniel Moy, of Costessey, near Norwich, has been accused of attacking Frank McGarahan's cousin in Norwich in the early hours of Sunday.The charge does not relate to the death of Mr McGarahan.Two men in their early 20s from theNorwich area are still being questioned in connection with the murder and magistrates have granted a warrant for the further detention of the pair.A police spokesman said Moy had been released on bail and would appear before Norwich Magistrates' Court on October 15.Mr McGarahan, of Much Hadham, Hertfordshire, who worked as the chief operating officer of Barclays Wealth, had been on a night out in Norwich before a family christening.He intervened with a group of men after seeing a couple attacked in the street.He was hurt after an exchange of words with the gang after walking about 20 yards from a taxi rank to confront the group, Detective Superintendent Chris Hobley said.Police said they thought the first man being attacked was homeless but they discovered that he lived at an address in the city.Copyright (c) Press Association Ltd. 2008, All Rights Reserved.
