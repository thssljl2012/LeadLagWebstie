Two brothers are to appear in court accused of murdering a banker who died after confronting a gang in Norwich city centre.Tom and Ben Cowles, aged 21 and 20, of Costessey, Norfolk, are charged with murdering 45-year-old Frank McGarahan.The chief operating officer of Barclays Wealth was pronounced dead on Monday following an incident in Norwich in the early hours ofSunday.A Norfolk Police spokeswoman said Ben Cowles has also charged with three counts of assault - one against a Lithuanian man and other offences against Mr McGarahan's brother and cousin.The pair were remanded in custody to appear at Norwich Magistrates' Court, the spokeswoman said.Sources close to the investigation confirmed the charged men were brothers.Father-of-two Mr McGarahan, of Much Hadham, Herts, had been visiting Norwich ahead of a family christening when he was fatally injured.Post-mortem tests revealed he died as a result of a 'bleed to the brain', police said.Detectives on Thursday charged 21-year-old Daniel Moy, of Costessey, with assaulting Mr McGarahan's cousin.Police said the charge was not 'directly associated' with Mr McGarahan's death.
