Two brothers have appeared in court accused of murdering a banker who died after confronting a gang in Norwich city centre.Tom and Ben Cowles, aged 21 and 20, of Costessey, Norwich, have been charged with killing 45-year-old father of two Frank McGarahan, of Much Hadham, Hertfordshire.The brothers appeared at Norwich Magistrates' Court and were remanded in custody until October 17.Their next appearance will be before a judge at Norwich Crown Court.The chief operating officer of Barclays Wealth died in hospital on Monday after suffering head injuries in an incident in Norwich at about 3am on Sunday.Neither defendant entered a plea to the charges and both spoke only to confirm their personal details during a brief hearing.Ben Cowles is also accused of assaulting Mr McGarahan's brother Kevin, Mr McGarahan's cousin, Sean Ryan, and a third man Robertas Sinkevicius.He entered no plea to any of those charges.A third man, Daniel Moy, also from Costessey, has also been charged with assaulting Mr Ryan and is due before magistrates at a later date.
