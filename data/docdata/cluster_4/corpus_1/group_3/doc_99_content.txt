A Norwich carpenter has been ordered to carry out 100 hours of unpaid work after admitting assaulting the cousin of a wealthy banker, who died after an early hours incident in the city centre.Daniel Moy, 21, punched Frank McGarahan's cousin Sean Ryan during an incident in Norwich at about 3am on September 28, Norwich magistrates heard.Moy was told to pay Mr Ryan 50 compensation and to pay costs of 60.Prosecutor Mark Davis told Norwich Magistrates' Court that Moy had been in central Norwich looking for a taxi at about 3am on September 28 after a night out with friends.He said a group of people were approached by Mr Ryan and two other men in their 40s.Moy had punched Mr Ryan once in the face then run away, and the incident was captured on closed circuit television.Prosecutors stressed that Moy had not been involved in any incident relating to Mr McGarahan.Mr McGarahan died in hospital on September 29 after being involved in an incident in central Norwich in the early hours of September 28.Two brothers - Tom Cowles, 21, and Ben Cowles, 20, both of Norwich - have been charged with his murder in separate legal proceedings.Mr McGarahan, of Much Hadham, Hertfordshire, was chief operating officer of Barclays Wealth - a sector of the bank which manages the assets of some of the world's richest people.Copyright (c) Press Association Ltd. 2008, All Rights Reserved.
