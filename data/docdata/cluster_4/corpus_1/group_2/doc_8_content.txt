Businesses have been advised to vary their cash delivery routines after a man was robbed on his way to a bank in Lydney, Gloucestershire. The 39-year-old was set upon at the rear entrance to Lloyds bank carrying 'a large quantity of cash'.Two men grabbed him and wrestled the bag from his grasp before fleeing.Both thieves are described as white men in their early 20s and 5ft 10. They were wearing baseball caps.Detective Sergeant Richard Pegler of Gloucestershire Police said: 'The victim was left very shaken as a result of the incident but fortunately was not injured.'This type of attack is unheard of in the area, further analysis of the town's CCTV and a forensic examination of recovered items will be carried out.'It is important that local businesses vary their banking routine and take additional care when delivering their money.'Witnesses are asked to call police on 0845 090 1234 or Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.Copyright (c) Press Association Ltd. 2008, All Rights Reserved.
