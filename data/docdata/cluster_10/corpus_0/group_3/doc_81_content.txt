The wave of layoffs sweeping the banking sector has arrived at Goldman Sachs Group Inc., which may lay off roughly 3,200 employees.  Published reports have the investment bank slashing 10% of its 32,500-strong work force, according to sources. The layoffs come on top of an estimated 125,000 in eliminated positions on Wall Street the this year alone, according to Bloomberg data. The glum development from the top-tier bank follows 24,000 lost jobs at Citigroup Inc. and 14,000 from Lehman Brothers Holdings Inc., with thousands more in expected layoffs from the merger of Merrill Lynch &amp; Co. and Bank of America Corp. - George White   See Bloomberg storySee Dealwatch on Wall Street layoffs  See Dealscape posts on layoffs
