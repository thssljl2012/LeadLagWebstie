Commerce, Ga-based Freedom Bank of Georgia became the 17th bank failure of 2009 late last night.   According to a statement of the FDIC, Freedom Bank had total assets of roughly $173 million and total deposits of $161 million as of March 4. Lavonia-based Northeast Georgia Bank has agreed to assume all of the banks deposits and will purchase approximately $167 million in assets at a discount of $13.65 million. The FDIC will retain the remaining assets for later disposition.   The FDIC estimated the cost of Freedom Banks failure to its deposit insurance fund will be roughly $36 million.
