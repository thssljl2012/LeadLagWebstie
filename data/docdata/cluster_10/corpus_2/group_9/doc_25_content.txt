U.S. regulators and the nation's largest banks have nearly completed "stress tests" to determine how well the lenders would fare if the recession proves to be deeper and longer than expected.     Citigroup Inc, Wells Fargo &amp; Co, Bank of America MetLife Inc and GMAC are among the 19 lenders under scrutiny who may get government help to strengthen their balance sheets following the May 4 announcement of the results.     On Friday, officials will release a document explaining the underlying stress testing assumptions and how to interpret the results. Regulators are expected to take care that test results do not put weaker banks into a tailspin, but may set some tough requirements on how the banks should rebuild.
