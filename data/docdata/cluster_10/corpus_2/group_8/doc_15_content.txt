      FDIC warns US bank deposit insurance fund could tank             FDIC: Bank deposit insurance fund ailing        US warning banks that fund could go broke this year as bank failures mount.              The US government is warning banks that its deposit insurance fund could go broke this year as bank failures mount.      The head of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, Sheila Bair, in a letter to bank chief executives dated March 2, defended the FDIC's plan to raise fees on banks and assess an emergency fee to shore up the fund and maintain investor confidence.
