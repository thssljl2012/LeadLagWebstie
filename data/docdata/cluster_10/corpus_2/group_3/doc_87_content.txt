Stressed banks to forgive defaulter      October 6, 2008   Page 1 of 2       A deal with the pariah, Argentina, now seems the lesser evil, writes Richard Reynolds in Buenos Aires      IT IS a happy financial marriage, but one made in hell. Three banks made utterly desperate by the credit crisis meet an equally desperate government and unexpectedly hatch a plan to solve one of the world&amp;#039;s worst and oldest debt crises.      It involves Argentina, which provoked the world&amp;#039;s biggest banking default in 2005, and three of the world&amp;#039;s biggest banks: Citigroup, Deutche Bank and Barclays. All three are desperate to revive their ravaged balance sheets.      And Argentina itself is in only slightly better shape. It faces an enormous bill of $US14 billion ($18 billion) next year just to service its still outstanding debt - a sum it could meet, but only with some effort and luck.      Also, its 2005 default means it has been frozen out of international capital markets, with multibillion-dollar lawsuits hanging over its head in the US and Europe.
