WASHINGTON, March 26 (Reuters) - The American Bankers Association, a top U.S. bank industry group, said in a statement on Thursday:   * says has 'serious concerns' about FDIC getting resolution authority over large non-bank financial firms   * says dangerous to risk confusing the mission of FDIC   * says FDIC should consult on systemic resolutions, actual resolution process should be located elsewhere   * says 'not at all clear' how FDIC's role with non-banking firms would be funded   * says nothing should be done to detract from Fed's monetary policy responsibilities
