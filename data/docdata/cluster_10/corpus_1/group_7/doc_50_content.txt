In case of a deeper recession, the Obama government has decided banks will receive requests for additional information about their assets.  In the coming weeks, financial regulators will launch a campaign to determine which of the largest US banks should get bigger shares of cash from the US bank bailout.  While the largest US banks appear to be well capitalized for the moment, the Obama administration wants to ensure that they are robust enough to withstand a more severe economic downturn.  The administration has tried to ease market fears that the government was poised to nationalize some large banks.
