ANZ Banking Group will replace Wachovia Corporation on the Dow Jones Indexes' Banks Titans 30 Index from tomorrow.The index is comprised of the world's biggest and best known financial services companies.Wachovia held the No. 21 spot on the index ahead of Commonwealth Bank of Australia at No. 23 and National Australia Bank at No. 27.On Monday, Citigroup reached an agreement to purchase Wachovia's troubled banking operations for $US2.16 billion or $US1 per share.Wachovia was the US's No. 1 provider of option adjustable rate mortgages with $US122 billion ($153 billion) worth of them.The North Carolina lender collapsed from its exposure to overdue mortgages.ANZ shares were up 1.7% at $19.06 in late trade.
