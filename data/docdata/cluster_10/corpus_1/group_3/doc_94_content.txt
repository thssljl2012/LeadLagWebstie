After selling off $1.75bn (970m) in assets to Barclays, bankrupt giant Lehman Brothers is to sell much of its money management business to Bain Capital and Hellman &amp;amp; Friedman for about $2.15bn (1.19bn) in cash, reports The American Lawyer. Weil Gotshal &amp;amp; Manges, Lehmans bankruptcy counsel, is handling the deal on Lehmans end.
