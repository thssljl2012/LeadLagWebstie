ALMATY, Kazakhstan -(Dow Jones)- Kazakh state oil and gas company KazMunaiGas, or KMG, said Thursday it had raised a $2.5 billion syndicated loan to refinance a $3.1 billion bridge loan.KMG said in a statement that the loan carried a three-year maturity and an 155 basis points above the London Interbank Offered Rate. The company has an option to extend the loan for one more year, KMG said.The $3.1 billion bridge loan had been used to finance the acquisition of a 75% stake in Rompetrol Group, KMG said.ABN AMRO, The Bank of Tokyo - Mitsubishi UFJ, Barclays Capital, Credit Suisse Group, Deutsche Bank AG, Societe Generale SA, Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation, and WestLB AG organized the loan, KMG said.Company Web site: www.kmg.kz-By Kadyr Toktogulov, Dow Jones Newswires; +7 701 726 4327, kadyr.toktogulov@ dowjones.comClick here to go to Dow Jones NewsPlus, a web front page of today's most important business and market news, analysis and commentary: http:// www.djnewsplus.com/al?rnd=k7jH2fAtuRIiFWKroO9thw%3D%3D.
