The National Australia Bank has denied reports it will be forced to write-down more bad debts. Analyst reports have suggested the bank will have to make provisions for losses on $4.5 billion of commercial debt. Earlier this year, NAB announced it would take a hit of more than $1 billion on losses from subprime-related investments. About 1pm AEST, NAB shares were down almost 1 per cent to $23.68.
