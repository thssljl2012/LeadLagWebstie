The Australian market followed Monday's 4.1 per cent gain with a 0.8 per cent fall, refusing to follow Wall Street's lead.  Time differences were in some degree responsible. Australian investors had already responded to the US infrastructure spending package, which has an estimated value of between $US500 billion and $US700 billion. And, with Westpac announcing it would conduct a $2.5 billion capital raising, they were more concerned about capital adequacy and potential capital raisings at Australia's other big banks.
