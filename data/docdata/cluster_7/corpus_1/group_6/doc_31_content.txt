Treasurys were little changed Thursday after the Labor Department said initial claims for unemployment benefits rose and wholesales prices declined.Ten-year notes yields , which move in the opposite direction as prices, rose 1 basis point, or 0.01%, to 2.21%.Two-year notes yielded 0.73%.Applications for state unemployment benefits rose 54,000 to a seasonally adjusted 524,000 in the week ending Jan. 10. The four-week average of continuing claims rose 27,500 to 4.5 million - the highest level since December 1982. See jobless claims story.Separately, the department said producer prices fell 1.9% last month, less than economists surveyed by MarketWatch had predicted.'This morning's round of data tells a familiar story, falling energy prices hinting of an ongoing deflationary trend, while the increase in initial unemployment claims suggests a weakening labor market,' said strategists at RBS Greenwich Capital. 'A great deal of negative economic data is currently priced into the Treasury market.'Also, the New York Federal Reserve Bank's manufacturing survey said activity in the New York area this month was not as weak as in December. Core prices rose.Also, the European Central Bank lowered its benchmark interest rate to 2%, as expected.Still to come, the Philadelphia Fed branch releases its report on manufacturing in the region at 10 a.m. Eastern, and three Fed officials have scheduled speeches.Yields were lower earlier amid reports that Bank of America may need more government aid.
