Coral Gables-based Mercantil Commercebank saw improvement in its earnings in the third quarter over the second quarter, but a nearly 50 percent drop from the prior-year period.The bank said it earned $5.2 million in the third quarter. Thats up from $4.4 million in the second quarter, but short of its $11.1 million profit in the third quarter of 2007.The bank said a higher reserve for loan loss made profit slide from a year ago.Mercantil Commercebank is the ninth-largest bank operating in South Florida, with $4.1 billion in deposits in 14 branches as of June 30, according to Federal Deposit Insurance Corp. data. The banks nationwide deposits increased to $4.5 billion as of Sept. 30, up from $4.4 billion on June 30. Its assets increased by $230 million to reach $6.27 billion.As previously announced, Mercantil Commercebank received a $70 million capital infusion from its parent company of Venezuelas Mercantil Servicios Financieros during the third quarter. That boosted its capital to $543.9 million.With that extra capital, the bank plans to open one branch in Broward County and two more in Palm Beach County in the next 12 months. We are monitoring the financial markets and feel confident in our ability to continue our growth based on sound business strategies, Mercantil Commercebank President and CEO J. Guillermo Villar said in a news release. Mercantil Commercebank did not release data on its noncurrent loans or its level of loan loss reserves in the third quarter. The bank saw a spike in problem loans in the second quarter.
