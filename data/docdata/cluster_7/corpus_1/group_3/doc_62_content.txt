Westpac has become the third major bank to announce it is opting into the Government's deposit guarantee scheme.Acting CEO Bruce McLachlan says Westpac will talk with the Finance Minister and Reserve Bank to work through the details. He says he supports the measure and understands the pressure for the government to act, given similar moves around the world.ASB and Kiwibank have also announced they will sign up to the scheme. Both agree the Government guarantee gives customers an extra level of certainty.The Government's move is intended to stop panic withdrawals in response to the international financial crisis. The guarantee will cover savings in banks, building societies, credit unions, finance companies and cash portfolio investment schemes.
