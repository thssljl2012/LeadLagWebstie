Article from: AAP  Font size: Decrease Increase  : Email  : Print  HOMEOWNERS are today keenly awaiting news from their banks after the Reserve Bank of Australia cut the official cash rate by three-quarters of a percentage point.  So far, only the Commonwealth Bank has said it will pass on any of the cut to homeowners, announcing it would cut its variable home loan interest rates by 58 basis points.  The central bank surprised economists by cutting its cash rate by a larger-than-expected 75 basis points yesterday in an attempt to further stimulate the faltering economy.  Further signs of how the economy is travelling will come today with the release of international trade and building approval figures for September.
