LONDON, Jan 12 (Reuters) - The Bank of England cut interest rates by 50    basis points to a record low 1.5 percent on Jan 8. As a result, the average rate    set by central banks of the Group of Seven nations fell to 1.15 percent from    1.25 percent.    The average rate set by 11 leading central banks -- the five that decide    monetary policy for the G7 nations plus six other major banks -- fell to 2.20    percent from 2.25 percent.
