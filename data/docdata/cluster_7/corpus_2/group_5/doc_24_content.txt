LONDON, Dec 22 (Reuters) - China's central bank cut banks' benchmark lending and deposit    rates by 0.27 percentage points on Monday, the fifth cut since mid-September.    The Bank of Japan cut its key policy rate to 0.10 percent on Dec. 19 from 0.30 percent. As    a result, the average rate set by central banks of the Group of Seven nations fell to 1.25    percent from 1.29 percent.    The Danish central bank cut its main rate by 50 basis points on Dec. 19 to 3.75 percent.    As a result of this and the BOJ cut the average rate set by 11 leading central banks -- the    five that decide monetary policy for the G7 nations plus six other major banks -- fell to 2.25    percent from 2.31 percent.
