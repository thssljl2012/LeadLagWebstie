* Banks should 'get back to banking' (Adds Buffett comment, Bank of America CEO) NEW YORK, March 9 (Reuters) - Warren Buffett said on Monday the U.S. economy had 'fallen off a cliff' but would eventually recover, although a rebound could kindle inflation worse than that experienced in the late 1970s.  Speaking on CNBC television, the 78-year-old billionaire said the country is experiencing a 'close to the worst-case' scenario of falling business activity and rising unemployment, causing consumer confidence and spending to tumble.  Buffett called on Democratic and Republican policymakers to set aside partisan differences and unite under the leadership of President Barack Obama to wage an 'economic war' that will fix the economy and restore confidence in banking.  He urged policymakers and regulators to communicate their efforts better to the public, though he stopped short of major, specific policy recommendations. 'People are confused and scared,' he said.
