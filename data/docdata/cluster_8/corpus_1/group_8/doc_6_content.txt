An outraged Congress threatened Tuesday to impose new taxes to recoup at least some of the $165 million in bonuses that insurance giant AIG awarded to executives and traders after getting billions in federal bailout money. 'The people who got those bonuses won't be able to keep all that money,' Majority Leader Harry Reid declared on the Senate floor.   Senate Finance Committee Chairman Max Baucus suggested taxing away the bonuses. 'What is the highest excise tax we can impose that will stand up in court?' Baucus asked. 'Let's find out what it is.'
