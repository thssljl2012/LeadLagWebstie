INDIANAPOLIS  The FBI said an Indianapolis man tried to extort more than $200,000 from insurance giant AIG after stealing a computer server that contained personal and health care information for more than 900,000 people.Kevin Michael Stewart, 27, was in federal custody Wednesday night at the Marion County Jail, said Special Agent Wendy Osborne, an FBI spokeswoman.Stewart was being held without bond pending a detention hearing.Stewart was charged under a new federal law that bans extorting money by threatening to disclose stolen confidential information, said Assistant U.S. Attorney Steven DeBrota. He also was charged with making threatening communications.If convicted, Stewart could face up to seven years in prison, DeBrota said.
