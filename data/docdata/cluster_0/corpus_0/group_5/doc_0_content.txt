Asian markets plunged Friday after a proposed U.S. government rescue of Detroit's ailing automakers collapsed in the Senate, intensifying fears about a protracted global slump.More dire economic data from the U.S. _ a vital export market _ and news of deep job cuts at Bank of America also added to the gloom.Japan's Nikkei 225 stock average tumbled 484.68 points, or 5.6 percent, to 8,235.87, and Hong Kong's Hang Seng index slid 5.9 percent to 14,692.79. South Korea's Kospi fell 4.4 percent.
