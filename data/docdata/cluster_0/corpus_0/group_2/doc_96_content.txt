Gold Holds Steady as US Nationalizes AIG; Counterparty Risks in Exchange-Traded Notes "Now Clear" to Investors THE PRICE OF PHYSICAL GOLD stuck to non-Dollar currencies early Wednesday, holding in the middle of this week's range as the US nationalization of AIG  the world's largest insurance group  whipped world stock markets. The Japanese Nikkei added 1.2% after the Federal Reserve seized four-fifths of the failed insurance giant for $85 billion, but Shanghai shares closed 3.6% lower.
