Oil prices slid further Tuesday, dropping to as low as $118 a barrel on widening expectations that the slumping U.S. economy will keep eroding consumer demand for gasoline and other petroleum products.    The U.S. dollar&amp;#039;s gains against the euro also contributed to oil&amp;#039;s decline. Light, sweet crude for September delivery was down $1.51 at $119.90 a barrel in premarket trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange. Earlier, it dropped to $118, before regaining some ground.    Crude has now fallen more than $25 since reaching a trading high of $147.27 on July 11. The growing belief that soaring prices have stifled demand has halted what appeared to be a relentless march higher.    &amp;quot;The main factor weighing on oil prices is worries about oil consumption being weakened, especially in the U.S.,&amp;quot; said David Moore, a commodity strategist with Commonwealth Bank of Australia in Sydney.
