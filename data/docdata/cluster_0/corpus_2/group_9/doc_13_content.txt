Asian Stocks Advance as Financial Concern Eases; Komatsu Gains  Share   Email   Print   A A A    By Jonathan Burgos and Masaki Kondo    April 22 (Bloomberg) -- Asian stocks rose, led by financial companies and automakers, after Treasury Secretary Timothy Geithner said most U.S. banks have enough capital, sparking optimism the global financial crisis is abating.    Nomura Holdings Inc., Japans largest brokerage, climbed 3.1 percent in Tokyo. Honda Motor Co., Japans second-biggest automaker, gained 1.7 percent as the Nikkei newspaper said the company will beat its earnings forecast. Komatsu Ltd., the worlds No. 2 maker of earthmoving equipment, jumped 2.1 percent after rival Caterpillar Inc.s profit beat analyst estimates.    Investors appetite for stocks has somewhat recovered, Hiroichi Nishi, general manager at Nikko Cordial Securities Co., said in an interview with Bloomberg Television. Wed like to see more positive news to bring investors back.    The MSCI Asia Pacific Index gained 0.4 percent to 88.67 at 9:53 a.m. in Tokyo. The gauge has climbed 26 percent from a five-year low reached on March 9 amid speculation government measures will succeed in easing the global recession.    Japans Nikkei 225 Stock Average gained 0.7 percent to 8,773.57. South Koreas Kospi index rose 0.7 percent and Australias S&amp;P/ASX 200 Index added 0.3 percent. All markets open for trading advanced.    Elpida Memory Inc., Japans biggest maker of computer- memory chips, surged 16 percent after saying it plans to raise prices. Woodside Petroleum Ltd., Australias second-largest oil producer, climbed 1.4 percent after crude-oil prices rose.    Futures on the Standard &amp; Poors 500 Index lost 0.5 percent. The gauge climbed 2.1 percent in New York yesterday after Treasury Secretary Geithner said the vast majority of U.S. banks have more capital than they need to be considered well capitalized. The Federal Reserve is overseeing assessments of the health of the 19 biggest U.S. banks, with results to be released on May 4.    Honda Earnings    Nomura climbed 3.1 percent to 596 yen. Westpac Banking Corp., Australias largest bank by market value, added 0.7 percent to A$19.88.    Honda gained 1.7 percent to 2,775 yen in Tokyo. The company may post an 84 percent decline in operating profit to 150 billion yen ($1.52 billion) for the year ended March 31, the Nikkei newspaper reported today. That would be higher than the automakers forecast by 10 billion yen, the newspaper said.    Honda President Takeo Fukui said there are signs this month that the U.S. market has bottomed, Reuters reported yesterday. Fukui spoke at a news conference in Kyoto, the news wire said.    Komatsu, Elpida    Komatsu climbed 2.1 percent to 1,243 yen. Caterpillar, the worlds biggest maker of construction machinery, posted first- quarter profit of 39 cents a share, surpassing analysts average estimate of 5 cents a share. Chief Executive Officer Jim Owens said Chinas stimulus spending may benefit the company this year and set the stage for growth in 2010.    Elpida surged 16 percent to 1,260 yen. The company plans to raise prices by as much as 50 percent next month after industry- wide production cuts eased the glut that drove chipmakers to record losses, Elpida President Yukio Sakamoto said in an interview yesterday.    Woodside Petroleum rose 1.4 percent to A$37.49 in Sydney. Inpex Corp., Japans largest oil explorer, added 1.9 percent to 641,000 yen. Crude oil for June delivery gained as much as 0.8 percent to $48.93 in after-hours trading.
