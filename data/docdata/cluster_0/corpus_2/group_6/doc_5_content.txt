HONG KONG, Jan 8 (Reuters) - Shares of Industrial and Commercial Bank of China (ICBC), the country's biggest bank, fell more than 6.3 percent amid worries investors Goldman Sachs, Allianz and American Express will trim their holdings when lock-up agreements lapse this year.    ICBC shares fell to as low as HK$3.7 late on Thursday morning with 281 million shares worth HK$1.06 billion changing hands.    Shares in China's biggest banks skidded further on Thursday on worries that big investors will offload more shares after Bank of America and Hong Kong tycoon Li Ka-shing dumped a combined $3.3 billion worth of shares in mainland lenders.    Bank of China shares were down 6.5 percent, while China Construction Bank was off by 3.2 percent.
