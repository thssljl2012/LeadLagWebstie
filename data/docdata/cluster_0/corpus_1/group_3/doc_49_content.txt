 MELBOURNE, Oct 7 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 1.7 percent on Tuesday, trimming early losses as the top miners and banks bounced off their lows ahead of an expected 50 basis point rate cut by the Reserve Bank of Australia. By 0150 GMT, Australia's benchmark /ASX 200 index was down 77.3 points at 4,463.1, after touching a new three-year low of 4,391.9, adding to a 3.3 percent decline on Monday. New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index fell 2.4 percent to 2,974.1. The index earlier sank to 2,962.4, its lowest since May 2005.
