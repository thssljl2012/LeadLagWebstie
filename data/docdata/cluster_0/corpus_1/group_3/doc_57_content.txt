The Australian share market is down in mid-afternoon trade, but off its earlier lows.The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index was down 0.5%, or 19 points, to 3982.1. The broader All Ordinaries index was down 0.3%, or 11.4 points, to 3945.9.'They have had a pretty good run, especially yesterday, so it could be a bit of profit taking coming in,'' said Eric Betts, equities strategist at Nomura Australia.'Given the times we are in, and with the weekend coming up, people probably don't want to be too long. Who knows what the weekend will bring ... no one is sticking their necks out at this stage.''Mining shares retreated after a key commodities index, the Reuters/Jefferies CRB gauge, sank 2.8% overnight.BHP Billitonshares were down $1.03, or 3.6%, to $27.57, Rio Tinto was down $1.17, or 1.5%, to $75.15 and Fortescue Metals was down 16 cents, or 5.1%, to $2.98.The banks were lower, although well up from their early lows. The Commonwealth Bank was down 9 cents, or 0.2%, to $40.10, ANZ was down 49 cents, or 2.8%, to $17.07, NAB was down 40 cents, or 1.6%, to $23.79, Westpac was down 1 cent to $20.29, but St George bucked the trend, rising 41 cents, or 1.5%, to $27.41.Macquarie Groupwas up 17 cents, or 0.6%, to $29.19, after earlier trading higher.Among the gold miners, Lihir Gold was down 11.5 cents, or 5.6%, to $1.93.5 despite the price of the metal rising overnight. Newcrest went the other way, rising 25 cents, or 1.2%, to $20.77.Cameron Securities client advisor Adrian Leppinus said market mood was flat today.'The metals were all down last night and that's where the market here is not really taking the late lead on Wall Street,'' Mr Leppinus said.'Again, (what we're seeing is) a bit of profit taking in the gold stocks after yesterday's big run.'Resources have come off the boil. I think that's the mood ...'It's a pretty flat day.'But Fridays are. No one wants to be long going into the weekend.''Mr Leppinus said the energy sector was also likely to be weaker following the dip in oil prices.Overnight, New York's main contract, light sweet crude for December delivery fell $US1.54 to close at $US65.96 per barrel.Energy stocks were mixed in noon trading. Woodside Petroleum was down $1.28, or 3%, to $41.88 and Santos was down 56 cents, or 4%, to $13.39 after opening higher.The retailers were mixed, with Wesfarmers up 70 cents, or 3.4%, to $21.49, Woolworths was up 31 cents, or 1.1%, to $27.80, but Harvey Norman was down 9 cents, or 3.4%, to $2.56.Shopping centre operator Westfield was up 79 cents, or 5.3%, to $15.64.The Australian dollar was trading at 67.41 US cents, 66.48 yen and 52.45 euro cents.
