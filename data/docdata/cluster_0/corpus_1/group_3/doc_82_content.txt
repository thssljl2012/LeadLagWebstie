THE London stock market dived by more than 6 per cent to hit a four-year low today, as banking stocks were battered by heightened concerns over the global financial crisis, dealers said.At 1000 GMT (2100 AEDT), the British capital's FTSE 100 index of leading shares showed a loss of 6.21 per cent at 4,670.89 points - the lowest point since November 1, 2004.Banking sector shares took a major hammering, with HBOS down 15.41 per cent, Royal Bank of Scotland shedding 13.75 per cent and Barclays losing 10.46 per cent.
