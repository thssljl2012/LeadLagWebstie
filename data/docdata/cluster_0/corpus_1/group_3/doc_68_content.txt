I think that's the mood.'It's a pretty flat day.'But Fridays are (like that). Noone wants to be long going into the weekend.'Mr Leppinus said the energy sector also was likely to be weaker following the dip in oil prices.Overnight, New York's main contract, light sweet crude for December delivery fell $US1.54 to close at $US65.96 per barrel.Locally, energy stocks were mixed.Woodside Petroleum fell $1.22, or 2.83 per cent, to $41.94, Oil Search gained two cents, or 0.47 per cent, to $4.32 and Santos shed 33 cents, or 2.37 per cent, to $13.62.At 10.39am (AEDT), spot gold was trading in Sydney at $US738.25 an ounce, down $US30.35 on yesterday's close of $US768.60.Among local gold stocks, Newcrest Mining was down 41 cents, or two per cent, to $20.11, Newmont Mining gained 17 cents, or 4.42 per cent, to $4.02 and Lihir Gold fell 8.5 cents, or 4.15 per cent, to $1.965.
