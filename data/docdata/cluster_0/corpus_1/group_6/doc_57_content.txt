NEW YORK (Reuters) - Stocks slipped at the open on Thursday as worries stemming from news of a push by Bank of America (NYSE:BAC - News) for more government aid more than offset reassuring quarterly results from JPMorgan Chase (NYSE:JPM - News).  * Shares of Bank of America dropped more than 15 percent to $8.67, but shares of JPMorgan climbed 3.2 percent to $26.75.  * Shares of Apple Inc (NasdaqGS:AAPL - News) fell on Nasdaq after the iPod and iPhone maker said Chief Executive Steve Jobs had decided to take a leave of absence due to health issues. The stock was off 3.6 percent to $82.20.  The Dow Jones industrial average (DJI:^DJI - News) was down 54.00 points, or 0.66 percent, at 8,155.06. The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 Index (^SPX - News) was down 5.48 points, or 0.65 percent, at 837.14. The Nasdaq Composite Index (Nasdaq:^IXIC - News) was down 5.13 points, or 0.34 percent, at 1,484.51.  (Editing by James Dalgleish)
