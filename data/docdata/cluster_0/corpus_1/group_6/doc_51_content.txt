 (RTTNews) - The New Zealand stock market opened slightly weaker on Friday, following a see-saw session on Wall Street overnight with the major U.S. averages ending the session mixed.  The benchmark NZX 50 index was down 5.82 points or 0.21% to 2,750.82 shortly after the market opened for the day, while the broader NZX All Capital Index lost 6.67 points or 0.24% to 2,761.20.  The New Zealand stock market closed lower on Thursday for the second straight session, following sharp losses overnight on Wall Street and declines in commodities prices.  The benchmark NZX50 index declined 7.59 points, or 0.27% to finish at 2,756.65 and the broader NZX All Capital Index gave away 1.37 points, or 0.05% to close at 2,767.88.  The New Zealand Stock Exchange posted trading volume for the session at 29.6 million shares carrying a total value of NZ$56.1 million.  Overseas, the U.S. stocks ended Thursday's trading on opposite sides of the unchanged line, as investors responded to mixed economic and corporate news.  The major U.S. averages eventually ended the session mixed, with the Dow stuck in negative territory. The Dow closed down 27.24 points or 0.3 percent at 8,742.46, while the NASDAQ closed up 17.95 points or 1.1 percent at 1,617.01 and the S&amp;amp;P 500 closed up 3.08 points or 0.3 percent at 909.73.  Crude oil fell again on the New York Mercantile Exchange on Thursday and reached its lowest mark of the new year. Concerns over energy demand continued amid the release of another weak jobs report. Oil added to Wednesday's sharp inventory-based plunge.  Light sweet crude for February delivery finished at $41.70, down 93 cents on the session. Oil fell as low as $40.54 per barrel, its lowest mark since New Year's Eve when prices fell below $37.  On the currency front, the New Zealand dollar opened firmer against most currencies, and was buying US59.35c at 8am on Friday, compared to US58.53c at 5pm on Thursday.  In Friday's early trading on the New Zealand Stock Exchange, the country's top ranked share Telecom dropped 0.42%, as second ranked Contact Energy remained unchanged and Fletcher Building, the third best stock, lost 1.19%.  In the retail sector Hallenstein Glasson, Warehouse, Pumpkin Patch and jewelry retailer Michael Hill International remained unchanged.  In the energy sector Vector and TrustPower remained unchanged in the early trading on Friday.  Among the dual listed issues, AMP, Australia and NZ Banking Corp, APN News &amp;amp; Media, Lion Nathan and Telstra remained unchanged, as Westpac Bank added 0.30%.
