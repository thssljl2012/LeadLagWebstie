THE share market was in the red at noon with lower base metals prices overnight weighing on mining stocks and as profit takers capitalised on solid gains earlier in the week.  At 12.01pm (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was down 39.7 points, or 1.13 per cent, at 3486.5, while the broader All Ordinaries index had lost 35.7 points, or 1.03 per cent, to 3425.6.  IG Markets research analyst Ben Potter said the local market had not been helped by weak earnings reports and record unemployment claims from the US overnight. 'We saw big jumps in materials and financials stocks this week and we had to see profit-taking coming in sooner rather than later,'' Mr Potter said. 'That's what we saw overseas last night, triggered by news of the worsening economic situation in the US. 'It's pretty much expected. 'And base metals fell 3 to 4 per cent overnight across the board.''  Big miners were all down.   Rio Tinto had dropped $1.53 to $39.18, while rival BHP Billiton had shed 2.12 per cent to $30.00.  Financial stocks were in negative territory.   Commonwealth Bank shed 3.01 per cent to $26.09, NAB retreated 1.23 per cent to $18.45, ANZ slid 2.86 per cent to $12.88 and Westpac fell 1.78 per cent to $15.42.   Energy stocks were surprisingly stronger.  Woodside Petroleum advanced 80 cents to $35.39, Santos gained 1.77 per cent, to $14.40 and Oil Search added 2.33 per cent, to $4.40.  Gold miners also defied the negative market trend.  Newcrest rose 3.22 per cent to $31.38, Newmont found 6.22 per cent to $6.15, Lihir Gold appreciated 4.39 per cent to $3.09 and Sino Gold surged 10.45 per cent, to $5.18.  The spot price of gold in Sydney was $US902.50 per fine ounce at 1210 AEDT, up $US18.95 on Thursday's local close of $US883.55.  Among key retail stocks, Woolworths gained 17 cents to $27.37, while rival and Coles owner Wesfarmers had dipped 3.54 per cent to $15.26.   Media stocks were mixed.   Fairfax inched 1 cent higher to $1.38 at 12.14pm, Consolidated Media was up 3 cents to $1.89 and News Corporation (the parent company of the publisher of news.com.au) had fallen 2.29 per cent, to $11.96.   Making headlines, engineering services provider Coffey International says it expects its best ever half year trading result, with earnings before interest and tax for the first half of fiscal 2009 expected to be between $29 million and $31 million.  Its shares had surged 18 cents, or 14.75 per cent, to $1.40 at 12.11pm.
