U.S. markets ended a see-saw day in positive territory Friday, in spite of losses at two of the largest U.S. banks.  Government intervention for Bank of America worth $138 billion announced Thursday did not pull the firm out of a skid. Bank of America shares fell 13.7 percent Friday, while Citigroup Inc. shares fell 8.62 percent.  At the close, the Dow Jones industrial average was up 68.73 points, or 0.84 percent, to 8,281.722. The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 rose 0.76 percent, 6.38 points, to 850.12. The Nasdaq composite index gained 68.73 points, 1.16 percent, to 1,529.33.  On the New York Stock Exchange, 2,012 stocks advanced and 1,036 declined on a volume of 7.9 billion shares traded.  The benchmark 10-year U.S. Treasury bond fell 1 9/32 to yield 2.337 percent.  The euro rose to $1.3289, compared to Thursday's $1.3154. Against the Japanese yen, the dollar rose to 90.59 yen, up from Thursday's 89.84 yen.  In Tokyo, the Nikkei average gained 206.84 points to 8,230.15, up 2.58 percent.  In London, the FTSE 100 index rose 0.63 percent, 25.95 points, to 4,147.06.
