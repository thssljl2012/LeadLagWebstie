Stocks on the move Real-time Equity news U.S. stock market report 1308 ET 29Jan2009-KBW downgrades six speciality finance companies ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Keefe, Bruyette &amp; Woods on Thursday downgraded six specialty finance companies, seeing an increased risk of dividend reductions or suspensions as the companies are forced to preserve capital in the wake of one of the worst economic crises since the Great Depression. Apollo Investment, Ares Capital and Pennant Park were all downgraded to market perform from outperfrom while American Capital , Allied Capital and MCG Capital were cut to underperform. KBW said that Allied, MCG and American Capital could face covenant issues in the futures, and that Apollo and Ares were most at risk of dividend cuts. Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 1225 ET 29Jan2009-Options fear gauge bounces to above 40 reading ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ The CBOE Volatility Index, the implied volatility measure of the S&amp;P 500 was again above a 40 reading at 42.25, up 6.56 percent, one day after briefly breaking below the 40 level. It closed at 39.66 on Wednesday, its first close below 40 since early January and the February, March and April VIX futures contracts all stayed above 40, at a premium to spot. In afternoon trade, the front-month February VIX futures traded at 43.60, March at 41.40 and April at 40.70. 'It appears that the VIX may be destined to remain in this higher range for quite some time. What this technically means is that people are simply willing to pay more for SPX options, especially the puts, said Chris McKhann, analyst at optionmonster.com in comments on the Web site. Reuters Messaging: doris.frankel.reuters.com@reuters.net 1201 ET 29Jan2009-Ford loss weighs on auto sector ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Shares of auto companies fell on Thursday after Ford Motor Co reported its biggest annual loss ever and said it would cut about 2,500 jobs. It also plans to draw down its remaining credit line after burning through $5.5 billion of cash in the quarter as the recession has weighed on auto sales. The stock slid 3 percent to $1.97 while the Dow Jones U.S. Automobile Index fell 2.5 percent to 34.55 and General Motors was down 1.8 percent to $3.36. Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 1150 ET 29Jan2009-Bank of America attracts options bulls and bears-analyst ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Shares of Bank of America dropped 6.22 percent to $6.93 near midday but the equity's options have attracted a contrarian bid as traders eyed its out-of-the-money call contracts for both February and March expiration, said Andrew Wilkinson, market analyst at Interactive Brokers Group. The February $9 and $10 calls are being bought as more than 32,000 contracts traded combined, said WhatsTrading.com analyst Frederic Ruffy. Elsewhere, the February $6 and $7.50 strike put options were also sought after, with investors paying premiums of 60 cents and $1.34 respectively, Wilkinson said. The stock's implied options volatility grew once again as investors digested the passage of a stimulus package raising the reading for the bank to 143 percent, he said. Reuters Messaging: doris.frankel.reuters.com@reuters.net 1128 ET 29Jan2009-Boeing falls, Russian airline cancels order ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Shares of Boeing Co tumbled on Thursday, dropping after Russian airline S7 canceled a contract to buy 15 Boeing 787 Dreamliners. It will lease the plans instead. 'S7 retains interest in using the Dreamliner and at the moment is looking into receiving the planes under a leasing scheme at an earlier date, for which it is in negotiations with several leasing companies,' the airline said. The planes were scheduled for delivery in 2014. Shares of Boeing, a Dow component, tumbled 4.8 percent to $41.18. Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 1113 ET 29Jan2009-Stocks down after earnings, dire economic data ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Major U.S. indexes extended their losses on Thursday, dropping after a batch of quarterly results, and some new economic data, showed how the economic recession was impacting consumer and companies across nearly all sectors. The Dow Jones industrial average fell 1.1 percent to 8,288.15 while the S&amp;P 500 slid 1.5 percent to 861.37 and the Nasdaq was down 1.5 percent to 1,534.74.
