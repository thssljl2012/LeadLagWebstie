THE share market was slightly higher at noon with mild gains in resources sector, despite a flat lead from Wall Street.  At 12.00pm (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index was 0.52 per cent higher, up 19.1 points to 3713.4.  The broader All Ordinaries index rose 16.2 points, or 0.44 per cent, to 3659.8. 'The US closed relatively flat and we had no surprises so that gave our markets a little confidence but I'm not sure if we're going to maintain it,'' said CMC Markets senior dealer Dominic Vaughan. 'The market is firmer at this stage but I expect a bit of profit taking later in the day. 'We're still trading thin and they're so real money into the market yet.''  Miner BHP Billiton, the country's largest company, rose 55 cents to $31.30 and Rio Tinto increased by 6 cents to $44.06.  In the banking sector, Westpac fell 8 cents to $16.49, NAB fell 3 cents to $20.83 and ANZ sank 6 cents to $15.16.  However, Commonwealth Bank was 3 cents higher at $28.59.  In company news, Macquarie Group dropped 4.15 per cent to $31.15 after announcing yesterday that it was expecting extremely challenging market conditions in the December quarter.  Light, sweet crude for February delivery fell 93 cents to $US41.70 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.  In the energy sector, Santos shares declined 4 cents to $14.59 and Woodside Petroleum lost 47 cents to $35.96. However, Oil Search gained 9 cents to $4.72.  At 12.20pm AEDT, the price of gold in Sydney was $US852.25 per fine ounce, up $US5.70 on Thursdays's close of $US846.55.  Gold stocks rose, with Lihir up 9.23 per cent to $2.69, Newmont 5.44 per cent higher at $5.23, and Newcrest up 6.1 per cent to $30.08.  Overnight, Wall Street shares shook off opening losses and ended mixed Thursday as investors looked past weak sales reports from the retail sector and other grim data.  At settlement, the Dow Jones dropped 27.24 points, or 0.31 per cent, to 8742.46. The Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index rose 3.08 points, or 0.34 per cent, to 909.73 points, while the Nasdaq was 17.95, or 1.12 per cent higher, at 1617.01.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index contract was five points lower at 3687 on a volume of 7286 deals.
