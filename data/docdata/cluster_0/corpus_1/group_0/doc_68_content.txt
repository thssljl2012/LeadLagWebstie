 HONG KONG (MarketWatch) -- Asian markets mostly declined Friday on the back of losses in financial stocks such as Sumitomo Trust &amp;amp; Banking Co. and Industrial Bank of Korea. Banking stocks also dropped in Sydney, narrowing the gains for a benchmark index, although energy producers advanced on a recovery in crude-oil prices. The Nikkei 225 Average lost 0.8% to 12,958.26 and the broader Topix index gave up 0.9% to 1,278.78. Australia's S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 climbed as high as 4,981.20 during the session, but came off that level. Recently, the index was little changed at 4,939.30. New Zealand's NZX 50 index fell 0.8% to 3,088.75 and South Korea's Kospi dropped 0.6% to 1,533.28, with both indexes reversing initial gains. Financial stocks in Tokyo retreated after advancing in the previous session, on persistent fears ahead of upcoming earnings reports from U.S. banks and brokerages.
