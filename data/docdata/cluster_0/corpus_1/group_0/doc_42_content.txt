 LONDON (MarketWatch) -- U.S. stock futures traded around weekly lows on Friday ahead of what may be flat durable-goods orders and declining new-home sales. S&amp;amp;P 500 futures fell 1.9 points to 1,251.90 while Nasdaq 100 futures rose three-quarters of a point to 1,820.25. Dow industrial futures fell 20 points. U.S. stocks dropped sharply on Thursday after Washington Mutual's losses and data showing existing-home sales falling to a 10-year low. The Dow Jones Industrial Average, the S&amp;amp;P 500 and the Nasdaq Composite each retreated around 2% during the session. Friday's session features releases on durable-goods orders, new-home sales and the final reading of University of Michigan consumer confidence. U.S. foreclosures in the second quarter more than doubled from the year-earlier quarter and rose nearly 14% from the previous quarter, RealtyTrac said Friday. The dollar dropped against major rivals, down 0.4% to 106.96 yen, and oil futures traded 60 cents higher to $126.09 a barrel. Gold futures rose $9.10 to $931.40 an ounce.
