 MELBOURNE -(Dow Jones)- Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Ltd. (ANZ.AU) Monday said cash earnings per share for the fiscal year ending Sep. 30 is expected to fall by 20%-25% on the previous year, with provisions in the second half likely to be around A$1.2 billion, up from A$980 million in the first half.It said credit impairment costs will remain high in the second half as a result of ongoing deterioration in global credit markets, a weak New Zealand economy and a softening Australian economy.Profit before provisions is expected to be up by around 8% in 2007-08 from the previous year, the bank said. 'ANZ's underlying business is continuing to deliver a solid performance, and we expect a cash profit of over $3 billion in 2008,' Chief Executive Mike Smith said in a statement.
