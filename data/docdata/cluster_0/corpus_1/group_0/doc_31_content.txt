 SYDNEY, July 15 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 0.4 percent to a near two-year low on Tuesday, as financial firms such as National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX: NAB.ax) came under fresh pressure on worries about further fallout from credit markets.However, firm oil and gold prices lent support to the resource sector, limiting losses. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index .AXJO fell 19.9 points to 4,901.1 by 0014 GMT, a level not seen since July 19, 2006. ($1=A$1.03) (Reporting by Geraldine Chua, Editing by Mark Bendeich)
