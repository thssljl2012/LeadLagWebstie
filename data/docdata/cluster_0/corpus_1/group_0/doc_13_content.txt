 About A$35 billion (NZ$45 billion) was wiped off the Australian share market this morning in the first 10 minutes of trading.The Australian stock market plummeted more than 3 per cent after National Australia Bank took an additional provision of A$830 million on its exposure to the US credit crisis. The bank's shares fell as much as 13 per cent in early trading, and it is on track to record its worst day of trading in seven years. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index fell as much as 3.3 per cent, or 167.2 points, to 4976.9, while the broader All Ordinaries dipped 139.4 points, or 2.69 per cent, to 5049.NAB dropped $3.25, or 10.59 per cent to $27.24, the Commonwealth Bank fell A$2.39, or 5.15 per cent to A$44.00, ANZ lost A$1.01, or 5.19 per cent, to A$18.44, and Westpac fell 82 cents, or 3.6 per cent, to A$21.98.
