 * What: Big Japanese banks' first-quarter earnings* When: Mizuho, Sumitomo Mitsui July 31, Mitsubishi UFJ Aug 5* Mitsubishi UFJ and Sumitomo Mitsui may post a 20 percent profit decline, and Mizuho a 7 percent fallTOKYO, July 29 (Reuters) - Japan's big banks are set to post weaker first-quarter earnings as a sputtering domestic economy curbs loan growth and slumping stock markets erode their customers' appetite for investment products.Although Japanese 'megabanks' Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group (8306.T: Quote, Profile, Research), Mizuho Financial Group (8411.T: Quote, Profile, Research) and Sumitomo Mitsui Financial Group (8316.T: Quote, Profile, Research) aren't expected to reveal massive new subprime losses, they could report more damage from risky structured credit products, analysts said.With the exception of Mizuho, Japan's second-largest bank, the country's large lenders have so far skirted most of the global credit crisis that crippled overseas rivals such as UBS (UBSN.VX: Quote, Profile, Research) and Citigroup (C.N: Quote, Profile, Research).Nonetheless, Japanese banks are stuck with a lacklustre scenario at home: tepid lending growth and rising bankruptcies.'I'm turning increasingly negative on the outlook,' said Jason Rogers, credit analyst at Barclays Capital in Singapore.
