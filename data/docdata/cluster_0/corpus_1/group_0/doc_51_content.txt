  * Shares slip after previous session's jump * Financials fall on American Express' weak results * Firm commodity prices buoy resource firms(Adds details, comments)SYDNEY, July 22 (Reuters) - Australian shares eased 0.1 percent on Tuesday with financials such as National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX: NAB.ax) weighed down by nagging credit concerns, offsetting gains in resource firms on firmer commodity prices.Market watchers said it was not surprising the market was unable to extend the previous session's rally given the outlook for equity markets was still clouded by concerns about tight credit conditions and slowing company profits.'You've got to think that company earnings are not going to be up to scratch with all the problems going on, especially in the financial sector,' said Martin Angel, a dealer at Patersons Securities.'The biggest problem is we don't know what else is coming and we don't know how bad it's going to get.'The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index .AXJO fell 6.2 points to 5,005.6, based on the latest available data, after rising 3.5 percent in the previous session.The index has dropped 21 percent since the start of the year following an 11.8 percent gain in 2007.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index .NZ50 edged down 0.1 percent, or 4.41 points, to 3,143.91. The most heavily weighted stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand (NZX: TEL.nz) , finished down 1.5 percent at NZ$3.40.BANKS, CSL WEAKFinancial firms fell after credit card company American Express Co AXP.N reported weaker-than-expected quarterly profits and set aside more money to cover credit losses [ID:nN21450430].Macquarie Group MQG.AX, Australia's top investment bank, lost 3.1 percent to A$46.59.The big banks were all weaker, with National Australia Bank falling 2.4 percent to A$27.65 and Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Ltd (ASX: ANZ.ax) down 3.9 percent at A$18.07.Citigroup downgraded its price targets for Australia's top five banks, citing slowing business lending and rising bad debts [ID:nSYD249940].CSL Ltd (ASX: CSL.ax) , which earns royalties from its cervical cancer vaccine Gardasil, was also under pressure, falling 9.3 percent to A$33.30.Merck &amp;amp; Co Inc MRK.N projected 2008 sales of between $1.4 billion and $1.6 billion for Gardasil, down from its prior view of $1.9 billion to $2.1 billion [ID:nN21455939].Mining firms advanced after gold prices XAU= rose on a weak U.S. dollar, while base metals such as copper, lead and zinc were also firmer.BHP Billiton Ltd (ASX: BHP.ax) , the world's top miner and Australia's top oil and gas producer, rose 2.1 percent to A$39.00, while its main rival and takeover target, Rio Tinto Ltd (ASX: RIO.ax) , added 1.9 percent to A$120.70.Kingsgate Consolidated Ltd (ASX: KCN.ax) , Thailand's only gold miner, jumped 28.8 percent to A$6.04.The company said it had received final ministerial approval for the Chatree North mining lease next to its existing operation in central Thailand.Elsewhere, property investor Mirvac Group (ASX: MGR.ax) fell 5.7 percent to A$2.31 after it said its operating earnings could fall as much as 24 percent in fiscal 2009 as it grapples with higher funding costs [ID:nSYD41206].
