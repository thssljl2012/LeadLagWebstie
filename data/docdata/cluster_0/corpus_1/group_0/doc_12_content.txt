 SYDNEY (Reuters) - Australia and New Zealand Banking Group (ASX:ANZ.AX - News), Australia's third-biggest lender, said on Monday its 2008 cash earnings per share were likely to fall between 20 percent and 25 percent on the previous year due to a rise in credit impairment costs.ANZ said its provisions in the second half were likely to be around A$1.2 billion ($1.1 billion) as a result of the ongoing deterioration in global credit markets. ($1=A$1.05)(Reporting by James Thornhill)
