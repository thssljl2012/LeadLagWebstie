 SYDNEY (Thomson Financial) - Australian shares rose 1 percent in early trade on Wednesday on gains in financial firms but index leader BHP Billiton retreated after the world's top miner warned of higher costs in its fourth-quarter production report. 'The market is a bit disappointed with BHP though costs are nothing new - overall it was still a good report with record production of a number of commodities,' said Michael Heffernan, an equities analyst at Austock Securities.At 0040 GMT, the S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 was up 49.1 points or 1.0 percent at 5,054.7 and the All Ordinaries index had gained 41.7 points or 0.8 percent to 5,117.6..BHP was down 2.2 percent at A$38.15 while Rio Tinto was off 1.8 percent at A$118.49, under pressure because of lower commodity prices.Financial firms were in favor after U.S. financial stocks rallied on Tuesday after the fourth- largest U.S. bank, Wachovia, announced measures to cut jobs and raise fresh capital.Australia's top investment bank, Macquarie Group, advanced 6.1 percent to A$49.41 after telling shareholders at its annual general meeting that it still expected healthy earnings in the year to March although it would be challenging to match last year's record net profit of A$1.8 billion ($1.75 billion).Commonwealth Bank of Australia gained 2.0 percent to A$43.86 after saying it was in talks to buy Dutch bank ABN Amro's Australian and New Zealand operations.
