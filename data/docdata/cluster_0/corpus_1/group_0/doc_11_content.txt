 SYDNEY (Thomson Financial) - Australia's consumer price index (CPI) rose 1.5 percent in the three months to June from the previous quarter and was up 4.5 percent year-on-year, the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) said on Wednesday.The market consensus was for a 1.3 percent rise quarter-on-quarter and a 4.3 percent gain year-on-year.In the first quarter CPI rose 1.3 percent quarter-on-quarter and 4.2 percent year-on-year.The ABS said CPI, excluding housing and financial and insurance services, rose 1.3 percent in the quarter and rose 3.3 percent over the year.The Reserve Bank of Australia's (RBA) trimmed mean CPI rose 1.2 percent quarter-on-quarter and 4.3 percent through the year.The RBA's weighted medium CPI rose 1.0 percent quarter-on-quarter and 4.5 percent year-on-year.Market forecasts centered on both the RBA trimmed mean and weighted medium CPI measures rising 1.1 percent in the quarter.The trimmed mean was forecast to rise 4.3 percent from a year ago, while the weighted medium was seen 4.5 percent higher for the year.The RBA focuses on the average of its two core measures of inflation when setting rates and seeks to keep this measure in a 2-3 percent year-on-year range.Following are the breakdown of inflation data from the ABS:CPI (All Groups) qtr/qtr pct yr/yr pct IndexQ2 +1.5 +4.5 164.6Q1 +1.3 +4.2 162.2Q2 2007 +1.2 +2.1 157.5Market sector goods and services CPI:Q2 +1.4 +4.2 162.4Q1 +0.8 +3.5 160.2Q2 2007 +0.7 +2.4 155.9Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) trimmed mean CPI:Q2 +1.2 +4.3Q1 +1.2 +4.0Q2 2007 +0.9 +2.7Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) weighted median CPI:Q2 +1.0 +4.5Q1 +1.3 +4.4Q2 2007 +1.0 +2.9bruce.hextall@thomsonreuters.combhx/msInteractive Investor brings you breaking news throughout the day to support your investment decisions.
