 * NAB lead declines on CDO provisions * Falling metals prices hurt resource firms * Index remain on track to snap nine straight week of declines(Adds details, comments)SYDNEY, July 25 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 2.5 percent on Friday as National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX: NAB.ax) led the banking sector sharply lower on deeping credit concerns, while falls in metals prices pressured resource firms.Shares in NAB tumbled as much as 12.7 percent, the biggest single-day percentage fall since September 2001, after it booked another A$830 million ($798 million) in losses from its exposure to U.S. mortgages [ID:nSYD337471].'You'd have expected profit-taking today anyway and this is a just a perfect catalyst,' said Eric Betts, equities strategist at Nomura Australia.'With NAB's provisions, this looks like they've provided about 90 percent of their CDOs exposure. So one hopes there is nothing more lurking ... although it will take a big bite out of this year's earnings.'Also hurting sentiment in the financial sector, data showed that sales of existing U.S. homes dropped to a 10-year low, triggering a sell-off in U.S. financial firms which have been badly bruised by the housing slump [ID:nN24267606].The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index .AXJO fell 128.9 points to 5,015.2 by 0053 GMT, ending two straight sessions of gains.Still, the benchmark index .AXJO is on track to end nine straight weeks of losses, after better-than-expected earnings at big U.S. banks, coupled with a drop in oil prices, lured investors back into the market earlier in the week.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index .NZ50 fell 0.4 percent, or 13.51 points, to 3,273.71. The most heavily weighted stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand (NZX: TEL.nz) , was up 0.9 percent at NZ$3.50.STOCKSON THE MOVE* Financial firms, which have posted strong gains in the previous two sessions, declined on growing credit market worries.NABfell 9.8 percent to A$27.70 and Commonwealth Bank of Australia Ltd (ASX: CBA.ax) lost 5.1 percent to A$44.01.Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Ltd (ASX: ANZ.ax) shed 5.3 percent to A$18.42 while Westpac Banking Corp (ASX: WBC.ax) was down 4.3 percent at A$21.82.Macquarie Group MQG.AX, Australia's top investment bank, declined 4.2 percent to A$51.04.The financial services index .AXFJ, which had climbed a total of 9.3 percent in the previous two sessions, dropped 5 percent, logging its biggest one-day percentage fall in six months.0049 GMT* Mining firms fell after concerns about global demand weighed down on industrial metals including copper, nickel and zinc.BHP Billiton Ltd (ASX: BHP.ax) , the world's top miner and Australia's top oil and gas producer, fell 1.2 percent to A$37.12, while its main rival and takeover target, Rio Tinto Ltd (ASX: RIO.ax) , slipped 0.6 percent to A$114.56.0047 GMT* Incitec Pivot Ltd (ASX: IPL.ax) , Australia's top fertiliser maker, fell 5.4 percent to A$141.50.The Australian Financial Review reported that the firm is considering raising up to A$1 billion ($960 million) to boost its balance sheet and finance expansion [ID:nSYD154776].0046 GMT ($1=A$1.04) (Reporting by Geraldine Chua)More Quotes and Company Information:AUSTRALIA AND NEW ZEALAND BANKING GROUP LIMITED (ASX: ANZ.ax)BHP BILLITON(ASX: BHP.ax)COMMONWEALTH BANK OF AUSTRALIA.(ASX: CBA.ax)INCITEC PIVOT LIMITED(ASX: IPL.ax)NATIONAL AUSTRALIA BANK LIMITED(ASX: NAB.ax)RIO TINTO LIMITED(ASX: RIO.ax)WESTPAC BANKING CORPORATION(ASX: WBC.ax)Telecom Corporation of New Zealand Limited(NZX: TEL.nz)
