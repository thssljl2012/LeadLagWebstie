 (Adds CSR, Anvil)SYDNEY, July 11 (Reuters) - Australian shares rose 0.3 percent on Friday as stronger oil and metals prices lifted resource firms such as BHP Billiton Ltd (ASX: BHP.ax) , though worries about more credit-related losses weighed down on the financials. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index .AXJO rose 12.8 points to 4,950.2 by 0148 GMT, after touching its lowest level in nearly two years in the previous session.New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index .NZ50 fell 0.7 percent, or 20.46 points, to 3,092.10. The most heavily weighted stock, Telecom Corp of New Zealand (NZX: TEL.nz) , was down 2.1 percent at NZ$3.30. STOCKS ON THE MOVE* Conglomerate CSR Ltd (ASX: CSR.ax) rose 7.1 percent to A$2.11, partially recovering after a 14.7 percent drop on Thursday -- its biggest one-day fall in over a decade -- after a disappointing earnings outlook [ID:nSYD6298].Brokers upgraded their ratings on the firm, saying that the stock was probably oversold and valuations looked attractive.ABN AMRO raised its rating on CSR to buy from hold and Deutsche Bank upgraded its recommendation to buy from hold.JP Morgan raised its rating to overweight from neutral, while Macquarie Research Equities upgraded its call to neutral from underperform.0146 GMT* Anvil Mining (ASX: AVM.ax) AVM.TO climbed 22.2 percent to A$11.00, tracking strong gains in their Toronto-listed shares, after the copper miner announced a C$296 million ($293 million) private placement at a premium to its recent share price [ID:nN10383423].0142 GMT* Energy firms rose after oil prices CLc1 jumped 4 percent to above $141 a barrel on Thursday.Woodside Petroleum Ltd (ASX: WPL.ax) gained 3.3 percent to A$59.80, Santos Ltd (ASX: STO.ax) added 2.8 percent to A$18.97, Oil Search Ltd (ASX: OSH.ax) rose 1.4 percent to A$5.80. 0050 GMT* Mining firms also advance after gold prices XAU= gained nearly 2 percent on Thursday, while base metals prices were also stronger, with aluminium jumping to a record high and lead surging over 12 percent.BHP Billiton, the world's top miner and Australia's top oil and gas producer, rose 2.3 percent to A$39.70, while its main rival and takeover target, Rio Tinto Ltd (ASX: RIO.ax) , added 1.7 percent to A$123.66. Gold miner Newcrest Mining (ASX: NCM.ax) gained 4.5 percent to A$31.16 and Alumina Ltd (ASX: AWC.ax) , which has alumina and aluminium making operations, climbed 7.3 percent to A$4.54. Alumina said after the market close on Thursday that a gas shortage in west Australia will reduce its third quarter after tax underlying earnings by about A$31 million ($29 million), though the impact was less than previously estimated [ID:nSYD199708].0048 GMT* Financials firms fell on ongoing concerns that global financial institutions face further credit-related writedowns.National Australia Bank Ltd (ASX: NAB.ax) (NAB) fell 2.6 percent to A$26.89.
