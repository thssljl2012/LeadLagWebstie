SYDNEY, Dec 8 (Reuters) - Australian stocks rose 4.2 percent on Monday, boosted by takeover speculation surrounding energy group Santos Ltd, a rally in financial stocks and gains for Qantas Airways Ltd. The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index stood 145 points higher at 3,634.9 at 0344 GMT. New Zealand's benchmark NZX-50 index rose 0.3 percent to 2,713.8. STOCKS ON THE MOVE * Australia's Qantas Airways Ltd was up 6.3 percent at A$2.36. Chief Executive Alan Joyce said at a lunch meeting there were key issues unresolved with regard to its potential merger with British Airways, and that there was a resonable chance the merger would not go ahead. For more see . 'The oil price is off again, and there has been a lot of newsflow from the company this morning. I guess people are looking at it in a positive manner,' said Martin Angel, dealer at Patersons Securities Ltd. 0317 GMT * Rio Tinto fell 2.5 percent to A$31.20, in contrast to BHP Billiton which stood 2.14 percent higher at A$26.70, though off a daily high of A$27. BHP Billiton is considered to have one of the sector's more robust balance sheets. 'Resource stocks generally are more subdued today with the exception of a few...but there is no general attraction on resource stocks at the moment,' said David Spry, research manager at F.W Holst. 0134 GMT * Australia's two largest banks outperformed the wider market, with No. 1 lender National Australia Bank up 4.6 percent at A$20.72, and No. 2 lender, Commonwealth Bank of Australia up 6.4 percent at A$32.90. 'They have been sold down heavily, and...given where some of them have been in recent days, it's probably reasonably attractive to put your toe in the water a bit,' said Spry. 0133 GMT * Australian oil and gas producer Santos Ltd's shares jumped 12 percent to A$13.61 following a report in the South China Morning Post paper saying China National Petroleum Corp is planning to team up with an unnamed foreign oil company to make a bid for Santos. 0006 GMT * Ivanhoe Australia rose 15.4 percent to A$0.30 after announcing exploration work had uncovered promising signs of a big molybdenum and rhenium find at its Cloncurry mine in Queensland state. 0006 GMT * Shares in Leighton Holdings Ltd, Australia's top engineering contractor, rose 8.4 percent to A$23.10 after the group said its Al Habtoor joint venture had won a A$2 billion ($1.3 billion) Dubai contract.
