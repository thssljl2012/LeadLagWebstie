THE share market was higher at noon after gains in the financial sector and following Wall Street's late rally on Friday.  At 12.00pm (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index had gained 117.9 points, or 3.38 per cent, to 3607.8.  The broader All Ordinaries was up 105.3 points, or 3.07 per cent, at 3532.5.  ABN AMRO Morgans private client adviser Bill Bishop said it was 'green across the screen''.  'The American market went up on Friday because the employment figures were so bad the market was hoping the government would pump more cheap money into the economy,'' Mr Bishop said.  'There has been a lot of optimism after the rally on the American market.  'Our banks are up between 2.5 and 5 per cent and it has been a very good start to the day.''  Big banks rose this morning. Commonwealth Bank was up 5.7 per cent to $32.67, NAB increased 5 per cent to $20.79, Westpac added 3.3 per cent to $17.40 and ANZ advanced 2.7 per cent to $14.99.  In mining stocks, Rio Tinto shares fell 1 per cent to $31.64 but BHP Billiton shares were up 2 per cent to $26.71.  In company news, retailer Gerry Harvey said some stores will close in the next 6 months as the economy slows. Harvey Norman shares increased 6cents to $2.41.  Pacific Brands stock fell half a cent to 60 cents after the company announced that it was not considering a capital raising, although deteriorating economic and retail conditions had forced it to reconsider its dividend policy.  Other retailers were stronger.  Wesfarmers, which owns Coles, was 3.5 per cent higher at $17.09, Woolworths rose 4.8 per cent to $27.35 and David Jones gained 2.9 per cent $2.86.  James Hardie Industries NV says a dramatic rise in its share price last week was in line with its counterparts in the US, where the majority of its sales are made. James Hardie was up 4.84 per cent to $4.33.  In energy stocks, Woodside Petroleum was up 5.9 per cent, at $32.25, Santos lifted 10.12 per cent to $13.38 and Oil Search increased 3 per cent to $4.12.  Wall Street rose on Friday, with the S&amp;amp;P500 index rose 3.7 per cent to 876.07, and the Nasdaq composite index rose 4.4 per cent to 1509.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the December share price index contract was up 98 points at 3621 on a volume of 15,760 contracts.
