FRANKFURT, Sept 9 (Reuters) - European shares fell on Tuesday as mining stock such as Anglo American lost ground amid renewed economic growth worries and energy shares tumbled on the back of a fall in crude oil prices.The FTSEurofirst 300 index of top European shares closed unofficially 0.6 percent lower at 1,155.58 points, having risen more than 1 percent earlier in the session.Basic resources, which includes miners, slumped 5 percent on the sector index as copper prices fell to a 7-1/2 month low. Anglo American lost 6.1 percent, with fellow miners BHP Billiton and Rio Tinto down 5.1 percent and 4.7 percent, respectively.Steel maker ArcelorMittal dropped 4.5 percent.Financials were mixed after Monday's sharp rally triggered by the U.S. government's weekend bailout of mortgage financiers Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac.On Tuesday, shares in U.S. investment bank Lehman Brothers fell close to 40 percent in New York before recovering to trade 29 percent lower at the European stock markets' close at 1530 GMT.Germany's Deutsche Bank gained 2.9 percent and British bank Barclays rose 2.6 percent while French insurer AXA dropped 3.0 percent.Among the day's top gainer was drugmaker Sanofi-Aventis , up 2.5 percent after news that Chris Viehbacher, head of North American pharmaceuticals at rival GlaxoSmithKline , is to take a top job at the French company.(Reporting by Peter Starck) Keywords: MARKETS EUROPE STOCKS/CLOSE tf.TFN-Europe_newsdesk@thomsonreuters.com cmr COPYRIGHT Copyright Thomson Financial News Limited 2008.
