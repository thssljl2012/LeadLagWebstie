SEOUL, Sept 12 (Reuters) - South Korea said on Friday it has postponed a sovereign bond issue to raise around $1 billion due to tough credit market conditions on concerns over troubles at Lehman Brothers Holdings Inc..The finance ministry said market conditions has deteriorated further, with a jump in spreads of the country's sovereign debts, on reports that North Korean leader Kim Jong-il was ill.South Korea plans to sell the bonds once conditions in global financial markets improve, the ministry said in a statement.South Korea could hold off on pricing a proposed sovereign bond issue as debt investors demand a higher yield to compensate for deteriorating credit market conditions, a senior finance ministry official said on Thursday.Barclays Capital, Goldman Sachs, Lehman Brothers, Samsung Securities and UBS are arranging the deal.
