AUSTRALIAN shares collapsed more than 2.5 per cent as a bloodbath on Wall Street sent investors rushing for the exits.Shortly after the opening of trading, the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index was down 111.9 points to a new three-year low of 4705.8 - more than 31 per cent blow its record high in November last year. Wall Street collapsed in the last hours of trading this morning, with the Dow Jones industrial average losing 504.48 points, or 4.4 per cent, to finish at 10,917.51. It was the biggest fall since the aftermath of the September 11 terror attacks in 2001.The bankruptcy of Lehman Brothers, the fire sale of Merrill Lynch to Bank of America and the problems engulfing giant insurer AIG weighed heavily on Australian bank stocks. ANZ slumped 91c to $15.96, Westpac lost 81c to $22.34, NAB was down 88c to $21.94 and the Commonwealth, which admitted a $150 million exposure to Lehman, fell 97c to $41.01. Macquarie Bank hit a new four-year low, with Babcock &amp;amp; Brown crashing 40 per cent.
