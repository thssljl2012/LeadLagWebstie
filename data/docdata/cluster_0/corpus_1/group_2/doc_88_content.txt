(RTTNews) - Traders get a deeper look Tuesday at the thinking among members of the Reserve Bank of Australia's board in this month's interest rate reduction.The RBA releases minutes of its September 2nd Monetary Policy Committee meeting at 9:30 PM EDT.At that meeting, the RBA decided to reduce its cash rate by 25 basis points to 7.0 percent. A statement by RBA Governor Glenn Stevens accompanying the announcement noted a slowing in spending in the economy. Stevens added that the bank felt inflation appeared to have been brought under control, and that 'there was now scope for monetary policy to become less restrictive.' New Zealand's calendar of economic data releases is clear for Tuesday.
