At 1635 AEDT, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 was up 44.8 points, or 1.27 per cent, at 3,559.1, while the broader All Ordinaries gained 38.2 points, or 1.1 per cent to 3,496.7  On the Sydney Futures Exchange at 1636 AEDT, the March share price index contract was 46 points higher at 3,517 on a volume of 23,834 contracts.  CMC Markets senior dealer Dominic Vaughan said the Australian market ended on a good note after a fairly positive week.  'We had a very good performance from the market today, with the financial sector holding up quite well, the only stock holding it down is Rio.'  In the resources sector, mining giant Rio Tinto lost $1.00, or 1.92 per cent, to $51.00.  Rio Tinto Ltd is confident its landmark $US19.5 billion ($A29.87 billion) alliance with China's Chinalco will clear all regulatory and government hurdles, amid a mixed response to the deal from investors.  Rival miner BHP Billiton added 23 cents to $32.70.  Among the major banks, National Australia Bank lost 15 cents to $18.45, Commonwealth Bank added 45 cents to $31.40 and Westpac found 30 cents at $16.95.  ANZ Banking Group Ltd says it has no current plans afoot for a capital raising despite market speculation.  ANZ rose $1.07, or nine per cent, to $12.96.  Making headlines this Friday, Australia's biggest gold company, Newcrest booked a modest first half profit increase.  Newcrest climbed 67 cents to $34.67, rival Lihir Gold lost six cents to $3.35 and Newmont rose five cents to $6.45.  At 1654 AEDT, the spot price of gold in Sydney was $US939.30 an ounce, up $US0.15 on Thursday's local close of $US939.15.  Financial services provider AMP expects its annual underlying profit for 2008 to be about $800 million, compared to $882 million in 2007.  Its shares were 10 cents higher at $5.20.  Furniture retailer Nick Scali was steady at 50 cents after reporting a 45 per cent drop in half year profit.  Among the major retailers, Woolworths added 16 cents to $27.80, David Jones gained 10 cents to $2.40 and Coles owner Wesfarmers rose 67 cents to $16.15.  In the energy sector, Woodside rose 75 cents to $32.90, Santos lost 13 cents to $14.77 and Oil Search increased two cents to $4.62.  The top traded stock was Rio Tinto with 8.23 million shares worth $415.88 million changing hands.  Market turnover was 1.09 billion shares worth $3.06 billion, with 507 stocks up, 333 down and 287 unchanged.
