A LATE recovery failed to stop the share market from closing in the red following weakness among the big miners.  At the 4.15pm (AEDT) close, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was 51.2 points, or 1.5 per cent, lower at 3351.2.  The broader All Ordinaries index fell 48.9 points, or 1.46 per cent, to 3304.1. 'We were was down around 80 points at one stage but there was a Wall Street Journal report on Citigroup ... the market has rallied on that,'' ABN Amro Morgans private client adviser Trent Muller said.  The Wall Street Journal reported that the US government was in talks that could lead to it taking a stake of 25 to 40 per cent in troubled US bank Citigroup.  Mr Muller said Rio Tinto was lower on concerns that Rio's $US19.5 billion ($30.32 billion) deal with China's state-backed aluminium group and major shareholder, Chinalco, to help pay down Rio's debt may not get approval from the Australian government.  Rio Tinto lost $3.42, or 6.8 per cent, to $46.87.  Elsewhere in the resources sector, global miner BHP Billiton, which was ex dividend, was down $1.20, or 3.93 per cent, to $29.31.  Fortescue Metals Group was in a trading halt amid expectations Chinese steel maker Hunan Valin Iron and Steel would take a stake in the iron ore miner as it raises up to $500 million in equity.  Fortescue last traded at $2.83.  BlueScope Steel slumped 34 cents, or 10.9 per cent, to $2.78 after the steel maker slashed its interim dividend and warned of a second half loss.  Oil and gas producer Woodside Petroleum reversed 60 cents to $32.77 and Santos eased 7 cents to $14.49.  Among the major banks, NAB was down 21 cents to $17.80, Commonwealth Bank nudged up 1 cent to $29.58, Westpac dipped 6 cents to $16.32, and ANZ improved 1 cent to $12.46.  In the media sector, Fairfax Media fell 3.5 cents to $1.015 after it reported a first half net loss of $365 million and said it was battening down the hatches to ride out the economic downturn.  News Corp (the parent company of the publisher of news.com.au) was 12 cents higher at $10.32, while its non-voting stock added 4 cents to $9.25.  Consolidated Media retreated six cents to $1.83.  Among gold stocks, Newcrest descended 34 cents to $32.69, Newmont strengthened 36 cents to $6.76 and Lihir gained 3 cents to $3.43.  The price of gold in Sydney at 4.30pm AEDT was $US987.90 per fine ounce, up $US14.70 on Friday's close of $US973.20 per fine ounce.  Telstra was four cents richer at $3.75, and Optus-owner Singapore Telecommunications lifted two cents to $2.51.  Retailer Woolworths advanced 31 cents to $27.93 and Wesfarmers, which owns Coles, was up 10 cents at $17.89.  On Wall Street on Friday, the Dow Jones Industrial Average index fell 100.28 points to 7365.67 - a new six-year as investors around the world kept selling on pessimism about the global economy.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract was off 38 points at 3324 on a volume of 22,062 contracts, according to preliminary calculations.
