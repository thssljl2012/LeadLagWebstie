Article from: AAP  Font size: Decrease Increase  : Email  : Print  THE stock market was marginally stronger at noon following gains in the financial sector.  By 12.00pm (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was up 30 points, or 0.9 per cent, at 3357.5 while the broader All Ordinaries index had gained 26.6 points, or 0.81 per cent, to 3308.1.  CMC Markets market analyst David Taylor said investors were feeling secure about banking stocks and consumer staples.  'We've had a couple of stocks dragging on the market, but generally speaking we're up,' he said.  'The banks, Westfield, there are a few heavyweights doing well.  'Really, the only thing dragging on the market are BHP and Telstra and Macquarie, but fortunately there are enough big cap stocks up to put us in positive territory.'  Financials were higher.  ANZwas up 33 cents, or 2.64 per cent, at $12.83 after saying its unaudited net profit fell 11 per cent in the first four months of fiscal 2009, to the end of January.  The bank also said cash earnings for the period, excluding provisions and credit intermediation trades, were up 18 per cent on the corresponding period of fiscal 2008.  NAB added 36 cents to $17.69, Commonwealth Bank found 27 cents to $29.40 and Westpac rose 32 cents to $16.50.  In the resources sector, market giant BHP Billiton held steady at $29.01 while rival Rio Tinto was up 29 cents to $46.47.  In company news, Telstra was down 7 cents at $3.70, after it announced chief executive Sol Trujillo would leave the telco on June 30.  The group also reported a 1 per cent dip in first half profit.  The property sector was abuzz with activity, Lend Lease reported a loss of almost $600 million for the first half, and has warned of continuing market volatility for the foreseeable future.  Lend lease climbed 2.45 per cent, to $5.02.  CentroProperties reported a $2.4 billion first half loss as the shopping centre owner was hit by falling property values, slumping derivative values and losses from the movement in exchange rates.  Centro was down 0.5 cent to 7.8 cents a share.  Shopping centre developer Westfield Group has reported a net loss for fiscal 2008 of $2.1966 billion, although it says underlying earnings were strong.  The property giant's shares added 51 cents, or 5.23 per cent, to $10.26.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract was up 17 points to 3344 on a volume of 13,177 contracts.
