NEW YORK (Reuters) - Stocks slid further on Thursday, sending indexes down more than 1 percent, as investors sold off financial shares on worries about the fate of the beleaguered banking sector and signs that the recession is deepening.  * Financial sector drags included Bank of America (NYSE:BAC - News) , down 13.3 percent to $3.96, and Citigroup (NYSE:C - News), off 13.1 percent at $2.52.  * The Dow Jones industrial average (DJI:^DJI - News) was down 83.39 points, or 1.10 percent, at 7,472.24. The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 Index (^SPX - News) was down 8.79 points, or 1.11 percent, at 779.63. The Nasdaq Composite Index (Nasdaq:^IXIC - News) was down 22.16 points, or 1.51 percent, at 1,445.81.  (Reporting by Ellis Mnyandu; Editing by Leslie Adler)
