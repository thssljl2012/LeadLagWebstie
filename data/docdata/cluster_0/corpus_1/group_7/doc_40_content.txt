North American stocks started the week with a whimper as investors on both sides of the border bite their nails, waiting for final word on U.S. government plans to bail out its economy and banking sector.  In Canada, The S&amp;P/TSX Composite index closed at 9047.28, up 39.26 points or 0.44%, after rising higher earlier in the day on a shortlived jump in oil prices. The TSX venture exchange lost 8.24 points or .91% to 901.10 and the Canadian dollar moved up 61 basis points to close at US82.21 cents.  Markets fluctuated throughout the day in nervous anticipation of two key economic developments this week. Tuesday, Treasury Secretary Timothy Geithner is expected to announce the U.S. Treasury's package for the ailing banking and housing sectors. Details of the plan, which was delayed by a day, are expected to include a provision for private funding in addition to taxpayer money. Meanwhile, President Obama is pushing to have his economic stimulus package passed sometime later this week.  'People are definitely waiting for the outcome of the stimulus package and the 'bad bank' plans to be announced,' says Tommy Nguyen, portfolio manager at Palos Capital Management. 'The next couple of days are very important for the direction of the market.'  Mr. Nguyen wouldn't be surprised to see markets rally as details of each plan are revealed, but added that a sustained recovery in sentiment will depend on the fine print. 'All the little details have to come out and be analyzed for the market to rerun its course.'  Energy stocks led Toronto higher, rising more 2.21% as a group, on a morning rally in light sweet crude prices. Up as high as US$42.43 a barrel, oil dropped below US$40 to close at US$39.60, down US57 cents. Canadian Natural Resources Ltd. climbed 4% to $47.36 and UTS Energy Inc. added 10 cents to close at $1.80 after its board of directors rejected Total SA's hostile $1.30 per share offer for UTS.  The financials group advanced 1.21%, moving in lockstep with its U.S. counterparts. Of Canada's six big banks, only Bank of Montreal fell into negative territory yesterday, dropping 0.84% to $31.78.  Mining and metals stocks weighed down gains on the country's main exchange. As a group, materials fell 3.01% as gold dropped to US$892.40 an ounce, down US$21.50.  In the U.S., stocks were virtually unchanged in a mixed day across the country's three main indices. The Dow Jones Industrial Average lost 9.72 points or .012% to 8270.87. The S&amp;P 500 climbed 1.29 points or 0.15% to 869.89 and the Nasdaq Composite lost .15 points or .01% to 1591.56. A rally in U.S. banks led by Bank of America, up 12% on the day and Hartford Financial Group Inc., up 18% and General Electric Co., up 13% both advanced on speculation they will get government aid. Coca Cola Co. fell almost 3% after The Australasian brewer Lion Nathan said yesterday it had abandoned its US$4.9-billion dollar bid for coke bottler Coca-Cola Amatil.
