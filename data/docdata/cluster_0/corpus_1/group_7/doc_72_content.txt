TOKYO, Feb 10 (Reuters) - Japan's Nikkei average rose 1.2 percent on Tuesday, with investors picking up recent decliners such as Kyocera Corp, but trade was set to be cautious ahead of the announcement of a U.S. bank rescue plan.   The benchmark Nikkei gained 97.91 points to 8,066.94, after ending 1.3 percent lower the previous day.  The broader Topix added 1.1 percent to 787.50.
