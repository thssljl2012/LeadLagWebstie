THE Australian stock market was down after Friday's open, as the US market slid to near its lowest level in three months.  By 1015 AEDT, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was down 24.5 points, or 0.71 per cent, at 3,424.4 while the broader  lost 21.8 points, or 0.64 per cent, to 3,376.2. On the , the March share price index futures contract shed nine points to 3,391 on a volume of 4,222 contracts.  The banking sector was mixed.  National Australia Bank lost 21 cents to $18.19, Commonwealth Bank lost 43 cents to $29.42, Westpac fell 19 cent to $16.81, and the ANZ lost ten cents to $12.50.  In the resources sector, market giant BHP Billiton added three cents to $30.19 while rival Rio Tinto lost 51 cents to $51.27.  In New York, Wall Street teetered near its lowest levels in three months, helped along by news that Hewlett-Packard Co was scaling back its 2009 forecast.  Hewlett-Packard Co fell eight per cent after the computer and printer company turned in disappointing fourth-quarter sales, hurt by tightening spending at many businesses.  When markets settled, the Dow Jones industrial average was down 89.68 points, or by 1.19 per cent, to 7,465.95. The  500 index fell 9.48, or 1.2 per cent, to settle at 778.94. The technology-heavy Nasdaq composite index settled down 25.15, or 1.71 per cent, to 1,442.82.  ABN AMRO Morgans senior client adviser Bill Bishop said the banks were leading the market lower. 'Because the markets around the world are lower of late, they're making a comment about the effectiveness of America's bailout package,' he said. 'Like it or lump it, we trade off America, and they're sending out mixed messages. It's frightened the markets.' By 1027 AEDT, Santos had added 24 cents to $14.65, Woodside had found 71 cents to $34.23 and Oil Search found 15 cents to $4.79.  In media stocks, Fairfax lost 4.5 cents to 94 cents, after hitting a record intraday low of 93 cents shortly after trading opened, News Corp lost three cents to $10.47, News's non-vioting scrip lost eight cents to $9.43, and the Ten Network was holding steady at 80 cents.  The Seven Network added five cents to $5.75.  Among the major retailers, Woolworths lost one cent to $27.49, David Jones held steady at $2.41 and Coles owner Wesfarmers shed eight cents to $17.01.  Telstra was holding at $3.68, while Optus owner Singtel lost four cents to $2.49.  Property developer Westfield Group added two cents to $10, Macquarie Group lost $1.21, or 5.53 per cent, to $20.68 and Lend Lease was down nine cents, to $5.20.  The national Airline, Qantas, lost one cent to $1.74.  By 1042 AEDT, the spot price of gold in Sydney was $US975.30 per fine ounce, down $US1.70 on Thursday's local close of $US977.00.  Gold miner Newcrest lost $1.62, or 4.51 per cent, to $34.28, Sino Gold lost 18 cents, or 3.1 per cent, to $5.62, while Lihir lost two cents to $3.51.  US-based Newmont, the world's second largest gold producer, returned to profitability in the fourth quarter as rising gold prices offset a sharp drop in copper demand. Its stock lost 14 cents, or 2.12 per cent, to $6.45.  Meanwhile, Gloucester Coal has agreed to a $545 million merger with Whitehaven Coal Ltd in a bid to better position the merged group for future growth.  Gloucester was holding steady at $3.28 a share while Whitehaven held at $1.51. Both stocks were in an adjust mode, during which trade orders might be modified but not yet processed.  Caltex Australia, the nation's only listed oil refiner and marketer, will not pay a final dividend after delivering a significant drop in profit for the year.  Caltex was down 88 cents, or 9.27 per cent, to $8.72 by 1042 AEDT.  The National Australia Bank, Australia's biggest bank by assets, has announced former head of Australian operations Ahmed Fahour will cease to be a member of the board and executive committee, effective Friday.  The most traded stock was Oz Minerals with 9.6 million shares worth $6.4 million changing hands, pushing the stock 1.5 cents higher, or by 2.31 per cent, to 66.5 cents by 1044 AEDT.  Market turnover was 176.6 million shares traded for a value of $516.8 million, with 200 stocks up, 292 down and 180 unchanged.
