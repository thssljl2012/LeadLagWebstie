Article from: AAP  Font size: Decrease Increase  : Email  : Print  THE stock market was lower at noon with the resources sector taking the biggest falls as most sectors fell into the red.  At 12.00pm (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was 84.3 points lower, or 2.48 per cent, at 3318.1.  The broader All Ordinaries index had lost 79.2 points, or 2.36 per cent, to 3273.8.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract was 67 points weaker at 3295 on a volume of 10,880 contracts.  Stocks with international exposure were among the worst performers when trade resumed for the week, Macquarie Private Wealth adviser Helen Spencer said.  'We're still very much on the back foot after a fairly weak session on Wall Street on Friday night,'' Ms Spencer said.  'Resources are probably taking much of the brunt today but it's really right across the board.''  Global miner BHP Billiton was down $1.28, or 4.2 per cent, to $29.23, while Rio Tinto had lost $3.40, or 6.76 per cent, to $46.89.  Energy stocks also were lower, with Santos dropping 39 cents, or 2.68 per cent, to $14.17, Woodside giving up $1.14, or 3.42 per cent, to $32.23 and Oil Search off 12 cents, or 2.63 per cent, to $4.45.  Fortescue Metals Group shares have entered a trading halt pending an announcement about a capital raising. They last traded at $2.83.  The financial sector was lower on the performance of peer stocks on Wall Street, Ms Spencer said.  'Financials are still seeing some weakness flowing through from the weaker financials in the US on Friday night, and insurers are on the back foot, as well,'' she said.  NAB was down 33 cents to $17.68, Commonwealth Bank had lost 69 cents to $28.88, Westpac gave up 19 cents to $16.19 and ANZ shed 28 cents to $12.17.  Macquarie Group fell 64 cents, or 3.1 per cent, to $20.01, QBE Insurance lost 57 cents, or 2.75 per cent, to $20.17 and Suncorp Metway shed 40 cents, or 7.53 per cent, to $4.91.  Making news on Monday, steel producer BlueScope Steel has reported first half profit up 251 per cent, but says its underlying full year result could be a loss if the economy continues to worsen.  Bluescope shares lost 31 cents, or 9.94 per cent, to $2.81.  Fairfax shares were 3.5 cents lower, or 3.33 per cent, to $1.015 after the media group reported a first half net loss of $365.27 million, and said it was making preparations to ride out the economic downturn.  Other media stocks were mixed, with Consolidated Media losing nine cents, or 4.76 per cent, to $1.80 and News Ltd (parent company of the publisher of news.com.au) up 1 cent to $10.21.  News's non-voting scrip added four cents to $9.25.  Budget airline Virgin Blue reported a first-half net loss of $101.4 million, and said the outlook for the rest of the financial year remained challenging.  Virgin Blue shares remained steady at 23.5 cents.  Rival Qantas was down 8 cents, or 4.78 per cent, to $1.595.  Wall Street fell on Friday, sending the Dow Jones industrials to new 6-year lows, down 100.28 points to 7365.67.  The Standard &amp; Poor's 500 index fell 8.89, or 1.14 per cent, to settle at 770.05, and the technology-heavy Nasdaq composite index fell 1.59, or 0.11 per cent, to 1441.23.
