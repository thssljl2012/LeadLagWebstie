THE share market closed more than 1 per cent lower following falls in the resources and financial sector after negative leads from overseas.  At 4.15pm (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 was down 51.1 points, or 1.48 per cent, at 3413.2, while the broader All Ordinaries dropped 45.3 points, or 1.33 per cent, to 3366.9. 'All sectors are down but it's financials and materials that are being hit,'' IG Markets research analyst Ben Potter said. 'There was warnings from the big credit ratings agency's on banks exposures to Eastern European debt.'' 'The US financial were hit badly overnight as well.''  In the resources sector, mining giant Rio Tinto added 48 cents to $50.55, its rival BHP Billiton dropped $1.41 to $30.31.  Among the major banks, ANZ lost two cents to $12.40, Commonwealth Bank added 15 cents to $29.16 and NAB added 26 cents to $18.14.  Westpac Banking Corporation shares lost 30 cents to $16.48 after it announced a $300 million exposure to struggling investment firm Babcock &amp; Brown. The exposure pushed the bank's impaired loans up $930 million during the first quarter of financial year 2009.  On Wall Street overnight, stocks tumbled by more than three per cent and the Dow Jones industrial average sank near the multi-year lows it reached last November.  The Dow dropped 297.81, or 3.79 per cent, to settle at 7,552.6 - just half a point away from the blue-chip index's five-and-a-half month closing low of 7,552.29 on November 20 last year.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange at 4.15pm, the March share price index contract was 63 points lower at 3378 on a volume of 25,809 contracts.
