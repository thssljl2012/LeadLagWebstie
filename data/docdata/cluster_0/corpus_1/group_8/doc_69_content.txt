Australian shares have slipped into the red on a slump in mining stocks, after making modest gains this morning.  In afternoon trade, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was down 0.2%, or 6.1 points, at 3445.8, while the broader All Ordinaries index was down 0.2%, or 7.5 points, at 3385.9.  The Australian dollar continued to hold above 66 US cents, trading recently at 66.19 US cents, up from yesterday's local close of 66 US cents. It was also buying 65.19 yen and 50.78 euro cents.  On Asian markets, the Nikkei 225 was up 1.41 points at 7590.54 and the Hang Seng was up 1.6% at 13,080.66.  Shares in Rio Tinto plunged today on speculation that Chinalco's bid for a stake in the global miner would fall through.  Rio's shares were down $4.63, or 8.9%, at $47.38, while rival BHP Billiton was down $1.02, or 3.2%, at $30.88.  Earlier, David Jones was one of the best performers on the ASX after posting a rise in first-half profit. Its shares were up as much as 10.5% this morning, and were recently up 16 cents, or 6.4%, at $2.65.  AXA Asia Pacific was up 19 cents, or 5.9%, at $3.41 following a $500 million institutional placement.  The materials sector was down 2.8%, but most sub-indexes of the market were higher. Financials was up 1%, while energy was flat.  Banks strong  The big banks were all higher. The Commonwealth Bank rose 43 cents, or 1.3%, to $32.90 and ANZ was up 25 cents, or 1.7%, to $14.68.  NAB added 16 cents, or 0.8%, to $19.09 and Westpac was up 15 cents, or 0.8%, at $17.86.  QBE Insurance was up 86 cents, or 4.81%, at $18.97 and IAG was down 1 cent, or 0.3%, at $3.49. AMP was up 1 cent, or 0.2%, at $4.32.  Elsewhere in the materials sector, OZ Minerals was down 1 cent, or 1.7%, at 57 cents, Fortescue Metals was down 8 cents, or 3.3%, at $2.34 and BlueScope Steel was up 9 cents, or 3.8%, at $2.44.  Gold miners down  Spot gold fell overnight and was recently trading $US1.73 lower at $US913.72 an ounce.  The gold miners were lower, with Lihir down 5 cents, or 1.6%, at $2.99, Newcrest was down 88 cents, or 2.7%, at $31.22 and Newmont lost 24 cents, or 4.2%, to $5.53.  Nymex crude oil futures rose overnight, but were down 61 US cents at $US48.55 a barrel in recent trade.  Woodside Petroleum was up 27 cents, or 0.7%, at $37.04 and Santos was up 33 cents, or 2.2%, at $15.49.  AGL Energy added 33 cents, or 2.3%, to $14.68 and Origin was up 17 cents, or 1.2%, at $14.26.  Arrow Energy rose 10 cents, or 3.8%, to $2.74 and Macarthur Coal lost 2 cents, or 0.6%, to $3.14.  Retailers rise  The retailers were mainly higher. Woolworths was up 18 cents, or 0.7%, at $25.52 and Wesfarmers was up 30 cents, or 1.6%, at $18.70.
