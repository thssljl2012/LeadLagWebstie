SYDNEY, March 9 (Reuters) - Australian shares briefly fell over 2 percent on Monday after a number of erroneous trades in insurer QBE, before the trades were cancelled and the index returned to trading flat. At 0353 GMT, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index was down 0.03 percent or 1 point, at 3,144.5. New Zealand's benchmark NZX 50 index was down 0.2 percent to 2,466.7. STOCKS ON THE MOVE * Shares in QBE fell 3.9 percent to A$15.66, after plunging due to a number of erroneous trades, including some at as low A$0.004. 0355 GMT * The majority of financial stocks fell, led by a 4.4 percent fall in NAB to A$16.06. No. 3 lender, Commonwealth Bank of Australia was down 1.5 percent at A$26.60. 0215 GMT * Fortescue Metals Group Ltd rose 2.8 percent to A$2.57, off highs of A$2.66 earlier in the day, after it announced heavy rains this month across northwestern Australia's iron ore belt had impacted production. 0155 GMT * Global miners BHP Billiton Ltd and Rio Tinto rose 5.1 percent and 4.7 percent to A$29.06 and A$47.83 respectively. Rio said on Monday it would not delay construction of the Oyu Tolgoi copper and gold mining project in Mongolia, denying media reports. 2350 GMT * Shares in oil and gas producer Woodside Petroleum Ltd gained 1.1 percent to A$35.95, and Santos rose 2.5 percent to A$15.58 after a rise in oil prices. 2349 GMT (Reporting by Mette Fraende) ((mette.fraende@reuters.com; +612-9373-1815; Reuters Messaging mette.fraende.reuters.com@reuters.net)) DIARIES &amp; DATA: IPO diary &amp; data Asia earnings diary U.S. earnings diary European diary Australia diary Wall Street Week Ahead Eurostocks Week Ahead ................................................................
