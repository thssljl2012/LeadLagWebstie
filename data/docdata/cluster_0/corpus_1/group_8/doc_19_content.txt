THE share market opened higher with the resources sector leading the way, after small gains on Wall Street on Friday and rises in commodity prices.  At 10.15am (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was up 15.4 points, or 0.49 per cent, at 3160.9, while the broader All Ordinaries index had gained 14.5 points, or 0.47 per cent, to 3126.2. 'The lead from the US was neutral but there was a lot of negative sentiment,' MS Global head of CFDs Anthony Anderson said. 'What was positive was that BHP and Rio had a positive run both in London and the US and commodity prices were okay.' 'China also reaffirmed its 8 per cent growth and that's made people more comfortable with the miners. 'On the other hand the banks were a little disappointing.' The big miners started the morning stronger, with market leader BHP Billiton up 4.59 per cent to $28.92 and rival Rio Tinto up 2.08 per cent to $46.65.  Energy stocks were also higher, with Santos up 30 cents at $15.50, Woodside adding 27 cents to $35.82 and Oil Search steady at $4.81.   The big banks were mixed.  NAB was down 15 cents at $16.65, Commonwealth Bank lost 35 cents to $26.65, Westpac fell 7 cents to $15.63, and ANZ added 8 cents to $12.44.  Gold stocks were higher.   Newcrest was up $1.02 to $32.15, Newmont Mining gained 23 cents to $6.31, and Lihir Gold rose 12 cents to $3.12.   At 10.38am, the spot price of gold in Sydney was $US936.35 per fine ounce, up $US1.80 on Friday's local close of $USS938.15.   On Wall Street on Friday stocks gained some lost ground after a jobs report was not as soft as many traders had feared.  The US unemployment rate rose 0.5 percentage points to 8.1 per cent in February as firms shed 651,000 workers during the month.  The Dow Jones industrial average added 32.5, or 0.49 per cent, to 6626.94, the S&amp;P 500 index gained 0.83, or 0.12 per cent, to 683.38, while the Nasdaq composite index fell 5.74, or 0.44 per cent, to 1293.85.  Making news today, Rio Tinto says further development of the Oyu Tolgoi joint venture in Mongolia needs to be carefully planned amid the economic downturn, as the company awaits an investment agreement from the government.  Shares in Nexus Energy have been placed in a trading halt while the company assesses proposals regarding possible asset sales. It last traded at 38 cents.  Printing group PMP is offering shareholders with small parcels of shares the chance to sell their stock without incurring brokerage fees.  Its stock was up half a cent to 29 cents.   On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract was 10 points higher at 3156 on a volume of 5443 contracts.
