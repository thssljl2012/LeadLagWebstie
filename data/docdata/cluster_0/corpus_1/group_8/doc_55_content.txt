SYDNEY, March 9 (Reuters) - Australian shares gained 0.3 percent on Monday, lifted by strength in the top miners, though gains were capped by fresh weakness in financials, including the nation's top lender National Australia Bank. The benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index gained 9 points to 3,154.5, based on the latest available data. New Zealand's benchmark NZX 50 index finished down 0.1 percent at 2,469.2.
