Australia shares set to Citi to lift banksBy: AFX 10 Mar 2009 05:45 PM ET Text Size SYDNEY, March 11 (Reuters) - Australian stocks are set to open higher on Wednesday, with financial firms likely to gain after Citigroup announced it was profitable in the first two months of 2009. The comments by Citi (see helped the Dow Jones jump 5.8 percent and gave  stocks their best day in four months. Australian March share price index (SPI) futures rose 99 points to 3,266 by 2118 GMT -- representing a 81.5 point premium to the underlying benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index close of 3,184.50 on Tuesday. Australian banks such as Commonwealth Bank of Australia and National Australia Bank will be in the the spotlight following Citigroup's announcement. In economic news, housing finance January figures will be released at 11:30 am (0030 GMT) and the Westpac/Melbourne Institute consumer sentiment index is due at 10:30 am.
