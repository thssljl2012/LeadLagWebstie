NEW YORK - Investors struggled overnight to turn Wall Street's best performance this year into a two-day advance.  Stocks fluctuated in afternoon trading as caution returned to the market after a day of furious buying.  Shortly before close, the Dow Jones industrial average had risen 54.96, or 0.79 per cent, to 6,981.45.  The S&amp;P 500 index had added 7.75, or 1.08 per cent, to 727.35, while the Nasdaq composite index had gained 23.77, or 1.76 per cent, to 1,382.21.  LONDON - European stock markets turned in a mixed performance, with the London FTSE 100 index shedding 21.42 points, or 0.58 per cent, to close at 3,693.81 points.  FRANKFURT - Germany's DAX was 27.12 points, or 0.7 per cent, higher at 3,914.1.  PARIS - The CAC-40 in France was up 10.52 points, or 0.39 per cent, at 2,674.2.  TOKYO - Japan's Nikkei average jumped 4.6 per cent, posting its biggest rise in six weeks a day after hitting a 26-year closing low, as financial shares gained after Citigroup's chief executive said the US bank was making profits.  The benchmark Nikkei gained 321.14 points to 7,376.12, marking its biggest one-day percentage gain since January 27 and coming right after it ended Tuesday at 7,054.98, its lowest close since October 1982.  HONG KONG - The Hang Seng Index closed up 236.61 points, or 2.02 per cent, at 11,930.66.
