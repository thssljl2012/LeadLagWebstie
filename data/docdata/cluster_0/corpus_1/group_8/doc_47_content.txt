(Adds QBE)  SYDNEY, March 9 (Reuters) - Australian shares briefly fell over 2 percent on Monday after a number of erroneous trades in insurer QBE , before the trades were cancelled and the index returned to trading flat.  At 0353 GMT, the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index  was down 0.03 percent or 1 point, at 3,144.5.  New Zealand's benchmark NZX 50 index  was down 0.2 percent to 2,466.7.  STOCKS ON THE MOVE  * Shares in QBE fell 3.9 percent to A$15.66, after plunging due to a number of erroneous trades, including some at as low A$0.004.  0355 GMT  * The majority of financial stocks fell, led by a 4.4 percent fall in NAB to A$16.06. No. 3 lender, Commonwealth Bank of Australia  was down 1.5 percent at A$26.60.  0215 GMT  * Fortescue Metals Group Ltd  rose 2.8 percent to A$2.57, off highs of A$2.66 earlier in the day, after it announced heavy rains this month across northwestern Australia's iron ore belt had impacted production.
