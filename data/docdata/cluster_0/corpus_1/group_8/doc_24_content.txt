MELBOURNE, March 12 (Reuters) - Australian stocks rose 0.7  percent on Thursday with National Australia Bank leading  the market higher following a well-received strategy update.  The benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index rose 23.4 points to  National Australia Bank climbed 4.5 percent to A$17.03. The  nation's top lender announced a 25 percent dividend cut, but  analysts said the market was happy the update did not contain any  more bad news.  New Zealand's benchmark NZX 50 index rose 2.1 points,  or 0.1 percent, to 2,495.4.
