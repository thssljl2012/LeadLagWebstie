THE share market was around 1.5 per cent lower at noon ahead of the Reserve Bank of Australia's interest rate decision, after diversified financials and transport stocks helped the market claw back some lost ground.  At 12.00pm (AEDT), the benchmark S&amp;P/ASX200 index was down 49.4 points, or 1.52 per cent, at 3200.7.  The broader All Ordinaries index lost 50.4 points or 1.57 per cent to 3153.4.  Resources, materials and the banking sector were lower following Wall Street's 4.2 per cent plunge overnight after the US government injected $US30 billion to prevent the collapse of insurance giant American International Group (AIG).  BHP Billiton fell 60 cents, or 2.15 per cent to $27.35, while Rio Tinto lost 78 cents or 1.76 per cent to $43.45.  NAB lost 10 cents, or 0.57 per cent, to $17.41, Commonwealth Bank shed 57 cents, or 2 per cent, to $27.94.  Westpac sank 19 cents to $16.11, and ANZ was down 7 cents to $13.03.  Suncorp Metway shares lost 3.18 per cent, to $4.56 on a downgrade by Moody's Investor Services to the bank's long-term deposit and debt ratings. 'Across the board, we are down but as the day goes on there are a few glimmers of hope,'' ABN AMRO Morgan's director of equities, Bill Chatterton said. 'Toll Holdings is quite strong and has a good balance sheet,'' he said.  Toll added 22 cents or 4.19 per cent to $5.47, while Brambles put on 18 cents or 4.2 per cent to $4.47.  CSL gained $1.25 or 3.63 per cent to $35.70.  The RBA will release its decision on the official interest rate at 2.30pm AEDT. It was expected to cut by between 25 and 50 basis points, Mr Chatterton said.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the March share price index futures contract was down 42 points to 3190 on a volume of 16,078 contracts.
