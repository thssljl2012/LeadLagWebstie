MEXICO CITY, March 10 (Reuters) - Mexico's peso firmed sharply on Tuesday and stocks jumped after Citigroup said it was profitable in the first two months of this year, easing fears about the U.S. banking sector. The peso  gained as much as 1.1 percent to 15.32 per dollar, bouncing back from a record low hit on Monday.  The peso has lost more than a third of its value against the dollar since August as investors have grown increasingly pessimistic about the Mexican economy as the U.S. recession deepens.  Evidence that the depreciation of the currency is feeding inflation is likely to prevent the central bank from further interest rate cuts at its next monthly meeting on March 20.  Mexican stocks have been battered by similar sentiment but some analysts now believe the slide in the market has been overdone.  UBS raised its rating on Tuesday on Mexican stocks to its 'top pick' in Latin America, saying battered share prices already price in the possibility of a severe recession in 2009. Mexican bonds managed to post gains despite the increasing likelihood that the central bank will not cut interest rates this month. The yield on Mexico's benchmark 10-year government bond  fell 6 basis points to 8.78 percent.
