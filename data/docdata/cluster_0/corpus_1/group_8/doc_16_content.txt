 The  plunged more than 5% as a 12.5bn cash call from HSBC sparked a major banking sell-off.  The index fell below the trading low of 3665 seen in October last year to hit levels not seen since April 2003.  In New York Wall Street fell below the psychologically important level of 7000.  The heavy Footsie falls put the top-flight index in sight of the 3287 nadir reached in March 2003.  That date marked the worst depths the market plunged to following the dot.com crash at the turn of the decade.  David Jones, chief market strategist at IG Index 'Today's move to beneath the previous low raises the real prospect of another significant tumble, with 2003 lows of around 3300 a popular target in the medium term.' The Footsie was driven lower by news of a record 12.5bn rights issue from Europe's biggest bank, HSBC.  The call for funds from one of the world's safest banks - as well as a 62% slide in annual profits - triggered a sell-off across the sector.  HSBC fell 20% - wiping around 12bn off the bank's value - and was followed by double-digit losses for , which slid 11%. Barclays fell almost 7% and  - nearly 70% taxpayer-owned - was 4% lower.  Wall Street's Dow Jones Industrial Average slid below 7,000 points for the first time since 1997, tumbling 164.87 points (2.33 per cent) to 6,898.06.  The market fell after a new bailout was announced for insurance giant AIG.  Figures showed the US economy contracted at its fastest pace for more than a quarter of a century - shrinking by a mammoth 6.2% in the final three months of last year.  All of Asia's markets were lower overnight, with Japan's Nikkei and Hong Kong's Hang Seng finishing almost 4% lower as fears over the worsening US economy also hit confidence.
