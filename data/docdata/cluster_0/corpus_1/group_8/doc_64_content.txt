SYDNEY, March 18 (Reuters) - Australian shares rose on  Wednesday to a one-month high led by bank stocks such as  Commonwealth Bank of Australia, but gains were capped by  losses in global miners such as Rio Tinto.  The benchmark S&amp;P/ASX 200 index climbed 21.2 points  to 3,473.1 by 2307 GMT. It closed at one-month high of 3,451.9  points on Tuesday.  New Zealand's benchmark NZX 50 index rose 1.8 percent  to 2,610.0.
