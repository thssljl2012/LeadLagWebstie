SYDNEY, Nov 20 (Reuters) - Australian shares fell 3.5 percent  on Thursday as concerns about a prolonged global recession  triggered sharp losses in leading miners BHP Billiton  and Rio Tinto.  The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX 200 index fell 124.3 points to  its weakest since February 2004. The index is down 47 percent so  far this year.  New Zealand's benchmark NZX 50 index ended down 2.3  percent at 2,644.7.  STOCKS ON THE MOVE  * Property related stocks went against the downtrend after  weeks of declines. AustraLand rose 12.3 percent to  A$0.36, while real estate trust Dexus Property Group  gained 2.3 percent to A$0.90 and property developer Stockland  Group was up 2.5 percent at A$3.63.  'They have been hit so hard over the last month or two, and  all the markets are expecting more interest cuts coming, and big  ones,' said Martin Angel, dealer at Patersons Securities Ltd.  0439 GMT  * Oz Minerals Ltd's, the world's second-largest zinc  miner and a major supplier of copper, fell 12.7 percent to  A$0.55, joining some of its larger peers as worries persisted  about the global commodities market.  The company warned investors on Wednesday of lower profits  and possible asset writedowns.  0307 GMT  * Companies geared to global growth such as miners BHP  Billiton and its takeover target Rio Tinto were hit hard on  worries a worldwide economic slowdown would signal an end to the  global commodities boom.  BHP tumbled 8.4 percent to A$21.26 and Rio Tinto lost 10  percent to A$59.35.  0001 GMT  * Banks fell on concerns they may need to further increase  provisions due to their exposure to troubled companies such  Centro Properties Group and Babcock&amp;amp;Brown Ltd . Babcock shares were placed on trading halt due to a  dispute the company had with a bank..  National Australia Bank Ltd, Australia's top lender,  slipped 4.5 percent to A$18.79, Commonwealth Bank of Australia  Ltd, the No. 2 lender, lost 4.1 percent to A$29.67.  0002 GMT  * Miner Territory Resources fell 31 percent to  A$0.10 after it said it was facing difficulties selling iron ore  on the spot market for delivery in early December because of  declining demand from China's steel mills.  0003 GMT  * Australian infrastructure firm SP Ausnet added 0.9  percent to A$1.18 after reporting a 2.4 percent rise in  underlying first-half net profit..  0003 GMT  * Food group Goodman Fielder Ltd fell 3.9 percent to  A$1.47 after forecasting a 15 percent fall in its first-half  profit.
