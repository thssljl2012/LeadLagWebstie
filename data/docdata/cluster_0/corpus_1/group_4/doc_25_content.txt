Article from: AAP  Font size: Decrease Increase  : Email  : Print  THE share market has opened relatively flat this morning, after a mixed session on Wall St failed to provide a solid direction for local shares.  On the Sydney Futures Exchange, the December share price index contract was up 26 points at 3645 on volume of 10,952 contracts.  BHP Billiton has abandoned its multi-billion-dollar hostile tilt for rival Rio Tinto Ltd due to the turmoil in global financial and commodity markets and Rio's high debt.  BHP Billiton and Rio Tinto shares were in adjustment, with BHP last closing at $26.22 and Rio at $63.90.  Among the banks, Commonwealth Bank fell $1.01, or 2.97 per cent, to $32.99, National Australia Bank dropped 19 cents to $19.68, ANZ lost 43 cents to $14.12 and Westpac declined 38 cents to $17.45.  Wall Street showed some signs of stability on Tuesday as investors, heartened by US government plans to aid consumer lending companies, selectively bought more stocks following a huge two-day rally.  The Dow Jones closed up 36.08 points, or 0.43 per cent, to 8,479.47, while the Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index added 5.58 points, or 0.66 per cent, to 857.39.  The Nasdaq lost 7.29 points, or 0.5 per cent, to 1,464.73.
