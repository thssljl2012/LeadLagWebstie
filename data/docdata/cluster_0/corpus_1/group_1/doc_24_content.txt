 The Australian stock market fell in morning trading as losses by banks outweighed early gains by miners.The benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 was recently off 38.1 points, or 0.8%, at 4891.4.The market was initially pushed higher by BHP Billiton, which gained as much as $1.08, or 2.8%, to $39.81, while Fortescue Metals Group added as much as 19 cents, or 2.6%, to $7.49.Among companies reporting results, Fairfax Media gave up early gains, losing as much as 7 cents, or 2.5%, to $2.71 after earlier advancing 9 cents to $2.87.Among banks to fall were Commonwealth Bank of Australia, which lost 35 cents to $40.75, while National Australia Bank dropped 39 cents to $23.71.NAB announced this morning that it would pass on a full 0.25 percentage point rate cut, if the Reserve bank cuts by that amount at its meeting in September.ANZ declined 22 cents to $15.81, Westpac lost 24 cents to $22.13, while the latter's takeover target, St George, fell 30 cents to $28.48.Overnight, Wall Street scored a moderate gain after a volatile session that saw the major indices buffeted by the price of oil and worries about the financial sector.The Dow Jones industrial average rose 68.88 points, or 0.61%, to 11,417.43. The Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index rose 7.85, or 0.62%, to 1,274.54, while the Nasdaq composite index rose 4.72, or 0.20%, to 2,389.08.
