 The Australian stock market is expected to open the week stronger, after US stocks rebounded sharply on Friday.Wall Street's rally was a result of a surge in the dollar and a drop in oil prices.The Australian share market closed flat on Friday with the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 index up 2.9 points, or 0.06 per cent, to 4986.2 and the broader All Ordinaries gaining 7.6 points, or 0.15 per cent, to 5037.6.The September share price index futures contract rose 72 points to 5034 on a volume of 2,006 contracts.Commonwealth Securities senior analyst Craig James said he expected the volatility in the Australian market to continue next week.'The Dow Jones was up over 300 points and that sets up a good day for our market in a broader sense although there are going to be a raft of winners and losers,' Mr James said.'The oil price being down $5 a barrel is certainly going to weigh down the energy sector, although banks should continue to do well.'The Dow closed 302.89 higher, or 2.65 per cent, to 11,734.32 and the Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index advanced 30.25, or 2.39 per cent, to 1,296.32.The technology-heavy Nasdaq composite index gained 58.37 points, or 2.48 per cent, to 2,414.10.Mr James said all eyes were going to be on the Reserve Bank's quarterly statement of monetary policy on Monday, as the chatter about a cut in interest rates continues.The Westpac/Melbourne Institute index of consumer sentiment and the Australian Bureau of Statistic labour price index for June quarter will be released on Wednesday.Amongst those reporting their annual results this week are Commonwealth Bank of Australia, Telstra, Specialty Fashion Group, Leighton Holdings and Futuris Corp. 2008 AAPBrought to you byWhen news happens:send photos, videos &amp;amp; tip-offs to 0424 SMS SMH (+61 424 767 764), or us.11:29AM Crowds gather for rally at Murray River11:28AM Overseas gains may push Aussie stocks up11:23AM Non-bank lenders won't disappear: Bowen
