 THE share market has opened stronger, buoyed by the big miners and financials, after US stocks gained for a third session overnight.At 10.15am (AEST), the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 was up 65 points, or 1.28 per cent to 5131.5, while the broader All Ordinaries lifted 61.6 points, or 1.2 per cent to 5204.9. The September share price index futures contract was up 64 points to 5123 on volume of 6179 contracts. Locally, BHP Billiton gained 26 cents, or 0.62 per cent, to $42.01 and takeover target Rio Tinto added 96 cents, or 0.76 per cent, to $127.46. The banks were stronger. Commonwealth Bank of Australia gained 94 cents to $42.04, Westpac rose 43 cents to $23.57, National Australia Bank was up 58 cents to $24.58 and ANZ lifted 34 cents to $16.54. Westpac takeover target St George was up 60 cents to $30.35. Making news this morning, property investor Centro Properties Group says it is making incremental steps to stabilise the group after booking an annual net loss of $2.06 billion for 2007/08. At 10.19am AEST, Centro was up two cents, or 10.81 per cent, to 20.5 cents. Allco Finance Group reported a net loss of $1.731 billion for the 2008/09 year and says it will restructure to focus on its core capabilities by selling non-core assets and exiting some businesses. Allco fell 0.5 cents, or 1.27 per cent, to 39 cents. Wall Street barreled higher yesterday after a better-than-expected reading on the gross domestic product and a drop in jobless claims gave investors some reassurance that the economy is holding up. The Dow Jones Industrial Average gained 212.67 points, or 1.85 per cent, to 11,715.18. The Nasdaq added 29.18 points, or 1.22 per cent, to 2,411.64 and the broad-market Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index increased 19.02 points, or 1.48 per cent, to ,300.68.
