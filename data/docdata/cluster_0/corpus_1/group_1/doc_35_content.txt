 The Australian dollar closed firmer for the third day in a row as reports the American government was considering bailing out the US's two biggest mortgage backers turned traders off the US currency.At 1700 AEST, the Australian dollar was trading at $US0.8786/90, up from Thursday's close of 0.8745/48.During the day, the currency moved between a low of $US0.8761 and a high of 0.8805.The Australian dollar rallied, climbing above $US0.8800 on Friday morning for the first time since August 12, as traders questioned the US currency's recent rally.The US dollar faced renewed selling pressure in the afternoon as media reports suggested the US Treasury was considering a bailout of US mortgage backers Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac.Financial markets research group Forecast senior currency analyst Lee Wai Tuck said the news sparked renewed worries about the US financial sector, turning traders off the US currency. 'There are concerns the (US) Treasury may be forced to intervene to prop up the companies,' Mr Lee said from Singapore. 'We saw some renewed concerns about the US banking sector. 'Because of that, investor sentiment has been affected.' Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac own or guarantee about $US5.2 trillion ($A5.96 trillion) in loans, about 40 per cent of the total value of American home loans.Mr Lee said the Australian and New Zealand US dollars, and the euro, benefited as traders questioned the viability of the US dollar's recent rally. 'They just want to have a position adjustment, so they're buying back the Aussie and the Kiwi and their recent falls,' he said. At 1600 AEST, the Reserve Bank of Australia's trade weighted index (TWI) was at 68.5, up from Thursday's close of 68.2.  2008 AAP Brought to you by
