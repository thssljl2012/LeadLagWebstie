 THE share market has opened marginally higher, lifted by the big miners but restrained by financials after US stocks declined overnight on continued bad news from the financial sector.At 10.17am (AEST), the benchmark S&amp;amp;P/ASX200 was 18.8 points higher, or 0.38 per cent to 4884.8, while the broader All Ordinaries added 20.3 points, or 0.41 per cent to 4950.7. The September share price index futures contract lost seven points to 4855 on volume of 5477 contracts. Mining giant BHP Billiton increased $1.03, or 2.78 per cent, to $38.03, while its takeover target, Rio Tinto, gained $3.32, or 2.98 per cent to $114.65. The Australian financial sector suffered. Commonwealth Bank of Australia fell 14 cents to $40.86, while National Australia Bank dropped eleven cents to $23.99. ANZ declined 19 cents to $15.96, Westpac lost 26 cents to $22.00, while the latter's takeover target, St George, fell 61 cents, or 2.09 per cent, to $28.51. Overnight, US stocks fell after wholesale prices rose faster than economists estimated, housing starts fell and concern grew that the nation's biggest financial firms would report more losses. The Dow Jones industrial average dropped 130.84 points, or 1.14 per cent, to 11,348.55, the Standard &amp;amp; Poor's 500 index fell 11.91 points to 1266.69, and the Nasdaq composite index fell 32.62 points to 2384.36.
