For nearly a half-hour yesterday, DeMaurice Smith spoke like what he used to be -- a lawyer. Then, in an unexpected moment of candor, he spoke like what he'd just become -- a union leader.  'There isn't a day I don't hope for peace, but there isn't a day we won't prepare for war,' Smith, the new head of the NFL Players Association, said from Maui, where the previous night he had been elected on the first ballot to replace the late Gene Upshaw as the union's executive director.
