  UPDATED: 3:53 pm MST March 5, 2009  SCOTTSDALE, Ariz. -- Scottsdale police are on the lookout for a man who robbed a bank Thursday morning.  According to police, the man walked into the Bank of America branch inside Frys Supermarket along Thomas Road at around 9:45 a.m.  The man demanded money and left with an undisclosed amount of cash, leaving behind a brown paper bag.  Scottsdale police arrived and evacuated the bank and the supermarket.  The bomb squad was called to the scene, and members of the squad found that the bag contained an improvised explosive device, according to the department.  Scottsdale police describe the man as white, 40-50 years old, with a scruffy beard. He was wearing a gray T-shirt at the time of the robbery.  Copyright 2009 by KPHO.com. All rights reserved.
