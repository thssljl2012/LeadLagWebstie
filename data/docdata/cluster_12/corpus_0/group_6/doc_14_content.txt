HUNDREDS of Australian jobs could be at risk after the chairman of an Indian company confessed this week to committing a massive fraud.- Massive fraud confession- Australian IT jobs at risk- NAB, Qantas, Telstra among customersB. Ramalinga Raju, the chairman and founder of Hyderabad-based IT firm Satyam, told company directors on Wednesday that he had falsified 50 billion rupees ($1.4 billion) of profit over several years."It was like riding a tiger, not knowing how to get off without being eaten," Mr Raju said in a letter of resignation.Satyam employs 1700 people at offices in Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane and counts among its customers some of Australia's biggest companies, including Qantas, National Australia Bank and Telstra, all of whom outsource IT jobs to Satyam in India.The affair casts a shadow over the increasingly popular practice of companies outsourcing work overseas.more at http://www.theage.com.au/national/indian-it-company-scandal-threatens-jobs-20090108-7cwa.html
