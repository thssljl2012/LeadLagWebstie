 Jan 23 (Reuters) - Industrial distributor Interline Brands Inc (IBI.N  The company, which had earlier forecast fourth-quarter earnings of 30 cents to 35 cents a share, projected sales of about $277 million for the quarter.  Interline, a provider of maintenance, repair and operation products, expects the workforce reduction and other cost-cutting actions to result in charges of about $1 million in the first quarter. It expects related annualized savings of about $12 million.  The company also announced plans of consolidating 10 distribution centers over the next six months and expects to incur severance and other charges of about $1.0 million in the first half of 2009. It also sees related annualized savings of about $4 million.  Shares of the Jacksonville, Florida-based company closed at $8.46 Thursday on the New York Stock Exchange.
