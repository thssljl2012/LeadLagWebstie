(RTTNews) - U.S. Bancorp (USB), the holding company for U.S. Bank that provides commercial banking and financial services, Wednesday reported a sharp decline in fourth-quarter profit, hurt by higher credit losses and securities impairments. Earnings for the quarter missed Wall Street analysts' forecast. The bank's quarterly revenues were up 1.4% on the back of an increase in net interest income.   For the fourth quarter, the bank's net income was $330 million, down from $942 million last year. Net income applicable to common equity declined to $260 million, or $0.15 per share, from $927 million, or $0.53 per share, in the prior-year quarter. For the sequentially preceding quarter, the company posted net income applicable to common equity of $557 million, or $0.32 per share.  Significant items included in 2008 fourth-quarter results were $253 million, or $0.09 per share, of securities and other market valuation losses. In addition, the company increased the allowance for credit losses by recording $635 million, or $0.09 per share, provision for credit losses in excess of net charge-offs. In total, significant items reduced earnings per share by about $0.34.  On average, nineteen analysts polled by First Call/Thomson financial expected the company to earn $0.22 per share for the quarter. Analysts' estimates typically exclude special items.U.S. Bancorp's total net revenue on a taxable-equivalent basis rose in the fourth quarter to $3.62 billion from $3.57 billion last year, reflecting a 22.6% increase in net interest income and a 19.2% decline in noninterest income. Net interest income for the quarter was $2.12 billion, up from $1.74 billion in the comparable quarter a year ago. Total non-interest income declined to $1.46 billion from $1.81 billion in the prior-year quarter.   Twelve analysts had a consensus revenue estimate of $3.78 billion for the quarter. The company's total third-quarter net revenue on a taxable-equivalent basis was $3.38 billion.  Commenting on the results, U.S. Bancorp Chairman, President and Chief Executive Officer Richard Davis said, 'The results were marked by outstanding growth in loans and deposits and an expanded net interest margin, but tempered by the unfavorable impact of higher credit losses and securities impairments.'For fiscal 2008, the bank earned $2.95 billion, lower than $4.32 billion reported last year. Net income applicable to common equity declined to $2.82 billion, or $1.61 per share, from $4.26 billion, or $2.43 per share, a year ago. Full-year net interest income was $7.73 billion, compared with $6.69 billion in the previous year. Total noninterest income fell to $6.81 billion from $7.3 billion in fiscal 2007. Street analysts were looking for earnings of $1.72 per share on revenues of $14.87 billion.   Among others in the sector, Bank of America Corp. (BAC) on January 16 reported a net loss for the fourth quarter compared to a profit in the year-ago period. The company, which made two key acquisition decisions in 2008, noted that quarterly results were driven by escalating credit costs, including additions to reserves, and significant write-downs and trading losses in the capital markets businesses.
