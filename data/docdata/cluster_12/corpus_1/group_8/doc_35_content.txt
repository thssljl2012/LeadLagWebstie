U.S. Bancorp CEO Richard Davis declined a $1.3 million cash bonus in 2008, according to filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission.  Davis received a compensation package of $5 million in 2008, up from $4.4 million in 2007. Most of Davis pay came from restricted stock and option awards, some of them granted in 2008 instead of cash bonuses for the banks 2007 performance. Davis, who also is chairman and president of Minneapolis-based U.S. Bancorp (NYSE: USB), received a base salary of $900,034, stock awards of $271,660 and option awards of $3.6 million. The rest of his compensation came from change in pension value and other items. U.S. Banks profits for 2008 dropped to $3.4 billion from $4.1 billion in 2007. Its stock also fell from $32 at the end of 2007 to $24 per share on Dec. 31.  William Chenevich, the banks vice chairman of technology and operations services, received $5.4 million for his 2008 pay package, up from $3.4 million in 2007. Lee Mitau, executive vice president and general counsel, received $3.5 million in 2008, compared to $3 million in 2007. Richard Hartnack, the banks vice chairman of consumer banking, made $2.6 million, up from $1.8 million in 2007. Andrew Cecere, vice chairman and chief financial officer, made $2.6 million in 2008, up from $2 million in 2007.  U.S. Bank, a subsidiary of U.S. Bancorp, has 190 branches in Oregon. It is the largest bank in Oregon, with deposits of more than $5.9 billion, according to the Portland Business Journals 2009 Book of Lists.
