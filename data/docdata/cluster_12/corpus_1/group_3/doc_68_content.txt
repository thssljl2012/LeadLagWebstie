The Intesa office in Ho Chi Minh City will be the companys first in Vietnam, the State Bank of Vietnam said in a statement posted Monday on its website.Milan-based Intesa will do market research, develop projects and follow up on contracts with Vietnamese companies, the statement said.Standard Chartered, HSBC Holdings and Australia and New Zealand Banking Group this year gained approval from the Vietnamese government to run fully foreign-owned units.A total of 29 overseas banks have branches and another 38 foreign lenders have opened offices in Vietnam, according to the central banks website.
