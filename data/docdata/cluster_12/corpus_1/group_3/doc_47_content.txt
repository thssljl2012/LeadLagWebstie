By George dela CruzContributor  THE Employee Union members and management of the Corazon Locsin Montelibano Memorial Regional Hospital (CLMMRH), a Department of Health-run medical institution, recently signed a new collective bargaining agreement (CBA).  The CBA grants the mandated wages and benefits under the Magna Carta for Public Health Workers, which will benefit some 448 regular employees and a hundred casual workers. The workers will also avail of P10,000 benefit as signing bonus, said CLMMRH Chief Administrative officer and CLMMRH Employees Union's (CLMMRH-EU) Bryan Baylon. The hospital's management has provided the use of four additional rooms for the employees and another four rooms for their dependents which will be used as personnel ward during the latter's hospitalization. A grievance committee was also set up to serve as a hearing body between the management and the employees. Both CBA parties also agreed to have a clear-cut presentation during the Bids and Awards Committee (BAC) bidding.
