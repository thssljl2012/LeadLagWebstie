The Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange (ADX) dipped further below the critical support line of 2,400 points. The ADX General Index lost an insignifcant 0.19%, closing at 2,349.61 points. Arkan Buildinig Materials posted the biggest loss on the second consecutive day and closed almost ten percent lower. Financial bellwether National Bank of Abu Dhabi (NBAD) gained 3.84%. Oil leaeder Taqa dipped by 7.88%. Oil prices stabilized around $45 a barrel. Nine tenth of the UAE oil reserves are in the emirate of Abu Dhabi. According to the latest emerging markets report issued by Bank of America Merrill Lynch, falling oil prices remain the biggest risk for the GCC economies. Trading volumes were up little (84m shares changed hands) at the ADX.
