NEW YORK  A sharp rebound in bank shares and easing worries about the economy pushed stocks to their best week since late November.  Fears that the nations major financial institutions would collapse or require more government lifelines to stay alive lessened during the week. Market veterans were quick to rein in hopes that stocks would chart an easy recovery, but many still saw the four straight days of gains as a good sign.  The overriding question people have is, Is this rally it? ' said Quincy Krosby, chief investment strategist at The Hartford. For that to happen, I think we need to see more evidence of a turnaround. We still have significant problems in terms of unemployment. The problems with the banks are still there.  On Friday, the Dow Jones industrial average rose 53.92, or 0.8 percent, to 7,223.98. The Dow hasnt put up four straight gains since late November.  The Standard&amp;Poors 500 index rose 5.81, or 0.8 percent, to 756.55. The Nasdaq composite index rose 5.40, or 0.4 percent, to 1,431.50.  For the week, the Dow jumped 9 percent, the S&amp;P 500 index added 10.7 percent and the Nasdaq rose 10.6 percent. It was the best week for the major indexes since the week that ended Nov. 28.  Financial stocks mostly rose Friday after reports that Citigroup Chairman Richard Parsons said the bank doesnt need additional government support. Bank of America Corp. and JPMorgan Chase&amp;Co. also said theyve been profitable this year.  Meanwhile, a government report on retail sales for February wasnt as bad as many analysts had feared.
