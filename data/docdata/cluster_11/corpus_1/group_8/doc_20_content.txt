JPMorgan Chase and US Bancorp extend Thursday's decline; Wells Fargo shoots higher after dividend cut. NEW YORK (CNNMoney.com) -- A day after Citigroup stock dipped below $1, bank stocks endured another selloff Friday, even as investors applauded Wells Fargo's plans to cut its dividend.Shares of JPMorgan Chase (JPM, Fortune 500) and US Bancorp (USB, Fortune 500) were lower in midday trading, falling 7% and 2% respectively. Two of the hardest hit institutions - Citigroup (C, Fortune 500) and Bank of America (BAC, Fortune 500) - remained little changed from Thursday's close.Bucking the trend was Wells Fargo (WFC, Fortune 500), whose shares gained 6%. The San Francisco-based banking giant unveiled plans before Friday's opening bell to cut its quarterly dividend 85%. The company said it expected the move to save $5 billion annually and help the firm grow its market share at a time when some of its peers are receiving extensive assistance from the government. 0:00 /3:20'This was a very difficult decision but it's absolutely right for our Company and our shareholders,' Wells Fargo CEO John Stumpf said in a statement.Ongoing fears about the health of the nation's largest banks have weighed not only on financial stocks, but the broader market. Major indexes finished at their lowest levels in nearly 12 years Thursday, hurt, in part, by worries about the big banks.
