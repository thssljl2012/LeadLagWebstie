Stocks on the move , Real-Time Equity news U.S. stock market report 0850 ET 10March2009-JPMorgan sees bottom for S&amp;P 500 at 650-600 ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ JPMorgan on Tuesday forecast a 'major S&amp;P 500 bottom in the 650-600 zone' in the upcoming weeks, which it said would arguably end the 2007 and 2000 secular bear markets 'in a base case scenario.' The firm also wrote that 'from a low in the mid-low 600s, we still expect a significant percentage rally in the second half of the year, targeting 950-1000 by year-end.' The index closed Monday at 676.53. Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 0840 ET 10March2009-Credit Suisse cuts price target on Merck ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Credit Suisse on Tuesday cut its price target on Merck &amp; Co, dropping it 20 percent to $24 after the pharmaceutical company said it would buy Schering-Plough for $41.1 billion. Though it cut its target, Credit Suisse said the deal made both strategic and financial sense. Shares of Merck rose 1.1 percent to $21.23 in premarket trade. Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 0820 ET 10March2009-Google, Cisco eyed as possible Dow candidates ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Analysts say U.S. bank Citigroup and automaker General Motors are most likely to be replaced on the Dow Jones Industrial Average. But there is little consensus among investors about the type of company that should be in the 30-stock blue-chip index. The list of contenders includes companies like Google and Cisco and former Dow star US Steel. For details, see Reuters Messaging: deepa.seetharaman.reuters.com@reuters.net 0824 ET 10March2009-General Growth asks bondholders for leeway ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ U.S. mall owner General Growth Properties late Monday began asking holders of $2.25 billion of bonds for nine more months of breathing room as it moves to refinance its debt. Warning that it may seek bankruptcy protection, it launched a so-called solicitation process to obtain consent from holders of unsecured notes, asking for consent to hold off on exercising remedies in case of defaults until Dec. 31, 2009. Shares of General Growth closed Monday at 38 cents. Reuters Messaging: ryan.vlastelica.reuters.com@reuters.net 0819 ET 10March2009-Roche says $93/share bid for Genentech is fair ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ Roche on Tuesday said that it deemed its $93 per share offer for Genentech was fair, after a source familiar with the situation said the two sides were in talks for a deal at a higher price. The source on Monday said that Roche was in talks to buy the 44 percent of Genentech that it doesn't already own for $95 per share. Shares of Genentech rose 0.5 percent to $93.13 in premarket trade.
