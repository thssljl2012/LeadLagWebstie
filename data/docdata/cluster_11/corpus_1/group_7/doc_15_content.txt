LONDON (SHARECAST) - US stocks closed little changed with investors adopting a wait-and-see attitude ahead of the vote on President Obama's stimulus plan.   Dow Jones closed up 9 at 8,270. Nasdaq was unchanged at 1,591 while the S&amp;P 500 added 1 at 869.   The Senate's compromise stimulus plan will be debated on the floor on Monday, with a vote expected on Tuesday.   Investors piled into banks ahead of a speech by Treasury Secretary Timothy Geithner on Tuesday that will outline the new plan for the bank bailout.   Bank of America and Citigroup both made gains but it was the smaller banks that made most progress. Fifth Third and Huntington Bancshares led the rally.   In other company news, Whirlpool, the world's biggest appliance manufacturer, saw fourth quarter earnings slump as weak economic conditions around the world hit sales.   The US firm said fourth-quarter net earnings decreased by 76% from the same period the previous year to $44m, or $0.60 per diluted share, from $187m, or $2.38 per diluted share. Sales of $4.3bn for the quarter decreased 19% from the $5.3bn reported in the fourth quarter of 2007.   The credit crunch showed little sign of hampering consumers' appetite for fast food January, with burger chain McDonald's reporting a 7.1% rise comparable stores. Comparable sales - which strip out the impact of new store openings - were up by 5.4% in the US, 7.1% in Europe and by 10.2% in the Asia.Pacific, Middle East and Africa area compared with January 2008.   General Motors is in talks to buy back portions of auto parts maker Delphi, which was spun off in the 1990s, according to the Wall Street Journal.   Life assurance group Hartford Financial Services surges ahead on hopes that the government will offer aid to the life assurance sector in the form of a reduction in the reserves requirement.
