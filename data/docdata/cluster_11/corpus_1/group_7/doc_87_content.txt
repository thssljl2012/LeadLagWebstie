(SmarTrend(R) Market Commentary via COMTEX) -- ROH   Quote   Chart   News   PowerRating -- Last Updated: February 9th, 2009: 4:30 PM ET---After a day of cautionary trading the major US equity indices closed near the unchanged level as investors remained hesitant ahead of Tuesday's pivotal Senate vote and details of the new financial rescue plan. The Dow closed slightly lower, off 0.10% to 8,271, with shares of Bank of America (NYSE:BAC) and General Electric (NYSE:GE) providing leadership, up 12.4% and 13.9%, respectively. The Nasdaq closed basically at the unchanged level, just 0.01% lower to 1,592. The S&amp;P 500 closed slightly higher, up 0.2% to 870. The Senate spent the day debating the new compromised stimulus package today and a vote for its approval will be held tomorrow. Republicans and Democrats have been tussling over the details of the package and especially the price tag. The altered financial bailout plan, which was announced over the weekend, is scheduled to be unveiled Tuesday as well by Treasury Department Secretary Tim Geithner who will also reveal how the remaining $350 billion in TARP funds are going to be used. Midday reports from CNBC indicated that the rescue plan may contain components that would allow private investors like hedge funds to hold certain assets backed by the US government. In earnings news, Loews (NYSE:L) reported a Q4 loss from continuing operations of $2.20 per share. The loss included an investment loss and a goodwill impairment loss and may not compare to analyst estimates of a $0.60 per share loss. Net revenues in the quarter fell 23% year-over-year to $2.74 billion. Rohm &amp; Haas (NYSE:ROH) reported Q4 earnings of $0.69 per share, ex-items, beating analyst estimates for earnings of $0.66 per share. Net revenues in the quarter fell 13% year-over-year to $2.03 billion, but came in short of consensus estimates of $2.23 billion. The company is suing Dow Chemical in order to close the $15.4 billion acquisition that Dow has delayed. Nissan (NASDAQ:NSANY) reported a fiscal Q3 loss of 20.42 yen per share, versus last year's net income of 32.32 yen per share. Net revenues in the quarter fell 34.4% year-over-year to 1.817 trillion yen or US $17.65 billion, down from 2.770 trillion yen in the year-ago period. The company lowered its guidance for its fiscal year ending March 31, 2009, to a full-year net loss of 265 billion yen (US$2.58 billion), or 65.06 yen per share, down from its previous forecast for a net profit of 160 billion yen (US$1.51 billion). The Japanese based automaker also said it will cut 20,000 jobs in an effort to curb operating expenses and cope with the rapidly deteriorating auto industry. Amazon.com (NASDAQ:AMZN) released its new Kindle, which is thinner and lighter than its predecessor, but has the same price tag. The revamped electronic book reader, known as the Kindle 2, boasts a more legible font, is half as thick as the first Kindle and has a speech function that will read the books out loud.
