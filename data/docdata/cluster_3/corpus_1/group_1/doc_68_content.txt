 p.m. COLUMBUS, Ohio (AP) - Nationwide Financial Services Inc., which manages company retirement plans, said Wednesday that its second-quarter profit declined 57 percent on investment losses.Separately, Nationwide also announced that its parent company Nationwide Mutual Insurance Co. has agreed to buy it out for $52.25 per Class A share in cash, representing a 13 percent premium over Wednesday's closing price of $46.45. The deal values Nationwide at about $2.4 billion.For the three months ended June 30, the company reported net income of $85.4 million, or 62 cents per share, compared with $197.3 million, or $1.37 per share, in the year-ago period. Results include realized investment losses of $41.5 million, or 29 cents per share, compared with investment losses of $1.2 million, or 1 penny per share, in the prior-year quarter.Operating earnings, which excludes some items, fell 36 percent to $127.6 million, or 92 cents per share, from $200.4 million, or $1.39 per share, in the second quarter of last year.Analysts, whose estimates typically exclude one-time items, expected a profit of $1.15 per share, according to a poll by Thomson Financial.Sales fell 6 percent to $4.27 billion from $4.54 billion.
