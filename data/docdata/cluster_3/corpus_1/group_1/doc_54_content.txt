 NAIROBI (Reuters) - Barclays Bank Kenya said on Friday pretax profits for the first half of 2008 rose 21 percent to 4.295 billion shillings. 'Our pre-tax profit rose 21 percent,' said chief executive Adan Mohammed. 'We realised excellent growth in non-interest income which came in at 4.7 billion compared to 3.4 billion in the first half of 2007.' The bank's 2007 pretax profits were 7.079 billion shillings.Barclays issued a seven-year bond late last year worth a total 5 billion shillings to finance long term lending.
