 Barclays is selling its 6.8bn life assurance business to Swiss Re in yet another sign that UK banks are still keen to raise cash to prop up balance sheets decimated by the credit crunch.The business, which was formed from Barclays and Woolwich life funds, closed for new business in 2001 and will cost Swiss Re 753m cash. Barclays finance director Chris Lucas said it was 'an attractive, high quality business which is not core.The cash will add to the 4.5bn Barclays raised last month through a share placing .Barclays is expected to reveal a 33% fall in its first-half profits on Thursday.
