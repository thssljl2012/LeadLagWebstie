 Anglian Home Improvements, which announced the closure of its Rochdale factory in February, has been taken over by a syndicate of investors. Peter Mottershead, chief executive of the double-glazing firm, will remain in post under the new majority shareholders, who include includes Bank of America Securities, Bank of Scotland, Barclays, BlueBay Investments, Lloyds TSB and the Royal Bank of Scotland. The deal involves a refinancing of the 292m turnover company which will cut its debt by 90m. Alchemy Partners, which took Anglian private in 2001, has retained a minority stake.
