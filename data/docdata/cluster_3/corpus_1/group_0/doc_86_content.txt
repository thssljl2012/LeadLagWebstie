 A south London bank robber who was caught red-handed when a dye mechanism hidden in the bank notes he had stolen leaked all over him will be sentenced.James Reilly, 45, of Wandsworth, was arrested by an off-duty police officer who spotted him leaking the red dye as he attempted to board a bus in Brighton, just minutes after he robbed a nearby branch of the Royal Bank of Scotland.He pleaded guilty to the robbery at Hove Crown Court in May, along with two other robberies, one at a Lloyds TSB in Tooting and one at a Nationwide Building Society in Streatham in February.In all cases he handed a note to the cashier demanding cash and gestured that he was armed and ready to shoot.PC Andrea Leahy, the police officer who confronted Reilly, was commended as she was in plain clothes at the time of the incident in Brighton's Castle Square on March 4.Reilly will be sentenced at Hove Crown Court on Tuesday.Copyright (c) Press Association Ltd. 2008, All Rights Reserved.
