 SYDNEY -(Dow Jones)- Australia and New Zealand Banking Group Ltd. (ANZ) said Wednesday it plans to incorporate a new fully-owned bank in Vietnam and open at least four new outlets in the country by the end of the year.ANZ, which is among the first foreign banks granted approval to incorporate in the country by the State Bank of Vietnam, said it plans to open further outlets in coming years.Chief Executive Mike Smith, a former executive at HSBC, wants to dramatically expand the bank's presence in Asia. He aims to generate around 20% of earnings from Asia by 2012. 'ANZ regards expansion in Vietnam as one of its highest priorities in Asia,' ANZ Group Managing Director for Asia Pacific Alex Thursby said in a statement. 'The newly incorporated bank will extend ANZ's distribution to meet the needs of Vietnam's growing affluent population who are looking for more convenient access to bank branches, as well as products such as mortgage loans, credit cards, car loans and better savings and investments,' Thursby said. ANZ said its plans are subject to regulatory approval.
