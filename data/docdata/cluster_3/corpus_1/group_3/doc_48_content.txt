LONDON - The British government has announced plans to partially nationalize major banks, with taxpayers taking a share stake in a bid to restore stability.The Treasury made the announcement Wednesday before markets opened, saying eight banks have signed up for the so-called recapitalization plan, which offers up to US$87.5 billion in the form of preference shares.The Treasury said the eight banks are Abbey, Barclays, HBOS, HSBC, Lloyds TSB, Nationwide Building Society, Royal Bank of Scotland and Standard Chartered.
