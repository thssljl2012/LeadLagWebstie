US stocks fell on Monday as the Dow plunged one percent, slipping below 7,000 for the first time since 1997 amid fears that the costs of rescuing troubled financial institutions is ballooning. European stocks at major exchanges also fell sharply. In Frankfurt, the Dax fell 3.48 percent, in London, the FTSE lost over five percent. Asian shares fell nearly four percent on Monday. Shares in Europe's largest bank, HSBC, plummetted after management announced a 14-billion-euro rights issue aimed at shoring up its balance sheet.
