European stocks slid early on Monday, with major exchanges in London, Frankfurt and Paris losing between 0.6 and 2.4 per cent in the first half hour of trade. Asian shares fell nearly four percent on Monday amid fears that the costs of rescuing troubled financial institutions is ballooning. Europe's largest bank, HSBC, has announced a 14-billion-euro rights issue aimed at shoring up its balance sheet. Meanwhile, the US government looks set to pour a further $30 billion into struggling insurance group AIG.
