A group of banks including Bank of America, Citigroup and JPMorgan Chase are putting up $70bn for a borrowing fund aimed at providing liquidity, reports Bloomberg. The Federal Reserve also said Sunday  it accepts for emergency loans to investment banks . The firms plan to use the facility beginning this week. Each participating firm will provide $7bn to establish the fund and have the ability to borrow up to a third of the total. Other banks include Barclays, Credit Suisse, Deutsche Bank, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, Merrill Lynch and UBS. The pool could expand as other companies join. The firms acted as Lehman Brothers prepared to file for bankruptcy protection after talks on a possible sale collapsed. Separately, Bank of America agreed to buy Merrill Lynch for about $44bn, after Merrills shares fell more than 35 percent last week.
