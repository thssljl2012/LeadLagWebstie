First Minister Alex Salmond has urged financial authorities to curb 'short-selling' to prevent financial institutions being laid low by speculators.He also pledged the Scottish Government would 'strain every sinew' in an effort to keep as many jobs in Scotland in the wake of the 12 billion takeover by Lloyds TSB of Halifax Bank of Scotland.Mr Salmond made the pledge in exchanges at First Minister's Questions with Iain Gray, making his debut appearance in that role as Scottish Labour leader.
