By my calculation, the two largest U.S. banks, Citi and Bank of America, have over the last year lost roughly as much in market capitalization as is the current value of the other six U.S. banks combined.  To be specific, Citi and BofA have together lost $296-billion in market capitalization over the last year, while JPMorgan, Wells Fargo, Bank of New York, Wachovia, WaMu, and U.S. Bank currently have a remaining combined capitalization of $283-billion.
