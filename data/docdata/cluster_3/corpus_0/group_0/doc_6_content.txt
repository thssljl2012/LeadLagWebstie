 National Australia Bank announced that it would take an A$830 mln writedown on its investments in US residential mortgages (SMH, WSJ).  Delinquencies on its portfolio of mortgages have risen from 10% to 40% during 2008.  The announcement follows an $181 mln charge taken on March 31 on these positions.  The combined writedowns represent 80% of the original value of the securities.  For the six months ended March 31, the company earned A$2.24 bln.Standard &amp; Poors affirmed the company's credit rating, but put it on negative credit watch outlook (SMH).
