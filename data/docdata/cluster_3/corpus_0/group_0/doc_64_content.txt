In case you missed it, OMB released its annual report on the benefits and costs of Federal regulations last week.  According to OMB:This report features the estimates of the total benefits and costs of regulations reviewed by OMB.  Similar to previous reports, the report includes a ten-year look-back at major Federal regulations reviewed by OMB to examine their quantified and monetized benefits and costs.  The average yearly cost of the major regulations issued between 2001 and 2006 is $2.9 billion which is about 47 percent less than the previous 20 year average of $5.5 billion.  So, if you're still looking for some summertime reading, check it out.
