Banks were accused of taking almost three billion pounds of extra cash from homeowners while blaming the credit crunch. The High Street lenders have made the money by raising their mortgage rates and fees over the past year, while interest rate cuts have made it cheaper for some of them to borrow money. A new study calculates that the country's five biggest banks - Halifax, HSBC, Barclays, Lloyds TSB and Royal Bank of Scotland - are taking almost three billion pounds more from mortgage borrowers compared to last year.
