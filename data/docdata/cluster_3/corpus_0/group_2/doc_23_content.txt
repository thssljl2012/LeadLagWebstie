 Dow Jones industrials fell more than 300 points at opening Monday, while treaury bond prices are now soaring.  Markets around the globe are tanking as investors react to the bankruptcy filing at Lehman Brothers Holdings Inc. and Merrill Lynch &amp;amp; Co's forced sale to Bank of America Corp. for $50 billion in stock. Further signs that the credit crisis is not yet over include American International Group Inc's request to the Federal Reserve for emergency funding.  AIG, the world's largest insurance company, plans to announce a major restructuring on Monday. European stocks shot up last week to a recent high when the US government announced that it would take over Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac.  However, with no more short-term government shore-ups in sight, Europe is now bracing for the worst.  America is in the same boat, getting prepared for the worst yet to come. These latest developments are the biggest yet in the now 14-month old credit crisis stemming from the collapse of the subprime mortgage industry. Talks of a recession are already beginning to spread in Europe, and rumblings of the "R" word are likely now in the US.
