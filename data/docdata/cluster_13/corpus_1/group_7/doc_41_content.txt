Time Warner Cable has elected Donna James, Edward Shirley and John Sununu to its board of directors. The company also named Glenn Britt to the additional position of chairman and Peter Haje, who has served as a director since 2006, has been named lead director. Don Logan will step down as chairman, but will remain on the board and Jeff Bewkes, chairman and CEO of the company will leave the board.   Ms James has served as a consultant, business advisor and managing director of Lardon &amp; Associates, a business advisory services firm, since April 2006. Prior to that, she served as president of Nationwide Strategic Investments, a division of Nationwide Mutual Insurance Company, a financial services and insurance company, from 2003, and as executive vice president and chief administrative officer of Nationwide Mutual from 2000.   Mr Shirley has served as vice-chairman of Global Beauty and Grooming, a business unit of The Procter &amp; Gamble Company, since July 2008, and as group president for North America from April 2006. Prior to that, he held several senior executive positions with The Gillette Company, which was acquired by Procter &amp; Gamble in 2005. He is expected to serve on Time Warner Cable's audit committee and the nominating and governance committee.   Mr Sununu served as a US Senator from New Hampshire from January 2002 to 2008. Prior to that, he represented New Hampshire in the US House of Representatives from 1996 to 2002 and was the CFO of Teletrol Systems, from 1993 to 1996.
