Two men, found guilty of robbing a Clovis bank, and leading Police on a high-speed chase, learned their fate on Tuesday.  In a Fresno County courtroom, Doug Jackson and Jamal Chambers were sentenced for the crime.  Jackson was sentenced to 48 years with the possibility of parole, while Chambers was given 38 years with the possibility of parole.  Back in December, Jackson and Chambers were both found guilty in the 2006 armed robbery of a Clovis Citibank and were convicted of evading police, the attempted murder of a Clovis police officer, assault with a gun, and being a felon in possession of a gun in a Fresno courtroom.
