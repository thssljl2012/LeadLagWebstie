Alistair Darling has pulled out of a visit to the World Economic Forum in Davos yesterday as efforts got under way at home to ease the banking crisis.  The Chancellor is the latest in a series of high-profile attendees to shun the annual business bash in Switzerland.  Foreign secretary David Miliband has decided not to fly out to the WEF, which suggested that Labour perhaps senses their attendance at the glamorous bash at a time of national austerity may not play well with the public.  The Treasury confirmed that Mr Darling would not be going to the event because a number of people he was due to meet had pulled out.  Earlier this week, Bob Diamond, president at Barclays decided he would not be attending, and neither would Vikram Pandit, chief executive of Citigroup, the embattled US bank.  An annual lecture by Richard Fuld, the former chief executive of the collapsed Lehman Brothers Holdings, has been cancelled.
