In&amp;nbsp;a new settlement Enron investors will receive $7.2 billion in damages part of which will be shelled out by&amp;nbsp;banks that allegedly participated in the accounting fraud that led to the energy company's collapse. The settlement has accrued interest since 2002 and includes $688 million in attorneys fees. Under the terms of the deal investors will receive an average of $6.79 per share of common stock and an average of $168.50 per share of preferred stock.
