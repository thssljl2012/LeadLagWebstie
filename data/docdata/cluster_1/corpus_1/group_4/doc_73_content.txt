Charlotte, NC -- American International Group is defending a sales meeting it held at a luxury resort in Arizona, saying it was a necessary training tool.  ABC News initially reported on the Phoenix meeting hours after the Federal Reserve and Treasury Department announced an infusion of another $40 billion into the ailing insurance giant.  ON THE WEB: Oct. 8, 2008 - AIG Execs Take Vacation After Taxpayer Loan /// Nov. 10, 2008 - Government Provides Additional $40B To AIG  AIG officials maintain the meeting held last week was a legitimate event that helped train about 150 independent financial advisers.  A spokesman adds that while some previous AIG-sponsored events may have been questionable, this one was needed to help the company move forward in paying back taxpayers backing the government-sponsored bailout package.
