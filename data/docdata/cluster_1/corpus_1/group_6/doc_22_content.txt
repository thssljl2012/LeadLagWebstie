 (RTTNews) - American International Group, Inc. (AIG:  News ) is reportedly planning to sell its Canadian life insurance unit in the next few days, according to people close to the source.  The potential buyers and the financial details of the transaction were not disclosed. However, Desjardins Group, a credit union, is expected to be one of the probable buyers. Desjardins Group offers individual and group life and health insurance, primarily in Quebec. The Canadian unit of AIG sells both life and health insurance.  AIG, once the world's biggest insurer, is selling its businesses to repay a $60 billion loan, which it received as part of the U.S government's bailout package. The company has said that it has plans to sell all its businesses, except the U.S. property and casualty business, foreign general insurance, and an ownership interest in some foreign life operations.  Shares of AIG closed Friday's regular trading session at $1.62. In the past 52 weeks, the stock has been trading in the range of $1.25 to $59.42.
