 Article Tools  Smaller  Larger Text  Story Tools  TORONTO - BMO Financial Group (TSX:BMO) says it has reached a deal to buy the Canadian life insurance business of American International Group Inc.  BMO says the all-cash transaction is valued at $375 million subject to any change in the division's book value. BMO says the purchase will expand its portfolio of investment, insurance and financial planning services.  The deal is expected to close by June and to begin impacting BMO's earnings next year.  AIG, once the world's largest insurer, is in the process of liquidating assets to repay a loan of US$60 billion loan from the U.S. government.  The loan is part of a US$150 billion bailout from the federal government.
