 THE Co-operative Group, Britain's biggest mutually owned retailer, is still in talks to buy Somerfield but reports an agreed deal was imminent were premature, sources close to the matter have claimed.If the acquisition is agreed the Co-op is likely to pay less than 1.7 billion, one said.Supermarket chain Somerfield is being sold by its owners, private equity firm Apax, Barclays Capital  a unit of Barclays  and property magnate Robert Tchenguiz.In April, Co-op chief executive Peter Marks confirmed that the firm was in talks to buy the Somerfield chain, as he unveiled a 35 per cent rise in annual operating profit to 322.7 million.The Co-operative Group was created last year through the merger of the Co-operative Group and United Co-operatives.
