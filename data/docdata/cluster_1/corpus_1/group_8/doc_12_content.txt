American International Group Inc (NYSE: AIG), an insurance and financial services firm, disclosed yesterday (5 March) that 100,000 shares of series C perpetual, convertible, participating preferred stock has been issued by the company on 4 March 2009 to the AIG Credit Facility Trust, established for the sole benefit of the US Treasury. There is a change of control of AIG with this issuance, since the above preferred stock holds about 77.9% of the total voting power of the AIG common stock, treating the series C preferred stock as converted. The company's most recent annual report on Form 10-K notes that the issuance of the shares of series C preferred stock were pursuant to the series C perpetual, convertible, participating preferred stock purchase agreement between the Trust and AIG on 1 March 2009 and as per the Credit Agreement between AIG and the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, signed on 22 September 2008.
