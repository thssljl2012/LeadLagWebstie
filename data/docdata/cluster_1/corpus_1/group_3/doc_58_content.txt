Insurance giant AIG says it's making plans to pay off its massive government loan.The company says it hopes to sell off a number of business units. AIG says it will keep its U.S. property and casualty as well as its foreign general insurance businesses. It also plans to retain an ownership interest in its foreign life insurance operations.AIG's new Chairman and Chief Executive Edward Liddy says he's hopeful that the 85 billion dollar loan will be enough to provide the company the flexibility it needs to work its way out of the current situations. He says the goal is to emerge 'as a smaller, but more nimble company that is solidly profitable' with prospects for long-term growth.Liddy says the Federal Reserve has been generous, and pledged AIG would do everything it can 'not to have to go back to them.'
