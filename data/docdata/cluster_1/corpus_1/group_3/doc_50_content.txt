SINGAPORE -(Dow Jones)- American International Group Inc. (AIG)'s non-life insurance operations in Southeast Asia have suffered 'minimal impact' from the financial problems faced by parent AIG, a senior company official said Monday.'But very clearly, our reputation has taken a bit of a hit,' Leslie J. Mouat, regional president for AIG's non-life business in Southeast Asia told a news conference.AIG general insurance's Southeast Asia operations include Hong Kong, Taiwan, Malaysia, Indonesia, the Philippines, Thailand, Vietnam and Singapore.AIG said Friday that it plans to sell a number of its businesses to pay off a $85 billion government-rescue loan.AIG's non-life operations in Southeast Asia are not up for sale.-By Patricia Kowsmann, Dow Jones Newswires; 65 6415 4157; patricia.kowsmann@dowjones.comClick here to go to Dow Jones NewsPlus, a web front page of today's most important business and market news, analysis and commentary: http://www.djnewsplus.com/al?rnd=p6N7fvxBgNVlFcXE2xeYCg%3D%3D.
