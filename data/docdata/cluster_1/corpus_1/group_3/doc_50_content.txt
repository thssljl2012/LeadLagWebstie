AIG, one of the world's biggest insurers, also said Friday in New York that it would sell a number of business units around the world, though it did not specifically disclose all the assets being considered for sale or their expected prices.A number of overseas life insurance companies are believed to be interested in buying the three Japan subsidiaries, according to the Nikkei financial daily.AIG's Japan unit said it will retain its casualty insurance operations, in line with the company's overall plan to refocus on its core property and casualty insurance businesses.
