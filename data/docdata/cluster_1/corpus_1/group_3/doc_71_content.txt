TOKYO -(Dow Jones)- Troubled U.S. insurer American International Group Inc. ( AIG) said Friday it has postponed a plan to merge two units in Japan pending a review of operations.The move comes as financial services industry watchers say the businesses, AIG Edison Life Insurance Co. and AIG Star Life Insurance Co., are the subject of potential bid interest from a raft of insurance companies and private equity houses. Along with another AIG unit, Alico, they represent a rare opportunity to enter the large and lucrative Japanese market.The insurer initially intended to merge the businesses from the start of next year. It didn't indicate a new schedule for the proposed merger Friday.AIG is selling a series of assets to address a massive capital shortfall and pay back its borrowing under a $85 billion emergency credit line from the U.S. government.-By Kenneth Maxwell, Dow Jones Newswires; 813-5255 2989; kenneth.maxwell@ dowjones.comClick here to go to Dow Jones NewsPlus, a web front page of today's most important business and market news, analysis and commentary: http:// www.djnewsplus.com/al?rnd=fPeOfbYQXb9LfAusoJ0ALQ%3D%3D.
