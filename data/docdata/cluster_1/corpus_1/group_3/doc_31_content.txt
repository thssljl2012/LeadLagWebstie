Of DOW JONES NEWSWIRESLONDON -(Dow Jones)- U.K. insurer Catlin Group Ltd. (CGL.LN) said Friday its losses arising from Hurricanes Ike and Gustav are estimated at around $200 million.It also said its total net investment return for the first nine months of the year was down 0.9% compared to the same period last year.The announcement highlights Catlin's exposure to natural catastrophes and weak investment markets.'The unprecedented events of this year, including the instability in international financial markets, have presented the group with both challenges and opportunities,' said Chief Executive Stephen Catlin. The company said the fall in investment return was calculated after valuing all investments on a mark-to-market basis.This includes an unrealized loss of $118 million on fixed income investments as at end-September.The company held $11 million of Lehman Brothers debt, which has been written down to $1 million.It said its investment, insurance and reinsurance exposures relating to American International Group Inc.
