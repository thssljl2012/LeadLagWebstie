Manchester United said it was business as usual yesterday after its main sponsor was bailed out  as experts said the clubs global power could even have seen it profit from an American International Group (AIG) collapse. In a week of turmoil in financial markets, insurance giant AIG was handed an unprecedented 85billion rescue loan on Tuesday by the US Federal Reserve in a bid to avoid a new global economic shock. AIGs shirt sponsorship deal with the soccer champions is the biggest in the sport  a four-year contract worth 56,5million signed in April 2006.  AFP
