American International Group will be allowed to use 20 billion dollars (11.1bn) of assets held by its subsidiaries to provide the cash needed for the troubled insurer to stay in business.The move comes as New York-based AIG reviews its operations and discusses alternatives with outside parties, reportedly including Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway, to shore up its business amid concern the world's largest insurer could need billions to strengthen its balance sheet.New York governor David Paterson asked state insurance regulators to essentially allow AIG to provide a bridging loan to itself.
