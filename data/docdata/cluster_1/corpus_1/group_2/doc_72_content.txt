BANGKOK, Sept 17 (Reuters) - The biggest life insurer in Thailand, which ispart of the AIG group, said on Wednesday it had ample reserves to cover itsobligations and that it was feeling no effect from its parent's liquidityproblems.     AIA (Thailand) is a unit of American International Assurance (AIA), itself awholly owned subsidiary of U.S. insurer American International Group.     It had life insurance reserves of 286.7 billion baht ($8.4 billion),executive vice president and general manager Thomas J. White said.     'The firm's reserve is adequate to pay all obligations and benefits asspecified in its insurance policies,' White told reporters in Bangkok.     AIG has been bailed out by the U.S. Federal Reserve with a loan of up to $85billion for two years in exchange for a 79.9 percent equity stake.     AIA (Thailand), founded in 1938, has 383 billion baht in assets, or about1.04 percent of AIG's total assets, the firm said in a statement.     It has about 4.8 million policy holders, according to its website, andoffers a wide range of insurance products and financial services including lifeinsurance, accident and health insurance and housing loans.     'AIA (Thailand) has been here for 70 years and we will go on for another 70years,' White said.
