 LITTLE ROCK -- Arkansans who have an AIG insurance policy need not fear that the company won't be able to pay claims, state Insurance Commissioner Julie Benafield Bowman said Tuesday.American International Group Inc., the world's largest insurer, is seeking a $40 billion loan to ease credit-rating agencies' skepticism of the firms' ability to raise enough capital to offset losses over the past few years.The company's stock fell 61 percent Monday as news of Merrill Lynch's forced buyout by Bank of America and the bankruptcy of Lehman Brothers cast a shadow over Wall Street.AIG's insurance subsidiaries are being asked to provide liquid assets to the financially distressed non-insurance parent company in exchange for non-liquid assets. State insurance regulators are reviewing the transaction.
