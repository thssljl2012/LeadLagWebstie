Gevity HR Inc., the local human resources outsourcing company, said it has recently received a return of $33.1 million of excess workers' compensation collateral from AIG Commercial Insurance.The cash infusion had previously been estimated at $17 million.The Lakewood Ranch-based company said it also has reached agreement with AIG where the big insurer, whose parent is now under government control, will waive approximately $14 million in workers' comp collateral payments from a Gevity-related company during the fourth quarter of 2008. Gevity's shares, which trade on the Nasdaq, were selling for $7.76 in morning trading on Thursday, down 22 cents, or 2.76 percent.
