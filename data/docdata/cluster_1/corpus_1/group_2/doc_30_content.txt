(Adds more comments from Cheung, saying Hong Kong will closely monitor situation in next few days, Hong Kong would be cautious about making AIG keep assets in Hong Kong.)HONG KONG -(Dow Jones)- Amid growing concerns about global insurance giant American International Group Inc. (AIG), Hong Kong's insurance commissioner said Tuesday the company's local operations are 'financially sound.'Commissioner of Insurance Clement Cheung told reporters he had consulted with U.S. insurance regulators as well as AIG on the financial health of the company that is now facing liquidity pressures.Cheung said that AIG's Hong Kong subsidiary was 'financially sound, based on the accounting statement and the information given to us.' But he said Hong Kong officials will closely monitor the situation for the next few days while staying in touch with AIG and U.S. regulators.Cheung acknowledged AIG is facing challenges, but he said the company's Hong Kong subsidiary is able to cover all the liabilities in its portfolio in Hong Kong. Cheung declined to specify the size of those liabilities.Cheung said that in an extreme case, Hong Kong officials have the statutory power to prevent AIG from moving assets away from Hong Kong.But Cheung said officials would be cautious about ever exercising that power 'because it would seriously interrupt the operation of insurance companies.'An AIG official in Hong Kong had said earlier Tuesday the Hong Kong operation 'remains normal.'-By Jackie Cheung and Chester Yung, Dow Jones Newswires; 852-2802-7002; jackie.cheung@dowjones.comClick here to go to Dow Jones NewsPlus, a web front page of today's most important business and market news, analysis and commentary: http://www.djnewsplus.com/al?rnd=Ft66e%2BXvbMLa7fjXiGgXbQ%3D%3D.
